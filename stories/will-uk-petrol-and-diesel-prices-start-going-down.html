Will UK petrol and diesel prices start going down?

Drivers are waiting to see whether the ceasefire and any stabilisation in supply translate into lower prices at the pump, though the pass-through to consumers may remain uneven.

UK drivers are waiting to see whether the latest ceasefire and signs of supply stabilisation translate into lower petrol and diesel prices. The answer is likely to be uneven, because retail prices do not always move in step with wholesale markets and suppliers can be slow to pass on reductions.

For motorists, that means relief at the pump may arrive later than the headline diplomatic news suggests. Prices are shaped not only by crude costs but by exchange rates, distribution charges and commercial decisions made by retailers and refiners.

The immediate question is whether lower wholesale costs are sustained long enough to filter through. If the supply route remains fragile, any drop may prove temporary; if tensions ease further, households could see some relief, though probably not as quickly or as sharply as they would hope.
