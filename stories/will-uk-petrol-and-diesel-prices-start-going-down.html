Will UK petrol and diesel prices start going down?

British motorists are watching for any relief after the ceasefire talks, but fuel costs are still being driven by the wider shock to global supply.

British drivers are looking for relief after talk of a ceasefire, but there is no guarantee that petrol and diesel prices will fall quickly. Fuel retailers price in global oil movements, shipping costs and the risk of renewed disruption, not just the latest headline.

That means any benefit from calmer diplomacy in the Gulf may take time to reach forecourts. Some of the recent rise was driven by the wider shock to crude markets, and retailers may be cautious about passing on reductions at once.

The key question is how stable the truce remains. If tensions flare again, wholesale costs could rise quickly; if supply routes remain open and markets steady, motorists may eventually see some easing, but not necessarily a dramatic one.
