Will UK petrol and diesel prices start going down?

Fuel costs in Britain have risen on the back of the conflict; the ceasefire may ease pressure, but the timing and scale of any fall remain uncertain.

Fuel costs in Britain have risen sharply because of the conflict in the Middle East, leaving drivers waiting to see whether the ceasefire will bring relief at the pumps. The answer depends on how quickly wholesale oil and refined fuel prices settle.

In principle, lower crude prices should eventually feed through to petrol and diesel. But that process is neither immediate nor guaranteed, because retailers, wholesalers and forecourts all adjust at different speeds and may be cautious if markets remain volatile.

For households, the key question is timing as much as direction. If the Strait of Hormuz stays open and shipping risk falls, prices should ease; if uncertainty returns, any drop could be delayed or very limited.
