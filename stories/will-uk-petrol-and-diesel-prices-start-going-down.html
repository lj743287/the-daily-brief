Will UK petrol and diesel prices start going down?

A ceasefire may ease some pressure, but British drivers are unlikely to see an immediate or uniform fall in forecourt prices.

The ceasefire may ease some pressure on fuel markets, but British drivers should not expect an immediate or even uniform fall in petrol and diesel prices. Forecourt costs often move more slowly than global oil prices, because retailers and wholesalers adjust at different speeds.

The UK has already seen prices rise on the back of the Middle East conflict, and the effect can linger after conditions improve. Costs built up in shipping, refining and distribution do not disappear overnight, so the consumer impact can continue even if crude prices settle.

What happens next will depend on whether the truce holds and whether tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz returns to normal. If it does, prices should eventually ease; if not, drivers may remain stuck with a higher baseline for longer than they had hoped.
