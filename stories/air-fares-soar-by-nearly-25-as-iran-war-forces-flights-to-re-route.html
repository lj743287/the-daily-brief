Air fares soar by nearly 25% as Iran war forces flights to re-route

Air fares have risen by nearly 25% as airlines reroute around conflict-related airspace restrictions, adding another channel through which the Iran war is hitting households and business travel.

Air fares have risen by nearly 25% as airlines reroute flights around conflict-related airspace restrictions linked to the Iran war.

The rise shows how quickly a regional conflict can hit ordinary travellers, not only through higher ticket prices but also through longer journeys, altered schedules and reduced availability on some routes.

For airlines, rerouting can mean extra fuel burn and higher operating costs at a time when margins are already under strain, which increases the chance that more of the cost is passed on to passengers.

That leaves both leisure and business travel exposed, especially if restrictions continue and carriers are forced to keep adapting their networks.

The broader risk is that aviation inflation becomes another channel through which the war reaches household budgets, alongside fuel, food and freight.
