EasyJet passengers describe EU border 'nightmare'

EasyJet passengers have faced long queues and missed flights as the new EU digital border control system caused disruption at Milan’s Linate airport and raised wider airline concerns.

EasyJet passengers have described a border-control “nightmare” at Milan’s Linate airport after the new EU digital border system caused long queues and missed flights.

The disruption highlights the practical problems that can come with new border technology, even when the policy aims are administrative rather than political. Airlines say they fear further problems as the system is rolled out more widely.

For passengers, the impact is immediate: delays, confusion and the risk of being left behind. EasyJet has already faced complaints from people who missed flights during the queues.

The wider concern is whether airports and airlines are ready for the scale of change. If the early teething problems persist, pressure will grow on Brussels and national authorities to slow implementation or improve staffing and systems.
