How the Iran war affects your money and bills

The Iran war is pushing up petrol, household energy and food costs, putting fresh pressure on household budgets in the UK and beyond.

The Iran war is already feeding into everyday costs, pushing up petrol prices, household energy bills and some food prices as markets react to the risk of a wider disruption in supply.

For consumers in the UK and elsewhere, the impact tends to arrive through familiar channels: higher fuel at the pump, dearer shipping and, eventually, more expensive goods in shops. The effect is often uneven, but it can be felt quickly by households with tight budgets.

Energy markets are especially sensitive because the conflict threatens one of the world’s key transport routes. That leaves governments, traders and suppliers trying to judge whether current price rises are a short-lived reaction or the start of something more persistent.

The key uncertainty is how long the conflict lasts and whether it spreads further. If tensions remain elevated, the pressure on bills could linger well beyond the immediate headlines.
