EU approves €90bn loan for Ukraine as pipeline is turned on ending deadlock

The European Union has approved a €90bn loan for Ukraine as Kyiv says it has reopened the Druzhba pipeline, ending a months-long stand-off over oil supplies to Hungary and easing one point of friction inside the bloc.

The European Union has approved a €90bn loan for Ukraine, while Kyiv says it has reopened the Druzhba pipeline in a move that appears to end a months-long dispute over oil supplies to Hungary.

The package is significant because financial support remains central to Ukraine’s war effort and its ability to keep government services running, while energy disputes inside the bloc have repeatedly exposed political tensions between member states.

The reopening of the pipeline matters too: Druzhba has been a focal point of friction over Russian oil flows, and the stalemate over supplies to Hungary had become one more source of division inside the EU.

Together, the two developments suggest a partial easing of deadlock on both the financial and energy fronts, though neither issue is fully resolved.

Ukraine and its partners still face the larger challenge of sustaining support as the war drags on, and Hungary’s energy dependence will continue to shape negotiations over future sanctions and supply arrangements.
