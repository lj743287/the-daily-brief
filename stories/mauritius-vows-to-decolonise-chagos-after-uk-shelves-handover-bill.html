Mauritius vows to ‘decolonise’ Chagos after UK shelves handover bill

Port Louis pledged to “spare no effort” to regain control after ministers in London ran out of time to pass enabling legislation. The delay follows a shift in US backing and raises questions over the UK’s timetable for resolving the long‑running dispute.

Mauritius pledged to “spare no effort” to regain control of the Chagos Islands after the UK shelved enabling legislation, saying it had run out of time to pass the bill this session, the Guardian reported.

The delay follows a shift in US backing and raises questions over London’s timetable for resolving the long‑running dispute, which has drawn international scrutiny.

The case traces back to Britain’s decision to detach the archipelago from Mauritius before independence, a move later criticised in an International Court of Justice advisory opinion and subsequent UN resolutions.

At the heart of the dispute are the rights of displaced Chagossians and the presence of a major US base on Diego Garcia, which Washington views as strategically vital.

Mauritius has proposed continued access for the United States under lease after a handover, seeking to balance strategic concerns with decolonisation claims. The UK says complex legal and security issues must be addressed in any settlement.

With legislation shelved, Port Louis is expected to intensify diplomatic outreach and legal avenues, while the UK signals that talks will continue in the next parliamentary session.
