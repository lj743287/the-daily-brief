UK will not join any US‑led Hormuz blockade

Despite US claims that NATO allies would assist in ‘cleaning out’ the strait, British officials distanced the UK from any blockade. London remains focused on de‑escalation and protecting shipping without deepening the conflict.

The UK will not participate in any blockade of the Strait of Hormuz despite claims from Washington that NATO allies would assist, the Guardian reported, after US statements suggested British minesweepers could be involved.

British officials have distanced London from talk of a blockade, emphasising de‑escalation, freedom of navigation and the protection of commercial shipping without steps that could deepen the conflict.

Prime Minister Keir Starmer has urged both the US and Iran to find a way through after peace talks faltered, warning against escalation, according to the BBC, as regional tensions remain high.

The UK is expected to continue maritime security activity in the region aimed at deterring threats and supporting merchant traffic, while avoiding involvement in actions that could be construed as coercive closure of a key waterway.

A blockade would carry legal and economic risks, potentially driving up global energy prices and insurance costs; officials in London are wary of measures that might undermine allied unity or breach international norms.

The stance underlines a gap between UK and US rhetoric, with London aligning closely with European partners on restraint while maintaining dialogue with Washington on safeguarding shipping lanes.
