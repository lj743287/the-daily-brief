Private health records of half a million Britons offered for sale on Chinese website

The UK government says the de-identified health records of 500,000 British volunteers from UK Biobank were offered for sale on Alibaba and have now been removed.

The UK government says the de-identified health records of 500,000 British volunteers from UK Biobank were offered for sale on Alibaba and later removed.

Technology minister Ian Murray told the Commons that the listings had been taken down after contact with the Chinese government and Alibaba. Officials say no personally identifiable information was made available, and it is not believed any sales were made.

The case matters because UK Biobank data is used in research that depends on public confidence and strict handling of sensitive medical information. Even de-identified records can raise alarm if they are being marketed abroad without authorisation.

The incident is likely to prompt renewed scrutiny of how research data is protected and where it can be stored or resold. It also underlines how quickly health information can become part of broader tensions over technology and data security.
