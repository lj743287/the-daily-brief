OpenAI faces criminal probe over role of ChatGPT in shooting

OpenAI is under criminal investigation over whether ChatGPT played a role in a shooting at Florida State University, a case that intensifies scrutiny of AI safety and liability.

OpenAI is under criminal investigation over whether ChatGPT played a role in a shooting at Florida State University, intensifying scrutiny of how AI tools may be used in real-world violence.

The company has said it is not responsible for the attack, but the probe raises difficult questions about liability, safety and the limits of chatbot design.

It also comes at a moment when governments are already under pressure to decide how much responsibility AI firms should bear for harmful uses of their systems, especially if users exploit them in ways developers did not intend.

For OpenAI, the case is likely to deepen concerns about reputational risk and legal exposure just as it tries to position its technology as useful and broadly safe.

What investigators conclude could shape the wider debate over whether AI firms need tougher oversight, clearer safeguards and more explicit rules on accountability.
