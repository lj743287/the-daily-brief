British Steel 'needs nationalising by the summer'

A Labour MP says public ownership may be the only workable outcome as talks over British Steel’s future continue.

British Steel may need to be nationalised by the summer, according to Labour MP Nic Dakin, as talks over the company's future continue. The remarks reflect growing concern that the current ownership and investment model may not be stable enough to secure the plant's long-term survival.

British Steel remains strategically important because of the jobs it supports and the industrial capability it represents. Any move towards public ownership would be politically significant as well as economically costly, raising questions about how much state intervention ministers are willing to contemplate in order to protect domestic production.

The immediate issue is whether a private solution can be reached before the business deteriorates further. If it cannot, pressure will build on the government to act, even if nationalisation is framed as a temporary measure rather than a permanent policy shift.
