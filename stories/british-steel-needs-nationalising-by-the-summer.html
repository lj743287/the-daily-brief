British Steel 'needs nationalising by the summer'

As ownership talks continue, pressure is mounting for a clearer state-backed solution to secure the company’s future and the industrial jobs around it.

British Steel is once again at the centre of an argument about whether the state should step in to protect a strategic industry.

Labour MP Nic Dakin has said nationalisation may be the best outcome if ownership talks do not deliver a viable future for the company. His intervention reflects a broader concern about jobs, industrial capability and the long-term security of UK steelmaking.

The government has been open to intervening in difficult cases when there is a case for protecting critical capacity, but full state ownership would be a significant step. It would raise questions about cost, management and what kind of industrial strategy ministers are prepared to pursue.

For the workforce and the surrounding supply chain, the immediate issue is uncertainty. For ministers, the issue is whether British Steel is treated as a commercial problem or a national strategic asset.
