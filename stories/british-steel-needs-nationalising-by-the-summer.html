British Steel 'needs nationalising by the summer'

Labour MP Nic Dakin says British Steel needs nationalising by the summer as ownership talks continue, adding pressure on ministers over the future of the plant and its workforce.

British Steel needs nationalising by the summer, according to Labour MP Nic Dakin, as ownership talks continue. The warning adds fresh pressure on ministers over the future of the plant and the jobs tied to it.

The argument for intervention is straightforward: Britain still relies on domestic steel capacity for strategic industries, and prolonged uncertainty risks further damage to a workforce that has already lived through repeated rounds of instability. Nationalisation would not solve every problem, but supporters see it as the clearest way to secure the site's future.

What happens next will depend on the negotiations now under way and on whether the government is prepared to act before the summer. The issue is no longer just about ownership; it is about industrial capacity, energy costs and whether Britain wants to keep steel-making onshore.
