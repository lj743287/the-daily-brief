Roblox defends expanded age‑checks after parents raise concerns over errors

Roblox says its expanded age-check system is aimed at protecting its 144 million daily users, even as parents warn that automated errors can wrongly classify accounts.

Roblox has defended its expanded age-check system, saying it is intended to protect its 144 million daily users even as parents warn that automated errors can wrongly classify accounts.

The company is extending its technology to create age-specific accounts, part of a wider effort to limit contact between children and adults online. That reflects growing pressure on platforms to do more about child safety.

But the controversy shows the difficulty of using automated systems at scale. If age checks misfire, legitimate users can be pushed into the wrong category, creating frustration and mistrust.

For parents, the concern is whether the safeguards are accurate enough to be worth the disruption. For Roblox, the issue is whether it can prove that stronger protection does not come at the cost of fairness and usability.

The debate is likely to continue as regulators and families demand better online safeguards.
