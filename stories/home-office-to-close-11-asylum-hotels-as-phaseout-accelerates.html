Home Office to close 11 asylum hotels as phase‑out accelerates

The closures form part of a pledge to end hotel use by the end of the parliament. Ministers argue alternative accommodation is scaling up, though campaigners warn about conditions and local capacity.

The Home Office will close 11 asylum hotels this week as part of its plan to end the use of hotels for asylum accommodation, according to the Guardian.

Ministers say alternative sites are being scaled up to meet demand and that the pledge to end hotel use by the end of the parliament remains on track.

Hotels became a widespread contingency option from the pandemic onwards, drawing criticism over costs and concerns about the impact on local communities, including occasional protests outside affected venues.

Charities and local campaigners warn that replacement accommodation must meet minimum standards and that councils will need support to manage pressures on services and community cohesion.

The shift coincides with efforts to speed up asylum decision‑making and reduce backlogs, with the government arguing that faster processing will cut reliance on emergency placements.

Officials have not confirmed the locations due to close, with notifications expected to go first to councils and contractors; MPs and local leaders are preparing to scrutinise the impact on affected areas.
