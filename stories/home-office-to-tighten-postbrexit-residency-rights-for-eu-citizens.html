Home Office to tighten post‑Brexit residency rights for EU citizens

Ministers will move to remove rights from those not ‘continuously’ resident, partly using travel data. The approach raises concerns after previous data errors led to wrongful benefit removals.

The government will move to remove residency rights from EU citizens deemed not to be ‘continuously’ resident in the UK, partly using travel data to assess absences, the Guardian reported.

Officials say the step is consistent with the 2020 Withdrawal Agreement, though the practical tests for continuity can be complex for people with irregular work patterns or caring responsibilities.

The plan to lean on border movement records has raised concerns given previous data errors that led to thousands of parents being wrongly stripped of child benefit, prompting a high‑profile HMRC and Home Office correction effort.

Advocacy groups warn that long‑term residents with limited documentation, and those who frequently travel for work or family reasons, could be unfairly caught out without clear avenues to challenge decisions.

The Home Office maintains it is enforcing existing rules while offering processes to rectify status where appropriate, but rights lawyers anticipate legal challenges over evidence standards and appeal rights.

EU governments are monitoring the approach amid reciprocal rights considerations for UK nationals living on the continent, adding a diplomatic dimension to the domestic policy shift.
