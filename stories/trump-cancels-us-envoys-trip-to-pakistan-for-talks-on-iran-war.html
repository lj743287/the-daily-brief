Trump cancels US envoys' trip to Pakistan for talks on Iran war

Donald Trump has cancelled a planned US envoys’ trip to Pakistan, underlining how the Iran war is disrupting American diplomacy and regional diplomacy more broadly. Iran had already said there were no plans for a direct meeting with the US delegation led by Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, suggesting the talks were uncertain before they were scrapped.

Donald Trump has cancelled a planned trip to Pakistan by US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, in a fresh sign that the Iran war is disrupting American diplomacy well beyond the battlefield.

The visit had been seen as part of wider efforts to manage regional tensions, but it was already uncertain. Iran had said there were no plans for a direct meeting with the US delegation, raising doubts about how much progress the talks could make.

The cancellation underlines how the conflict is complicating not only US-Iran contacts but also contacts with regional powers that Washington may need to lean on as the war continues.

Pakistan has often tried to balance relations between the US, Iran and Gulf states, and any sudden change in travel plans will be read in that context. The episode suggests diplomacy is moving more erratically as the conflict widens.

For Washington, the immediate question is whether this is only a postponement or a deeper sign that the channel has stalled. For regional capitals, it is another reminder that the war is now shaping political decisions far from Tehran and Jerusalem.

If the fighting drags on, more such meetings may be delayed or abandoned altogether, making it harder to build even limited de-escalation talks.
