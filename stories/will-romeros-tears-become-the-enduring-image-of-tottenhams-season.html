Will Romero's tears become the enduring image of Tottenham's season?

Tottenham’s defeat at Sunderland deepens their crisis and raises the prospect of a season-defining collapse.

Cristian Romero's tears after Tottenham's defeat at Sunderland may come to symbolise a season slipping beyond control. The loss leaves Spurs in the relegation zone and deepens the sense that the club is in a crisis that cannot be explained away as a bad afternoon.

The result is especially damaging because it came in Roberto De Zerbi's first game in charge. A new manager usually brings at least a brief lift, but Tottenham instead looked fragile and short of the authority needed to climb out of trouble.

If the season does collapse, Romero's reaction could become the image supporters remember most. It captured not just disappointment but the sense of a club under intense strain, with little certainty about how quickly its form can be rescued.
