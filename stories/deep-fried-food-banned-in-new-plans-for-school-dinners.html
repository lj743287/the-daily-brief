Deep-fried food banned in new plans for school dinners

The government plans to bar deep-fried food from school dinners and push schools toward more vegetables and whole grains, in a major diet overhaul.

The government is planning to ban deep-fried food from school dinners and push schools towards more vegetables and whole grains. The overhaul is part of a wider attempt to improve children’s diets and tackle obesity through the school meal system.

The move matters because school food is one of the few places where the state can shape habits at scale. Ministers are betting that changes to what children are served each day will have more impact than repeated advice to families alone.

The proposal follows years of concern that school menus have not kept pace with public health guidance. If it is enforced properly, it could mark a more ambitious break from the old standards that have governed school food for years.

The challenge will be practical as much as political. Schools will need enough time, money and kitchen capacity to adjust menus without simply shifting the burden elsewhere. What sounds straightforward in policy terms can be difficult in the dining hall.
