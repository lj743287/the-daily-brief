Deep-fried food banned in new plans for school dinners

New school food standards aim to push healthier diets in England, with childhood obesity and nutrition now central to education policy.

New plans for school meals in England would ban deep-fried food and push schools towards more vegetables, whole grains and less sugary food, as ministers try to improve children’s diets.

The reforms reflect a broader concern about childhood obesity and long-term health. School meals are one of the few places where government can shape nutrition at scale, especially for children who rely on them every day.

The challenge is whether schools can meet the new standards without making meals more expensive or less popular. The policy may improve dietary quality, but it will also test budgets, kitchens and the willingness of children to eat what is put in front of them.
