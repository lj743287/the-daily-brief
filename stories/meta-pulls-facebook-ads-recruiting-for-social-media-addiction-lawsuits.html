Meta pulls Facebook ads recruiting for social media addiction lawsuits

Meta has removed Facebook ads linked to social media addiction lawsuits after recently losing a landmark case in California.

Meta has removed Facebook ads linked to social media addiction lawsuits after recently losing a landmark case in California. The company did not publicly dispute the existence of the ads, but their removal shows how quickly litigation can spill into platform policy.

The case matters because it goes to the heart of how social media firms are being challenged over the harms associated with their products. If courts continue to accept claims around addiction and design, the legal and financial pressure on Meta and its rivals could grow.

The broader question is whether the company can keep distancing itself from the accusations while also defending the business model that those accusations target. Further lawsuits, and further scrutiny of ad practices, look likely.
