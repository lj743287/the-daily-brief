Meta pulls Facebook ads recruiting for social media addiction lawsuits

Meta has removed ads recruiting people for social-media addiction lawsuits after losing a landmark trial in California over the effects of Facebook and related products.

Meta has removed Facebook ads recruiting people for social media addiction lawsuits after losing a landmark trial in California over the effects of Facebook and related products. The move suggests the company is trying to limit the spread of a legal campaign that could expose it to further claims about harm to users.

The case matters because it goes to the heart of the debate over how social media platforms affect behaviour, especially among younger users. A trial loss does not settle the science, but it does strengthen the hand of plaintiffs who argue that Meta knew more about the risks than it acknowledged.

By pulling the ads, Meta may be trying to reduce the visibility of litigation that could attract more claimants. But that does not remove the underlying dispute, which is likely to continue in courtrooms and in public debate about regulation.

The broader issue is whether social-media firms can keep presenting themselves as neutral platforms when they are under pressure over the effects of their products. The legal and reputational stakes are likely to remain high.
