Meta pulls Facebook ads recruiting for social media addiction lawsuits

Meta has removed ads that appeared to recruit users for lawsuits over social media addiction. The move comes against the backdrop of wider legal and public pressure on large platforms over alleged harms.

Meta has removed Facebook advertisements that appeared to recruit users for lawsuits over alleged social media addiction. The company’s intervention comes as large platforms face growing scrutiny over the effects of their products on children and teenagers, and over how legal claims are being organised around those concerns.

The ads sit at the intersection of public health concerns, litigation and platform moderation. Meta is already under pressure after losing a landmark social media addiction case in California, and any suggestion that its own services are being used to solicit claimants gives the company another reputational problem to manage.

What happens next will depend on whether the material breaches Meta’s advertising rules and whether similar campaigns are being run elsewhere online. The wider issue, however, is unlikely to fade: regulators, parents and lawyers are all pressing for clearer limits on how social media platforms design, promote and police engagement.
