Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Both Ukraine and Russia accused the other of breaching ceasefire understandings hundreds of times, underscoring the persistent volatility of the front. The claims point to sustained exchanges of artillery, drones and small‑unit engagements as each side probes for openings.

Civilian areas near the line of contact continue to face danger, with damage to homes and utilities reported in multiple districts. Access for humanitarian teams remains uneven, complicating efforts to deliver aid and assess needs as winter gives way to a muddy spring.

Militarily, the pattern suggests a contest of attrition, with logistics, air defence and electronic warfare playing decisive roles. Rotations and re‑supply are under constant strain, and commanders appear intent on testing one another’s defences rather than ceding ground.

Diplomatically, momentum is limited. Western capitals reiterate support for Ukraine while pressing for restraint to reduce civilian harm, and Russia signals it will match any escalation. Without a credible framework for talks, the prospects for a durable pause in hostilities remain remote.
