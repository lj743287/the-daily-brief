Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Both sides say the other has broken the truce repeatedly, underscoring how little trust remains and how quickly violence could resume.

Ukraine and Russia have accused each other of carrying out hundreds of breaches of a ceasefire, underlining how little confidence remains between them. Both sides say the other is responsible for repeated attacks, with Kyiv warning that it would respond symmetrically if struck again.

The claims are important because ceasefires often fail first at the edges, through artillery fire, drone attacks or local skirmishes that then spiral into wider violence. Even when a truce formally exists, the absence of trust can make every incident politically explosive.

For civilians near the front line, the accusations are a sign that calm could prove temporary. For diplomats, they are a reminder that a ceasefire without verification, enforcement and a political process behind it can quickly become little more than a pause in fighting.
