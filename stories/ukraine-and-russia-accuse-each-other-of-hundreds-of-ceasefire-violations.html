Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Both sides say the other has broken the truce repeatedly, underlining how fragile any pause in the fighting remains despite calls for restraint.

Ukraine and Russia have each accused the other of breaching a ceasefire hundreds of times, underlining how fragile any pause in the fighting remains. The accusations come against a backdrop of repeated breakdowns in limited truces since Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine began.

President Volodymyr Zelensky said Ukrainian forces would respond symmetrically if attacked, a warning that reflects the low level of confidence on both sides. Even when a truce is announced, neither side appears prepared to trust the other enough to step back for long.

That matters because ceasefires can only work if both parties believe there is a credible cost to breaking them and a realistic path to a wider settlement. At present, neither condition appears in place, so even a temporary pause risks becoming another brief interruption rather than a foundation for talks.
