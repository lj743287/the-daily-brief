Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Moscow and Kyiv are again trading accusations over ceasefire breaches, a reminder that the battlefield truce remains unstable and vulnerable to collapse.

Ukraine and Russia have again accused each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations, underscoring how fragile the battlefield truce remains despite repeated diplomatic attempts to stabilise it.

President Volodymyr Zelensky said Ukrainian forces would respond symmetrically to Russian attacks, signalling that Kyiv does not intend to absorb repeated breaches without reply. Moscow, for its part, has made similar accusations, continuing the pattern of mutual blame that has complicated efforts to keep the truce intact.

The exchanges matter because even limited violations can unravel agreements that are already under strain. Each side is trying to show it will not be outmanoeuvred, but that makes the risk of escalation higher if commanders on the ground decide restraint is no longer in their interests.
