Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

The fragile ceasefire is fraying almost as soon as it takes hold, with both sides accusing the other of hundreds of breaches and warning of reciprocal responses.

The fragile ceasefire between Ukraine and Russia is fraying almost as soon as it takes hold, with each side accusing the other of hundreds of violations. The result is a familiar pattern: a brief pause in fighting, followed by mutual claims of bad faith and warnings that any further breach will be answered in kind.

Volodymyr Zelensky has said Ukraine would respond symmetrically to Russian attacks, underlining how little trust exists between the two sides. That matters because even a limited truce depends on some level of restraint and communication, both of which remain in short supply after years of war.

The accusations also suggest that the ceasefire may be more a tactical pause than a step towards negotiations. If the breaches continue, the arrangement could collapse altogether, making it harder for outside powers to argue that diplomacy can still contain the war without stronger guarantees or enforcement.
