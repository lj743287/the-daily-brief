Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

The Easter truce narrative is already under strain as both sides trade accusations over breaches. The exchange is a reminder that local ceasefires can be politically useful while remaining operationally fragile.

Ukraine and Russia have accused each other of hundreds of breaches of the Easter truce, underlining how fragile even a short pause in fighting can be. The claims come as both sides look to shape the political narrative around a ceasefire that was never likely to settle the wider war.

President Volodymyr Zelensky said Ukrainian forces would respond symmetrically to Russian attacks, a sign that battlefield caution is already wearing thin. Moscow, meanwhile, has also blamed Kyiv for violations, as each side seeks to place responsibility for any collapse on the other.

The exchange matters because it shows how quickly local ceasefires can become tools of messaging rather than steps towards lasting calm. Unless there is a more durable arrangement, the truce risks becoming another brief interruption in a war that has repeatedly outlasted diplomatic hopes.
