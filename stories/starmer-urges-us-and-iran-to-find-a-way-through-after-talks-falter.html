Starmer urges US and Iran to ‘find a way through’ after talks falter

The prime minister warned against escalation and backed renewed diplomacy after marathon negotiations ended without a deal. The stance reflects UK efforts to remain aligned with allies while limiting direct involvement.

Keir Starmer has urged the United States and Iran to “find a way through” after marathon negotiations ended without a deal, warning against escalation and backing renewed diplomacy, as the UK tries to remain aligned with allies while limiting direct involvement.

Downing Street’s stance reflects a preference for de‑escalation and a rules‑based approach to regional security, with support for diplomatic efforts to reopen channels and reduce the risk of a wider war following weeks of rising tension.

Amid debate in Washington about options in the Strait of Hormuz, UK officials have indicated Britain will not participate in any blockade, though it may consider roles that focus on maritime security and mine‑countermeasure activity alongside partners.

The government has sought to draw a clear line between supporting defensive measures to uphold freedom of navigation and being drawn into offensive operations. Ministers face pressure at home to ensure any UK facilitation of allied actions complies with domestic and international law.

The crisis has fed through to households and businesses via higher energy prices and uncertainty over shipping, adding to the economic case for restraint and coordination among allies. The UK continues to work with partners on sanctions enforcement and efforts to protect commercial traffic.

Attention now turns to whether talks can resume and hold. Any UK contribution to maritime missions is likely to be scrutinised in Parliament, while the outcome will shape London’s relations with Washington and its broader European diplomacy in the months ahead.
