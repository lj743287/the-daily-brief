EU airline industry warns of fuel shortages if Strait of Hormuz stays closed

Airlines are warning that prolonged disruption to Hormuz shipments could squeeze jet fuel supplies and raise the cost of travel.

European airline groups are warning that prolonged disruption to shipments through the Strait of Hormuz could create fuel shortages and raise the cost of travel. Jet fuel supply is already a concern for carriers that rely on complex international refining and shipping networks.

The warning reflects how quickly an oil-and-gas shock in the Gulf can spread through aviation. Even when airlines are not directly buying crude from the region, they depend on fuel markets that are sensitive to any interruption in Middle Eastern exports.

If the strait remains constrained, carriers may face higher operating costs, tighter margins and pressure to pass some of that on to passengers. The bigger risk is that a regional security crisis becomes a broader travel and trade problem for Europe and beyond.
