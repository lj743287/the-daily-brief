EU airline industry warns of fuel shortages if Strait of Hormuz stays closed

European aviation groups warn that prolonged disruption in the Gulf could tighten jet fuel supply and raise operating costs.

European aviation groups have warned that prolonged disruption in the Strait of Hormuz could tighten jet fuel supplies and increase operating costs. The alert reflects the industry’s dependence on steady fuel flows through one of the most important routes for Gulf energy exports.

Airlines are exposed in two ways: first through the cost of fuel itself, and then through the knock-on effects on ticket prices, route planning and margins. Even if supplies are not cut off completely, a sustained squeeze could force carriers to absorb higher bills or pass them on to passengers.

The warning is another sign that the crisis is now being priced well beyond the Gulf. Aviation, like shipping and manufacturing, depends on predictable access to fuel, and the longer the uncertainty lasts, the more likely it is that business decisions will be shaped by the risk of further disruption.
