EU airline industry warns of fuel shortages if Strait of Hormuz stays closed

Europe’s airlines are warning that jet fuel supply could tighten quickly if the waterway remains effectively shut.

Europe's airline industry has warned that jet fuel supply could tighten quickly if the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively shut. Airports Council International Europe says members are increasingly concerned about availability, a sign that the disruption is already moving beyond oil markets into aviation planning.

Airlines are especially exposed because fuel is one of their biggest costs and supply chains are finely balanced. If deliveries from the Gulf are delayed or rerouted, carriers may face higher prices, thinner margins and difficult choices over schedules and fares.

The warning matters because it shows how a shipping crisis can spread through the real economy. Even without a complete stoppage, prolonged uncertainty can force airlines and airports to build in contingency costs, which eventually tend to be passed on to passengers.
