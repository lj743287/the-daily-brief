EU airline industry warns of fuel shortages if Strait of Hormuz stays closed

European airlines are warning of tighter jet-fuel availability if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed, raising the risk of travel disruption and wider pressure on transport costs.

European airlines are warning that jet fuel supplies could tighten if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed. That would matter not only for aviation but for the wider transport sector, where fuel availability and price stability are already under pressure from the Middle East crisis.

Airlines operate on thin margins and rely on predictable fuel flows, so even temporary shortages can force costly changes to schedules, refuelling plans and ticket prices. The warning is therefore as much about operational resilience as it is about headline fuel costs.

If the route stays shut, the effects could spread quickly through Europe’s travel network. A prolonged disruption would raise the risk of cancellations, route changes and higher costs for passengers and freight, making the Strait of Hormuz a direct issue for the continent’s summer travel and trade plans.
