Political turmoil in Indian border state as nine million lose voting rights

Nine million voters in West Bengal have been dropped from the rolls, raising fresh concerns in India about exclusion, fairness and the integrity of the electoral process.

Nine million voters in West Bengal have been dropped from the rolls, creating fresh political turmoil in one of India's most sensitive border states. The removals have prompted concern about exclusion, fairness and the integrity of the electoral process.

The scale of the cut is significant because West Bengal is both politically important and closely watched in national debates over citizenship, documentation and voting rights. Any large-scale deletion from the register is likely to deepen suspicion among communities already anxious about whether they can prove eligibility.

What remains unclear is how many of those removed will be reinstated and whether the process was driven by routine revision, administrative error or something more contentious. The dispute is likely to feed broader arguments over India's election machinery, which opposition figures have increasingly accused of being used to shape outcomes.
