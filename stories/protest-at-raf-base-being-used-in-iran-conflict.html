Hundreds of protesters have gathered at an RAF base being used in the Iran conflict, reflecting growing domestic opposition to Britain's military role.

The protest matters because it shows how the war is now generating political pressure inside the UK as well as abroad. Military facilities are becoming flashpoints for public anger over Britain's involvement.

The use of the base links the conflict in the Middle East to a debate at home about escalation, accountability and the limits of Britain's support for US-led operations.

While the demonstration does not change policy on its own, it adds to the sense that the government's position is under scrutiny from both parliament and the public.

The size of the crowd suggests the issue is resonating beyond activist circles, particularly as the war continues to raise questions about air strikes, logistics and the UK's role in the wider coalition.

If the conflict deepens, more protests around military sites would be likely, especially where facilities are seen as directly connected to operations.
