UK Biobank health data listed for sale in China, government confirms

The government says medical data from 500,000 people linked to UK Biobank was listed for sale in China, though no personally identifiable information was made available.

The government says medical data linked to 500,000 people in UK Biobank was listed for sale in China, though no personally identifiable information was made available.

Ian Murray, the technology minister, told MPs that the “de-identified” data had been advertised on Chinese online listings and later removed after intervention involving the Chinese authorities and Alibaba. It is not believed any sales were completed.

The case matters because UK Biobank is one of Britain’s most important health research resources, and public trust in how data is used is central to its value. Even if the records were stripped of direct identifiers, the incident raises concerns about misuse and overseas access to sensitive medical material.

The government says the material has now been taken down, but questions remain about how it was listed in the first place and whether similar data could appear elsewhere.
