Dolce & Gabbana co‑founder steps down as chair

The Italian fashion house faces debt pressures and softer luxury demand. Leadership changes come as brands reset strategies in a slower, more price‑sensitive market.

A co‑founder of Dolce & Gabbana has stepped down as chair, the BBC reports, as the Italian fashion house contends with debt pressures and a slowdown in luxury demand.

The change at the top signals a recalibration in a sector that has shifted from post‑pandemic exuberance to a more price‑sensitive market, forcing labels to balance scarcity and growth while protecting margins.

Global headwinds — from softer spending in China to currency swings and weaker tourist flows — have cooled sales at many high‑end brands. Houses are managing inventories more cautiously and weighing up where to invest in stores, digital and clienteling without diluting brand equity.

Debt adds to the challenge, constraining optionality on acquisitions, beauty licensing and supply‑chain upgrades. A leadership refresh could pave the way for refinancing moves or partnerships designed to ease balance‑sheet strain.

Strategy will likely focus on deepening core categories while being selective on collaborations and diffusion lines, as consumers scrutinise value and trade down in some markets. Maintaining pricing power while sustaining desirability remains the central tension.

Investors, suppliers and Italian authorities will watch for continuity in creative direction and clearer guidance on costs, jobs and growth. The reshuffle comes as peers also reset leadership teams to navigate a slower cycle without eroding long‑term brand strength.
