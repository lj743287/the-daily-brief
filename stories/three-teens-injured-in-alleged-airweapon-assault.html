Three teens injured in alleged air‑weapon assault

Police are investigating after three teenagers were hurt in an attack reportedly involving men armed with an air weapon. Officers appealed for witnesses as patrols were stepped up.

Police in Wales are investigating after three teenagers were injured in an incident that reportedly involved men armed with an air weapon. Officers said enquiries are under way to establish the full circumstances and to identify all those involved.

Additional patrols have been deployed in the area to reassure residents while detectives gather witness statements and review any available footage. The force has encouraged people not to speculate on social media while the investigation is active.

An appeal has been issued for information, including dashcam or doorbell video from the time of the incident. Anyone who saw a group leaving the scene or who has knowledge of an air weapon recently acquired or discarded locally is urged to contact police or Crimestoppers.

Air weapons are subject to specific controls in the UK, including age restrictions on possession and requirements around secure storage. While they are distinct from firearms, officers treat any report of a weapon in public as a serious matter and respond accordingly.

Local schools and youth services have been briefed on the incident, with safeguarding teams reminded of referral routes if concerns arise. Victim support services have also been notified to provide assistance to those affected and their families.

No arrests had been confirmed at the time of writing. Police said updates would follow as significant developments occur and asked the public to report rumours to officers rather than sharing unverified claims online.
