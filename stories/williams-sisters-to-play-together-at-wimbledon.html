Williams sisters to play together at Wimbledon

Serena Williams has received a Wimbledon doubles wildcard to play alongside Venus Williams, confirming a notable return to the Championships for the sisters and one of the tournament’s biggest attention points.

Serena Williams has been handed a Wimbledon doubles wildcard to play alongside her sister Venus Williams. The pairing guarantees one of the tournament’s most watched storylines and marks a notable return to the Championships for the sisters.

The Williams sisters have shaped modern tennis for more than two decades, so their appearance together still carries unusual weight. Even with their careers at different stages, they remain capable of drawing attention far beyond the doubles draw itself.

For Wimbledon, the decision is also a reminder of the tournament’s ability to blend sporting competition with narrative appeal. A wildcard for Serena gives organisers a headline attraction, while fans will be watching to see how the sisters perform together in a field that has changed around them.

The main question now is how much tennis they can offer at this stage of their careers. Whatever the result, the pairing will be one of the event’s defining sights.
