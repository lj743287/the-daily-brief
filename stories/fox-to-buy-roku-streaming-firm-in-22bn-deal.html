Fox to buy Roku streaming firm in $22bn deal

Fox has agreed to buy Roku in a $22bn deal, betting that combining streaming, sport and news will help it compete as television audiences continue to migrate online.

Fox has agreed to buy Roku in a $22bn deal, betting that a larger streaming business can help it compete as television audiences keep moving online. The merger brings together a major media name with a platform that sits at the centre of the connected-TV market.

The logic is straightforward: combining streaming, sport and news could give Fox a stronger position in a market where viewers are fragmenting and legacy TV advertising is under pressure. Roku’s reach into homes also gives the deal strategic value beyond its direct revenues.

The transaction will still need to clear regulatory and other approvals, and large media deals often face scrutiny over competition and content control. But the broader trend is clear: traditional broadcasters are trying to buy their way into the streaming era rather than merely adapt to it.

For viewers and advertisers, the significance lies in how content is packaged and distributed. If the deal goes through, it could influence who controls access to streaming audiences and how much power large media groups have over the viewing experience.
