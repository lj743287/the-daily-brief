Trump tells BBC that King's visit could 'absolutely' help repair relations with UK

In a BBC phone interview, Donald Trump said next week’s visit by King Charles III could help repair relations with the UK, adding a diplomatic note to his increasingly combative remarks about Keir Starmer and British policy.

Donald Trump has said the forthcoming visit by King Charles III could help mend relations with the UK, offering a softer note alongside his recent criticism of Sir Keir Starmer and British policy.

In a phone interview with the BBC’s North America editor, Trump suggested the state visit next week could play a diplomatic role. His remarks came as he continued to press the UK government over trade, defence and the wider relationship with Washington.

The comment underlines the unusual mix of ceremony and politics that often shapes Anglo-American ties. Royal visits can provide a public display of continuity, but they do not remove the underlying disputes between governments.

What happens next will depend less on symbolism than on whether London and Washington can stabilise their disagreements over tariffs, security and the war in Iran. The King’s visit may create a more courteous backdrop, but it is not, on its own, a settlement.
