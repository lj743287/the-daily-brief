UK watchdog to investigate Telegram over alleged child sexual abuse material

Ofcom has launched an investigation into Telegram after receiving evidence that alleged child sexual abuse material was being shared on the messaging platform.

Ofcom has launched an investigation into Telegram after receiving evidence that alleged child sexual abuse material was being shared on the messaging platform.

The case is significant because it tests how far the UK’s Online Safety Act can force large platforms to prevent the spread of illegal content, even when they operate at global scale.

Telegram has long been criticised for the difficulty of moderating content on its service, and any finding against it could have wider implications for messaging apps that rely on encrypted or semi-private spaces.

The regulator’s inquiry follows evidence from the Canadian Centre for Child Protection, but the outcome is not yet known and Telegram has said it categorically denies the accusations in related reporting.

What happens next will help determine whether the new legal framework can produce meaningful enforcement rather than simply more warnings.
