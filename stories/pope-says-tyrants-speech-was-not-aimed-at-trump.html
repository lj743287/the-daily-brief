Pope says 'tyrants' speech was not aimed at Trump

Pope Francis has said his remarks about “tyrants” were misinterpreted after a public spat with Donald Trump, attempting to lower the temperature after the exchange drew wide attention.

Pope Francis has said his remarks about “tyrants” were misinterpreted after a public clash with Donald Trump, in an effort to cool a row that drew wide attention. The pontiff’s clarification suggests he wants to lower the temperature after a brief but visible transatlantic spat.

The dispute mattered because it touched on the relationship between religion and politics, and on the influence the pope still wields when he speaks about leaders and power. Even a short exchange can quickly become part of a wider culture war.

Francis’s explanation appears intended to narrow the dispute rather than prolong it. He has not changed the substance of his concerns, but he is signalling that his comments were not a direct attack on the US president.

The episode also shows how quickly papal remarks can be folded into partisan argument, especially in the US. That leaves room for both sides to claim they have been misunderstood, while the original message risks getting lost.

For now, the Vatican seems to be trying to draw a line under the argument before it hardens into a larger diplomatic or political issue.
