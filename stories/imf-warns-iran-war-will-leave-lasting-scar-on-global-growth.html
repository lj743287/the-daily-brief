IMF warns Iran war will leave lasting ‘scar’ on global growth

Kristalina Georgieva says even a durable peace would not quickly reverse the conflict’s drag on investment and living standards. The Fund is preparing growth downgrades as fragmentation and supply shocks harden.

The IMF has warned that the conflict involving Iran is likely to leave a lasting scar on the world economy, with weaker investment and slower productivity even if a durable peace takes hold.

Businesses are repricing geopolitical risk, fragmenting supply chains and delaying capital spending, developments that can depress potential growth across advanced and emerging markets.

Energy and shipping shocks are lifting operating costs and the likely floor for inflation, complicating central banks’ efforts to guide price pressures back to target.

Highly indebted emerging economies face tighter external financing and rising subsidy bills, as governments cushion households and firms from energy and food price spikes.

In richer countries, resources are being diverted towards resilience, redundancy and defence, choices that may crowd out more productive investment in the near term.

The Fund is preparing growth downgrades and urges targeted support and structural reform, while cautioning that the economic scarring from conflict and fragmentation is typically slow to heal.
