Asos demands £7m from US as firms rush to claim tariff refunds

Asos is demanding £7m from the US as hundreds of thousands of firms rush to claim refunds after tariffs were struck down.

Asos is demanding £7m from the US as hundreds of thousands of companies rush to claim refunds after tariffs were struck down.

The case is part of a wider scramble by businesses to recover money they say they overpaid under trade measures that have since been invalidated. For retailers such as Asos, the sums can be significant and the legal process may be worth pursuing even if the timing is uncertain.

The broader significance is that tariff policy can leave a long tail of claims, disputes and administrative work even after a court ruling or policy change. Businesses that imported goods under the higher rates now have an incentive to recover as much as they can.

It remains unclear how many firms will succeed or how quickly any refunds will arrive. But the episode shows how trade policy can continue to affect company cash flow long after the headline tariffs have gone.
