Van Aert beats Pogacar in thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint

Wout van Aert has won Paris-Roubaix after a dramatic sprint finish against Tadej Pogacar, one of the season’s standout one-day races.

Wout van Aert has won Paris-Roubaix after a dramatic sprint finish against Tadej Pogacar, sealing one of the standout one-day races of the season. The victory came after a brutal contest in which both riders were pushed to the limit before the final decisive move.

The result matters because Paris-Roubaix rewards resilience as much as raw power. Beating a rider of Pogacar’s calibre in that setting underlines van Aert’s standing among cycling’s elite and adds another major line to his classics record.

For Pogacar, defeat in such a tight finish is no disgrace, but it will still sharpen the sense that the spring classics are becoming an even fiercer battleground. For van Aert, it is a prize that confirms he remains capable of winning the sport’s hardest and most unforgiving races.
