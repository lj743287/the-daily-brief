Van Aert beats Pogacar in thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint

Wout van Aert’s victory in one of cycling’s most demanding races was a major result in a marquee classic.

Wout van Aert has won Paris-Roubaix after beating Tadej Pogacar in a dramatic sprint finish, a major result in one of cycling’s most demanding one-day races. The contest lived up to the classic’s reputation for attrition, dust and tactical brutality.

The victory matters because Roubaix is one of the sport’s most prestigious prizes and because Pogacar’s presence raised the stakes even further. Van Aert’s ability to outlast and outkick one of the modern era’s strongest riders gives the win particular weight.

It also reinforces how unpredictable the cobbled monuments can be, even when the strongest names are in the race. In a season where every major result is closely scrutinised, this one will stand out as both a marquee win and a reminder of van Aert’s range.
