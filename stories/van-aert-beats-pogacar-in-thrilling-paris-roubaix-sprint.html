Van Aert beats Pogacar in thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint

Wout van Aert won a classic battle with Tadej Pogacar to take one of cycling’s biggest one-day races.

Wout van Aert beat Tadej Pogacar in a thrilling sprint finish to win Paris-Roubaix, after one of the most compelling editions of cycling's toughest one-day race. The result underlined Van Aert's resilience and gave him a victory that will sit high among his biggest classics wins.

Pogacar, a four-time Tour de France champion, was central to the drama throughout the race, helping turn it into a rare head-to-head battle between two of the sport's biggest names. The contest mattered because it reminded fans how powerful a clash of elite riders can be in a race built on exhaustion, dust and brutality.

Paris-Roubaix remains one of cycling's great tests because it punishes any weakness in positioning, timing or endurance. Winning it requires not just strength but nerve, and Van Aert's sprint finish showed he had both when it mattered most.
