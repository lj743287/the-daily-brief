Van Aert beats Pogacar in thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint

Wout van Aert’s victory over Tadej Pogacar at Paris-Roubaix is the weekend’s stand-out cycling result, ending a long wait for the Belgian and underlining the race’s brutal unpredictability.

Wout van Aert has beaten Tadej Pogacar in a thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint to claim one of cycling’s most punishing and prestigious races. It is a major victory for the Belgian and a reminder of how unpredictable Roubaix can be.

The result matters because Pogacar is rarely beaten in such a setting, and Van Aert’s win underlines both his resilience and the brutality of the course. Paris-Roubaix is as much about surviving the cobbles as about having the strongest legs.

For cycling, the race offered exactly the kind of high-drama finish it is known for. For Van Aert, it ends a long wait for another marquee success and gives him a result that will define his season.
