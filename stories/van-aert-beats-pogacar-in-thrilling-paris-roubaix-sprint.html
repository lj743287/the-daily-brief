Van Aert beats Pogacar in thrilling Paris-Roubaix sprint

Wout van Aert has won Paris-Roubaix after edging Tadej Pogacar in a dramatic sprint finish. It is one of the season’s biggest one-day racing results and a major marker for the cobbled classics.

Wout van Aert has won Paris-Roubaix after edging Tadej Pogacar in a dramatic sprint finish, sealing one of the standout one-day results of the cycling season. The victory adds another major cobbled classic to Van Aert’s record and denies Pogacar a famous win in only his first appearance at the race.

Paris-Roubaix remains one of cycling’s most punishing tests, with rough pavé sectors often deciding the outcome long before the finish. In a race where bike handling and resilience matter as much as raw climbing talent, Van Aert’s ability to hold his nerve against one of the sport’s biggest names underlines why the event is so prized.

For Pogacar, the result will still be read as evidence that he can contend on terrain far beyond his usual strengths, even if the final sprint went against him. For the wider classics picture, it confirms that the spring remains open and unpredictable, with the biggest riders now converging on the hardest races.
