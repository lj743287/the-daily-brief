Has Salah played last Liverpool game? Slot says 'wait and see'

Arne Slot says Liverpool will have to wait and see whether Mohamed Salah has played his final game for the club. The uncertainty over Salah adds a major transfer and squad-planning question to Liverpool’s close season.

Liverpool manager Arne Slot says supporters will have to wait and see whether Mohamed Salah has played his final game for the club.

The uncertainty is significant because Salah remains one of Liverpool's defining players and any suggestion that his time at Anfield could be ending would shape transfer planning, squad building and the team's attacking future.

Slot's comments do not confirm an exit, but they keep open a question that would have major sporting and commercial implications for the club.

Liverpool are approaching a period when decisions about senior players matter as much as results, and Salah's status will be watched closely by fans and rivals alike.

For now, the club's message appears to be caution rather than clarity. That may be tactical, or it may reflect genuine uncertainty about what comes next.

Either way, the answer will affect not just Liverpool's summer, but the structure of the side for the season ahead.
