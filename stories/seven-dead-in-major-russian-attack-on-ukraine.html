Seven dead in major Russian attack on Ukraine

At least seven people were killed in a major Russian attack on Ukraine, with Dnipro hardest hit and officials saying four died in a strike on a residential building. The attack again shows the continuing vulnerability of Ukrainian cities despite months of war.

At least seven people have been killed in a major Russian attack on Ukraine, with the city of Dnipro among the hardest hit.

Officials said four of the dead were in a strike on a residential building in Dnipro, reinforcing how often civilian areas remain exposed despite repeated international calls to limit attacks on non-military targets.

The attack is another reminder that Ukrainian cities remain vulnerable to large-scale strikes even after months of war, with ordinary housing and infrastructure still bearing the cost.

Dnipro has repeatedly been struck during the conflict because of its strategic location and role as a major regional centre, and each new attack deepens the humanitarian toll.

The wider significance is familiar but grim: Russia continues to show it can project force deep into Ukraine, while Kyiv must keep diverting resources to air defence and emergency response.

Any further escalation would likely add to pressure on Ukraine’s allies, who are watching closely for signs of whether Moscow is seeking military advantage or simply prolonging the conflict.
