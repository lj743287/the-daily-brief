Slade’s late kick sends Exeter into Challenge Cup semi‑final

Henry Slade held his nerve with a last‑minute penalty against Benetton to book Exeter’s place. The Premiership side’s European campaign remains alive after a tense finish.

Henry Slade kept his nerve with a last‑minute penalty to edge Benetton and send Exeter into the European Challenge Cup semi‑finals. It was a finish befitting a match that had offered little daylight either way.

Exeter leaned on set‑piece and defensive organisation, conceding few clean looks and forcing Benetton to play from deep. Discipline, long a hallmark of knockout rugby, tipped the balance in the closing stages.

Slade’s composure and leadership were central throughout, guiding territory and tempo as well as delivering from the tee. In a youthful squad, his decision‑making remains a stabilising force.

The semi‑final brings logistical demands alongside tactical ones, with travel, quick turnarounds and Premiership commitments to juggle. Exeter’s depth will be tested as the domestic run‑in intensifies.

Benetton depart with credit after pushing the tie to the limit, a further sign of Italian rugby’s steady rise in European competition. The margins at this stage are slim; one infringement or one clean strike decides careers’ worth of toil.

For Exeter, the competition offers a direct route to silverware and confidence, particularly in a season of transition. Banked experience in such finishes is currency they can spend again in the weeks ahead.
