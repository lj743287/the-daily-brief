At least 30 die in crush at historic Haitian fortress

Crowds at the Citadelle Henry tourist site triggered a deadly crush, with many injured and missing. Authorities have launched an investigation and emergency response in a country already beset by instability.

At least 30 people were killed in a crowd crush at the Citadelle Henry in northern Haiti, with many others injured and some reported missing, according to officials cited by the BBC and the Guardian.

Authorities have launched an investigation and mobilised emergency services after the incident, which took place during a heavily attended event and drew many young people.

The 19th‑century mountaintop fortress, also known as the Citadelle Laferrière, is part of a historic complex that attracts large numbers of visitors despite difficult access routes.

Emergency response efforts were complicated by the site’s location, with narrow roads and limited medical capacity nearby likely slowing evacuations and treatment.

The tragedy comes amid a wider crisis marked by political instability, gang violence and overstretched public services, factors that have undermined Haiti’s ability to respond to disasters.

Officials appealed for calm and urged people to avoid large gatherings as assessments continue, with the possibility of international support being explored to assist victims and bolster crowd‑management measures.
