‘Too powerful for the public’: Inside Anthropic’s bid to win the AI publicity war

Anthropic says it withheld a model on cybersecurity grounds, but sceptics argue the move was also a publicity strategy as the firm competes for influence and investment.

Anthropic says it withheld a model on cybersecurity grounds, but sceptics argue the move was also part of a publicity strategy. The episode shows how fiercely AI companies are now competing not just on technology, but on credibility and influence.

The company says the model was too dangerous to release, a claim that immediately drew attention from banks, politicians and social media. Critics, however, see a familiar pattern: an apparently cautious announcement that also serves to raise the firm's profile and attract investment.

That tension sits at the heart of the AI boom. Firms want to appear responsible while still convincing the market that they are at the frontier, and those two goals do not always sit comfortably together.
