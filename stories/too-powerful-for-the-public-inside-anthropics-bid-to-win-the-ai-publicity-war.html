‘Too powerful for the public’: Inside Anthropic’s bid to win the AI publicity war

Anthropic’s claims about a withheld model raise familiar questions about safety, marketing and the politics of AI credibility.

Anthropic's claims about a model too powerful to release have put safety and marketing back at the centre of the AI debate. The company is presenting itself as more cautious than some rivals, but the message also functions as part of a wider competition for credibility in a crowded market.

The episode matters because AI companies are no longer competing only on technical performance. They are also competing on trust, trying to persuade regulators, journalists and customers that their systems are advanced enough to matter but controlled enough not to alarm the public.

That is a difficult line to hold. If a company stresses danger too strongly, it risks looking opportunistic or evasive; if it downplays the risk, it can seem reckless. Anthropic's challenge is to show that its warnings are a genuine safety stance rather than a branding exercise.
