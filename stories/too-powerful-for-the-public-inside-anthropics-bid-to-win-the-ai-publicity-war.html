‘Too powerful for the public’: Inside Anthropic’s bid to win the AI publicity war

Anthropic’s decision not to release a model has become a reputational and regulatory campaign, as firms compete to define AI safety, power and legitimacy.

Anthropic has made a strategic choice not to release one of its models, turning a technical decision into a broader argument about power, safety and legitimacy. In doing so, it is trying to position itself as the company that takes AI risks more seriously than its rivals.

That approach matters because the AI industry is fighting for public trust at the same time as regulators are asking harder questions about security, labour disruption and abuse. A firm that appears cautious may win credibility, but it also risks looking evasive or elitist.

The publicity battle is therefore not just about branding. It is about who gets to define what responsible AI looks like, who sets the standards and whether safety claims are backed by transparent evidence or simply by messaging.

Anthropic’s challenge is that restraint can be read in two ways: as prudence or as a marketing tactic. As the technology becomes more capable, that distinction is likely to matter more, not less.
