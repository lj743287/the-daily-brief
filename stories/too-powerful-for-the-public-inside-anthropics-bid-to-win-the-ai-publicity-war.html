‘Too powerful for the public’: Inside Anthropic’s bid to win the AI publicity war

Anthropic’s claims about its latest model have prompted scepticism as well as alarm, illustrating the race to control the narrative around frontier AI.

Anthropic’s latest claims about its model have drawn both attention and scepticism, highlighting the intensifying struggle among frontier AI firms to control the public narrative. As the technology advances, questions about safety and governance have become almost as important as the models themselves.

The company’s message appears designed to present its systems as powerful but responsible, at a moment when rivals are also trying to reassure regulators, customers and the public. That public-relations battle matters because trust is becoming a commercial asset in its own right.

What is uncertain is how much such claims can persuade outside the industry. In a field marked by secrecy, hype and rapid change, scepticism is likely to remain high, especially when companies are asking the public to accept that systems are both transformative and safely contained.
