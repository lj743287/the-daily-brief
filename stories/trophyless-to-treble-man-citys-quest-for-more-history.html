Trophyless to treble? Man City's quest for more history

Manchester City remain in contention for a domestic treble after a season that looked far less secure last year, when they were staring at a trophyless campaign. Their run to another final underlines the club’s sustained dominance.

Manchester City remain in contention for a domestic treble, a striking turnaround from this time last year when they were staring at a first trophyless season since 2017.

The shift matters because it shows how quickly City can move from uncertainty to another chance at making history. Even a difficult campaign has not stopped them from competing on multiple fronts.

Their run to another final reinforces the club's sustained dominance and the depth of squad and coaching resources that allow them to reset after setbacks.

For rivals, that consistency is part of the challenge: City can absorb a poor spell and still end the season chasing silverware.

The story is not only about trophies, but about expectation. At City, anything less than competing for major honours is treated as failure.

If they finish the job, the club will again underline how difficult it is for English football's other elite sides to keep pace over an entire season.
