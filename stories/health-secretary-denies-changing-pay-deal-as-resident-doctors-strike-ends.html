Health secretary denies changing pay deal as resident doctors’ strike ends

Wes Streeting rejected claims the junior doctors’ settlement had been altered during a six‑day walkout. The dispute highlights the difficulty of restoring NHS staffing stability amid financial constraints.

Wes Streeting has rejected claims that the junior doctors’ pay settlement was altered during a six‑day walkout in England, the BBC reported, as the industrial action ended on Monday morning.

The strike marked the latest phase in a long‑running dispute over pay and conditions, which has tested efforts to restore staffing stability across the NHS.

Ministers say the agreement strikes a balance between fairness to staff and fiscal responsibility, while union voices remain divided over how far it moves towards pay restoration.

Prolonged industrial action has disrupted elective recovery plans and created operational pressures, even where trusts have mitigated the impact through rescheduling and contingency rotas.

The episode underscores the fragility of workforce morale after successive stoppages across multiple staff groups, and the complexity of translating national deals into local staffing improvements.

Officials argue that medium‑term gains in retention and recruitment will depend on delivering the NHS workforce plan and sustained funding, alongside reforms to training and career progression.
