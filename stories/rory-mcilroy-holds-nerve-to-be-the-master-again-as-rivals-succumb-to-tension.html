Rory McIlroy holds nerve to be the Master again as rivals succumb to tension

McIlroy has defended his Masters title, joining an elite group of repeat winners and confirming his place in golf’s modern record books.

Rory McIlroy has defended his Masters title at Augusta, confirming his place in golf’s modern record books and joining a very small group of repeat winners. The victory came after a tense final round in which he stayed calm while rivals stumbled under pressure.

The result matters because repeated success at Augusta is rare and because McIlroy’s stature in the game has now been reinforced in the most demanding way possible: by backing up a major triumph. It also adds to the sense that he remains one of the defining figures in men’s golf.

For his rivals, the message is clear. McIlroy still has the composure to win on the biggest stage, and the long chase for major consistency has only been strengthened by this defence. What comes next will be whether he can extend that form beyond Augusta.
