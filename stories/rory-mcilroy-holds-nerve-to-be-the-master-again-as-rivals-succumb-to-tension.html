Rory McIlroy holds nerve to be the Master again as rivals succumb to tension

Rory McIlroy defended the Masters at Augusta National, becoming only the fourth golfer to retain the title and matching Nick Faldo’s haul of six major championships.

Rory McIlroy has defended his Masters title at Augusta National, becoming only the fourth golfer to retain the green jacket and matching Nick Faldo’s tally of six major championships. The victory confirms his place among the game’s greats and adds another layer to a career that once seemed defined by missed chances at Augusta.

This was a tournament in which McIlroy was not at his best for long stretches, but he still found enough to finish on 12 under and win by one shot from Scottie Scheffler. That matters because it shows how elite golf is often decided not by brilliance alone but by the ability to hold nerve when others tighten up.

The result also deepens the historical comparison. Few players have managed to defend the Masters, and McIlroy now joins a small group that includes Jack Nicklaus, Faldo and Tiger Woods. For a player who had spent years chasing an Augusta breakthrough, this is no longer redemption alone but sustained excellence.

The question now is how far he can climb in the sport’s all-time rankings. With six major titles, he has already moved beyond Seve Ballesteros, and the record books are now the clear next target.
