Rory McIlroy holds nerve to be the Master again as rivals succumb to tension

Rory McIlroy has defended his Masters title at Augusta, becoming only the fourth golfer to retain it and adding a sixth major after a one-shot win over Scottie Scheffler.

Rory McIlroy has defended his Masters title at Augusta, becoming only the fourth golfer to do so and claiming a sixth major with a one-shot win over Scottie Scheffler. The 36-year-old's latest triumph confirms that last year's breakthrough was no one-off.

The significance is partly statistical: only Jack Nicklaus, Nick Faldo and Tiger Woods had previously managed to retain the Masters in the modern era. For McIlroy, it also closes another chapter in a career long shaped by near-misses and expectation.

He was not at his best for much of the tournament, but the win underlines a different kind of greatness - resilience under pressure. The result will now fuel fresh debate over where he stands among golf's all-time elite.
