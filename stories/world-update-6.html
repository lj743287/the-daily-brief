World update 6

A significant development in world with wider consequences under review.

Donald Trump has cancelled a planned trip by US envoys to Pakistan for talks linked to the Iran war, underscoring how the conflict is unsettling American diplomacy across the region.

Iran had already said there were no plans for a direct meeting with the US delegation led by Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, which suggested the talks were fragile even before they were shelved.

The wider significance is that regional governments are now being pulled into a conflict that is reshaping security, trade and political calculations well beyond the battlefield.

Pakistan in particular sits at the edge of several overlapping pressures: its ties with Washington, its proximity to Iran and the broader instability affecting energy and shipping routes.

The cancellation may simply reflect a pause in planning, but it could also signal deeper difficulty in finding a credible negotiating track while the war continues.

Either way, the episode is a reminder that the Iran war is now a diplomatic problem as much as a military one.
