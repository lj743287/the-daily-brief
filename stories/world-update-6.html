World update 6

A significant development in world with wider consequences under review.

Viktor Orbán's defeat in Hungary is the most significant political shift in this world update, signalling the end of a 16-year era and the arrival of Péter Magyar as the country's new standard-bearer. The result will be watched closely in Brussels and Moscow alike.

Orbán's fall matters because Hungary has long been a barrier to European consensus on issues such as sanctions, rule-of-law disputes and support for Ukraine. A change in Budapest could ease some of those tensions, although the size of the transition will only become clear once Magyar begins to govern.

The wider message is that established power blocs remain vulnerable when economic frustration, institutional fatigue and personal politics converge. Europe's response to Hungary will now depend on whether the new leader proves willing to reset relations without simply inheriting the old confrontations.
