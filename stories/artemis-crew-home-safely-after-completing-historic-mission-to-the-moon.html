Artemis crew home safely after completing historic mission to the Moon

Four astronauts have returned safely after a nine-day mission that took them farther from Earth than any humans have been, marking a major milestone for the programme.

Four astronauts have returned safely to Earth after completing a nine-day Artemis mission that took them farther from the planet than any humans have yet travelled. Their splashdown marks a milestone for a programme that is intended to take NASA back to the Moon on a sustained basis.

The mission matters not only for its symbolism but also for the testing it gives NASA on spacecraft systems, crew safety and deep-space operations. Each successful flight helps build confidence for longer journeys that will eventually be needed if the agency is to reach the lunar surface and beyond.

For now, the safe return is the immediate achievement. The next question is how quickly the programme can move from demonstration to routine missions, and whether funding, technical setbacks or shifting political priorities could slow that path.
