Firms scramble to be ‘seen’ by AI search

Companies are restructuring websites and data so chatbots and AI search engines surface their content. The shift threatens to upend SEO playbooks and distribution economics.

As AI‑generated answers proliferate atop search results, companies are reorganising websites and data so that chatbots and AI search engines surface their content, the BBC reports. The shift threatens to erode traditional search‑engine optimisation and the traffic models built around it.

Tactics include richer structured data using Schema.org, concise FAQs in plain language, machine‑readable sitemaps, and canonical pages designed to be summarised. Some firms publish evergreen explainers and glossary entries to feed retrieval‑augmented systems, while experimenting with licensing so their material is ingested with attribution.

Attribution and measurement are proving difficult. Referral data from AI products is sparse, and firms report more ‘zero‑click’ sessions where answers appear without visits. New tools aim to detect citations, track model mentions and watermark snippets, but coverage is patchy and inconsistent across platforms.

Product design is changing accordingly: developer documentation is being rewritten into smaller, self‑contained chunks, while customer‑support portals expose verified answers with provenance. Paywalled publishers are weighing teaser summaries or machine‑readable abstracts to avoid being bypassed yet still credited.

Risks abound. Hallucinated prices or specifications can create liability in regulated sectors; content laundering may strip brands from sourced material; and automated answers can crowd out niche providers. Antitrust and copyright questions loom over scraping practices and whether platforms privilege their own content.

Economically, the funnel is shifting from click‑throughs to brand presence inside AI answers. That pushes companies towards direct subscriptions, newsletters and communities, while seeking data‑licensing revenue. Agencies are rewriting playbooks around information architecture, structured data and relationship‑building with AI platforms.
