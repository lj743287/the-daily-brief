Koch wins Paris‑Roubaix Femmes as Brand injured in spectator collision

Germany’s Franziska Koch edged Marianne Vos to take the women’s title. Lucinda Brand suffered neck pain after colliding with a fan, renewing safety concerns on crowded sections of the course.

Germany’s Franziska Koch edged Marianne Vos to win Paris‑Roubaix Femmes, prevailing from a select group after a tense finale on the Roubaix velodrome.

The front of the race coalesced through the most punishing sectors, where repeated accelerations and slips on the cobbles whittled the contenders. Vos, one of the sport’s defining figures, was forced to settle for second as marginal gains in the closing metres proved decisive.

The day was marred by an incident involving Lucinda Brand, who collided with a spectator and suffered neck pain. Initial reports pointed to an impact on a crowded section of the route, renewing focus on rider safety in races that funnel through narrow, exposed roads.

Spectator proximity to the racing line has been a recurring concern on cobbled classics, where barriers and marshals cannot cover every metre. Organisers and governing bodies face fresh questions over crowd management, particularly at sector entries and exits where visibility and space are most compromised.

Since its launch in 2021, Paris‑Roubaix Femmes has grown rapidly in prestige and tactical complexity. A German winner against a field long dominated by Dutch strength underlines the depth now present across WorldTour teams and the increasing unpredictability of the women’s spring calendar.

Attention turns to how squads adjust positioning strategies and equipment for the remaining spring races, and whether additional stewarding or targeted barriers will be trialled next year. For Koch, the win sets a powerful platform for the rest of the classics and beyond.
