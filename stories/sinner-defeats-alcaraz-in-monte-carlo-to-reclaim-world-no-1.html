Sinner defeats Alcaraz in Monte Carlo to reclaim world No 1

Jannik Sinner won the Masters 1000 final in straight sets, adding to Indian Wells and Miami titles to cap a dominant spring. The result reorders the men’s rankings ahead of the clay‑court swing.

Jannik Sinner beat Carlos Alcaraz 7-6 (5), 6-3 to win the Monte Carlo Masters and return to world No 1. The Italian added a third Masters 1000 title of the year to cap a dominant spring.

The opening set hinged on a tight tie‑break in which Sinner’s weight of shot and composure proved decisive. He then accelerated through the second with cleaner first‑strike tennis and assured serving under pressure.

This victory follows his Indian Wells and Miami triumphs, underlining an all‑surface consistency that has reordered the men’s rankings heading into the European clay swing. Alcaraz, who has set the pace in recent seasons, now chases.

The contest offered another data point in a rivalry that is reshaping the tour’s hierarchy. Sinner’s improved return game and balance off both wings limited Alcaraz’s capacity to dictate with forehand aggression.

For Italian tennis, Sinner’s ascent signals sustained relevance at the sport’s summit beyond the hard‑court season. His trajectory now draws a natural line towards Roland Garros with expectation rather than hope.

Attention turns to Madrid and Rome before Paris, with ranking points and seeding implications at stake. Alcaraz will seek a response on higher‑bouncing clay, while Sinner aims to consolidate his lead with further deep runs.
