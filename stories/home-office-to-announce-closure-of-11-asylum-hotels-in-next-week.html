Home Office to announce closure of 11 asylum hotels in next week

Labour is moving to shut more asylum hotels, but the policy still leaves difficult questions about accommodation, costs and political pressure.

The Home Office is expected to announce the closure of 11 asylum hotels next week, in another sign that Labour is trying to reduce the visible use of temporary accommodation for people seeking asylum.

The political pressure is obvious. Asylum hotels have become a symbol of a system under strain, with ministers facing criticism over cost, community impact and the long-running backlog in processing claims.

But closing hotels does not by itself solve the underlying problem. The government still needs more stable accommodation, faster decision-making and a plan that can survive legal, operational and political scrutiny.

The key question is whether the closures mark the start of a more durable reset or simply shift pressure elsewhere in an already overstretched asylum system.
