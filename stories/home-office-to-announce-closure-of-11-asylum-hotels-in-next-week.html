Home Office to announce closure of 11 asylum hotels in next week

Ministers are moving to reduce hotel use for asylum housing, a politically sensitive policy shift that will test delivery and capacity.

The Home Office is preparing to announce the closure of 11 asylum hotels, signalling a further attempt to reduce the use of temporary accommodation for people seeking refuge in the UK. The policy is politically sensitive because hotel use has become a flashpoint in debates over immigration, cost and local pressure.

The closures matter because asylum housing remains one of the most visible and contentious parts of the system. Ministers want to bring down costs and reduce reliance on hotels, but doing so requires alternative accommodation, administrative capacity and a faster pace of decision-making.

What remains uncertain is whether the closures mark the start of a sustained reduction or a limited rearrangement. If the Home Office cannot replace hotel places quickly enough, pressure is likely to shift elsewhere rather than disappear.
