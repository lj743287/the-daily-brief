Home Office to announce closure of 11 asylum hotels in next week

Ministers are preparing a visible move on asylum accommodation as pressure mounts to show progress on reducing hotel use. The central test is whether closures translate into a more durable housing and processing system.

Ministers are preparing to announce the closure of 11 asylum hotels as pressure continues to mount over the use of hotel accommodation. It is intended as a visible sign of progress on a problem that has become politically toxic.

But shutting hotels is only part of the challenge. The real test is whether asylum seekers are moved into a system that is more stable, cheaper and better managed, rather than simply shifting the pressure elsewhere in the short term.

The government wants to show that it is reducing reliance on hotels, but success will depend on processing claims faster and securing alternative accommodation. Without that, the closures may look more like a temporary easing than a durable fix.
