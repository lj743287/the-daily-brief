Faisal Islam: Why the government is relaxed about Chinese car imports

The government’s stance suggests it sees cheap Chinese EVs as a consumer gain and an industrial challenge rather than an outright threat.

The government appears relaxed about the rise of Chinese car imports because it sees them as both a consumer benefit and an industrial challenge. Cheap electric vehicles can help households and accelerate the shift away from petrol and diesel, even if they intensify pressure on domestic manufacturers.

That stance reflects a broader dilemma for ministers: protecting strategic industry versus giving consumers lower prices and more choice. On balance, the government seems to believe that blocking imports would do more harm than good, especially while the UK is trying to expand EV adoption.

The unanswered question is whether domestic producers can adapt quickly enough. If Chinese brands continue to gain ground, Britain may need to decide whether support should focus on competitiveness, resilience and supply chains rather than trying to hold back the market.
