Australia urges Israel to halt Lebanon strikes as tensions persist

Prime Minister Anthony Albanese called for an immediate stop to intensified Israeli operations in Lebanon and urged Hezbollah to cease reprisals. Canberra extended its regional surveillance deployment amid concern the Middle East ceasefire could fray.

Australian prime minister Anthony Albanese called for an immediate halt to intensified Israeli operations in Lebanon and urged Hezbollah to cease reprisals, warning that a fragile Middle East ceasefire could fray, the Guardian reported.

Canberra also extended its regional surveillance deployment, describing it as a contribution to situational awareness and maritime security as tensions ripple across borders.

Australia framed its appeal as support for de‑escalation that includes Lebanon, noting the risk of a broader conflict if cross‑border exchanges continue despite pauses elsewhere.

Israel says its actions respond to attacks launched from Lebanese territory, while Hezbollah has claimed retaliation; these assertions could not be independently verified.

Humanitarian agencies have reported growing needs in southern Lebanon and northern Israel, where periodic strikes have disrupted livelihoods and displaced residents.

Diplomatic attention now focuses on whether back‑channel arrangements can curb hostilities and reinforce ceasefire mechanisms before the situation hardens into sustained confrontation.
