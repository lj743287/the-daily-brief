Trump says he will ‘probably put a big tariff on the UK’ if it doesn’t drop digital services tax

Donald Trump has threatened the UK with a tariff unless ministers drop the 2% digital services tax, raising the stakes in a dispute over how American tech firms are taxed.

Donald Trump has threatened the UK with a new tariff unless ministers drop the 2% digital services tax on large US technology companies, escalating pressure on Sir Keir Starmer’s government.

The tax, introduced in 2020, applies to the revenues of several big American tech firms. Trump has accused Britain of trying to “make an easy buck” from US companies, turning what was already a sensitive tax dispute into a broader trade confrontation.

The issue matters because it goes to the heart of how governments tax digital businesses and how the US responds when those taxes target its most profitable firms. Britain has been trying to project stability in the transatlantic relationship, but this threat suggests that leverage remains with Washington.

It is not yet clear whether the warning will become a formal tariff or remain bargaining pressure. But the risk is that a technical tax row becomes another source of friction in an already unsettled global trading environment.
