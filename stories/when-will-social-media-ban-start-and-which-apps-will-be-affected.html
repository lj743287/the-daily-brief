When will social media ban start, and which apps will be affected?

The proposed ban is expected to affect apps including TikTok and Snapchat in early 2027, with the government still defining how age checks and platform enforcement will work in practice.

The proposed social media ban for under-16s is expected to begin in early 2027, with apps including TikTok and Snapchat among those likely to be affected. The government has not yet fully set out how the system will work in practice.

The main challenge is enforcement. Age checks can be bypassed, and any rule that relies on platforms policing millions of users will depend on technology that is not yet fully tested at scale.

That makes this less a simple ban than a large and complicated regulatory project. Ministers are trying to draw a line around childhood online while avoiding a system that is either easy to evade or too intrusive to be workable.

The next few months will show whether the government can turn broad political intent into a practical framework. Until then, the key uncertainties are which apps, which checks and which penalties will apply.
