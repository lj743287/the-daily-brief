‘Your photos will be deleted’: Apple users warned over ‘nasty’ iCloud storage scam

The scam uses panic around cloud storage limits to trick users into handing over personal and financial information.

Apple users are being warned about a scam that threatens to delete photos unless victims respond, exploiting the panic many people feel about cloud storage and personal data. The fraud appears designed to pressure users into handing over information quickly before they have time to check it.

The danger is not only financial but personal, because access to cloud accounts can expose images, contacts and other sensitive material. Scams that use fear of losing photos or files are effective precisely because they target something people instinctively want to protect.

The warning is a reminder that cybercrime increasingly relies on psychological manipulation rather than technical sophistication alone. Users who receive such messages should treat them with caution, because urgent threats are often designed to create exactly the sort of rushed response scammers need.
