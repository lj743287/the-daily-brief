Rangers captain Tavernier to leave in summer after 11 years

James Tavernier says he will leave Rangers at the end of the season after 11 years at Ibrox, a significant change for the Scottish club.

Rangers captain James Tavernier will leave Ibrox at the end of the season after 11 years at the club.

His departure marks a significant change for Rangers, where Tavernier has been one of the most recognisable figures of the modern era and a long-serving leader on and off the pitch.

Such exits matter not only for squad planning but also for dressing-room continuity, especially at a club where expectations remain high every season.

The next question is how Rangers replace both his experience and his leadership, and whether they do so through an internal successor or the transfer market.

For now, the club is facing another summer of transition.
