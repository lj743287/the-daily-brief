Cardiff stages thriller while Women’s Six Nations favourites show strength

Wales and Scotland produced a dramatic Women’s Six Nations opener in Cardiff, where 10,569 watched Scotland’s late comeback win at the Principality Stadium.

Wales and Scotland produced a dramatic Women’s Six Nations opener in Cardiff, with 10,569 supporters at the Principality Stadium watching Scotland complete a late comeback. It was the kind of match that showed both the quality of the tournament and the continued challenge of giving women’s rugby the crowd it deserves.

The finish was the most striking part of the day. Wales appeared to have done enough, only for Scotland to claw their way back in the closing minutes and steal the win. That kind of late swing can define a championship, especially in a competition where momentum matters as much as points.

Elsewhere, the favourites also underlined their strength, which may mean the title race quickly narrows around the sides expected to contend. But Cardiff’s thriller offered something more immediate: proof that the women’s game can produce the same tension and drama that have long made the men’s tournament so compelling.

The attendance also points to a wider issue. The crowd was a record for a Wales-Scotland fixture in Wales, yet the atmosphere might have been better served by a more compact venue. The rugby was worthy of a bigger stage, even if the numbers still lag behind the quality on show.
