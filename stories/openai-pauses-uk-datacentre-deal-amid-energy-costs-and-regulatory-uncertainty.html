OpenAI pauses UK data‑centre deal amid energy costs and regulatory uncertainty

The AI firm has halted a UK data‑centre project that was part of a wider tech‑investment push. Rising power costs and an evolving regulatory regime were cited, dealing a setback to the UK’s bid to brand itself an ‘AI superpower’.

OpenAI has paused a UK data‑centre project, citing rising power costs and an evolving regulatory regime, in a setback to ministers’ efforts to market Britain as an “AI superpower”, according to the BBC.

The facility had been trailed as part of a wider tech‑investment push, with the government positioning large‑scale compute as critical to advanced AI research and commercial deployment. The pause will raise fresh questions about whether the UK can deliver the energy, land and permitting at the pace hyperscalers require.

Data centres are power‑hungry and sensitive to electricity prices and grid reliability. Following recent energy shocks, UK industrial users have faced volatile bills, while operators increasingly seek long‑term power purchase agreements and proximity to low‑carbon generation to contain costs and improve sustainability credentials.

The regulatory picture for AI and digital infrastructure is also in flux. While the UK has favoured a principles‑based approach distinct from the EU’s AI Act, companies still want clarity on obligations around data, safety and compute oversight before committing to multi‑billion‑pound capacity.

Investment may tilt towards locations offering cheaper power, faster permitting and rich incentives, from Nordic countries to US states competing aggressively for cloud and AI spend. The global race to secure hyperscale facilities has intensified as firms concentrate capacity in a few supportive jurisdictions.

OpenAI has not ruled out future UK investment, and the government insists Britain remains attractive given its research base and talent. The decision nonetheless underlines the gap between ambition and delivery when energy markets are tight and policy signals are still bedding in.
