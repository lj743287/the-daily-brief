Apple names new boss to replace Tim Cook after 15 years

Apple has confirmed John Ternus will succeed Tim Cook in September after 15 years at the helm, marking a major transition at the company as it confronts pressure in AI and hardware.

Apple has confirmed that John Ternus will succeed Tim Cook in September, ending a 15-year era under the chief executive who turned the company into one of the world’s most valuable businesses.

The transition is important because Apple is entering a period of pressure in both AI and hardware, with investors watching whether the company can stay ahead in new product cycles while remaining as commercially disciplined as it has been under Cook.

Ternus, who has led hardware engineering, is seen as a product-focused insider rather than a radical outsider, which may reassure staff and markets looking for continuity.

But the handover also raises questions about whether Apple can broaden beyond the iPhone, sharpen its response to rivals in artificial intelligence and keep its huge installed base engaged.

The next few months will be watched for signs of how much strategic change the new chief is prepared to make, and how much of Cook’s model he intends to preserve.
