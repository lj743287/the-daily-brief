Woman and child die after getting into difficulty in water at London park

A woman and child died after getting into difficulty in the water at Elthorne Park in west London, and police are working to identify the pair’s next of kin. The incident is being treated as tragic by the Metropolitan Police.

A woman and child have died after getting into difficulty in the water at Elthorne Park in west London.

The Metropolitan Police described the incident as tragic and said they are working to identify the pair's next of kin. The immediate focus is on supporting the families and establishing exactly what happened.

Such incidents are rare but deeply distressing, especially when they involve a child and take place in a public park. They can also prompt renewed attention to local water safety and supervision.

Police have not set out further details, and it is not yet clear whether the deaths were the result of an accident, a medical emergency or some other factor.

For the local community, the news will be especially painful because park settings are usually associated with family leisure, not fatal danger.

Further information from investigators may follow once relatives have been informed and the circumstances are better understood.
