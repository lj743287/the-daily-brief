California coffee chain faces backlash over Pride flag removal plan

Philz Coffee drew criticism after announcing the removal of Pride flags as part of a move to standardise store decor. The company says it seeks a consistent ‘inclusive’ approach, but petitioners call the decision a snub to LGBTQ+ customers.

Philz Coffee is facing a backlash after confirming plans to remove Pride flags as part of a move to standardise store decor, The Guardian reported. The San Francisco‑based chain said the aim was to create a consistent, inclusive experience across locations.

A petition with more than 4,000 signatures criticised the decision as a snub to LGBTQ+ customers and staff. Campaigners argued the policy undermines a community that has supported the brand.

Philz said the change would include removing a variety of flags and non‑standard decorations, while reaffirming its commitment to inclusivity. The company has not announced a timetable for implementation.

The episode underscores how US consumer brands are navigating polarised cultural debates, with marketing and in‑store choices scrutinised by activists on all sides. Backlash can spread quickly across social platforms.

Retail analysts note that such decisions carry reputational risk and may affect employee morale and customer loyalty, particularly in core urban markets. Companies often face trade‑offs between uniformity and local expression.

Philz has not indicated whether it will modify the policy in response to criticism. How stores apply the guidance, and whether local managers retain discretion, may determine the scale of any lasting impact.
