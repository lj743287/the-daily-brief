Djibouti's president wins unprecedented sixth term with 97.8% of vote

Ismail Omar Guelleh has extended his long rule in an election boycotted by the main opposition, again highlighting the country’s constrained political competition.

Djibouti’s president, Ismail Omar Guelleh, has won an unprecedented sixth term after an election in which the main opposition was absent. The scale of the result reflects not only his long hold on power but also the weakness of political competition in the small Horn of Africa state.

Guelleh has now extended a rule that began more than two decades ago, while critics have long argued that opposition space in Djibouti is tightly constrained. Boycotts and limited contestation have made recent elections more about ratifying the status quo than testing it.

Djibouti matters well beyond its borders because of its strategic location at the mouth of the Red Sea and its role as a host to foreign military bases. That gives the country outsized geopolitical significance, even as domestic politics remain tightly managed.
