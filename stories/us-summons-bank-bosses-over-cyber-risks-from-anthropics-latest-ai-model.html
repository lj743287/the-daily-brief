US summons bank bosses over cyber risks from Anthropic’s latest AI model

US officials have summoned major bank chiefs in Washington after Anthropic’s Claude Mythos model prompted fresh concern about cyber security risk.

US officials have summoned major bank chiefs to Washington after concerns emerged over the cyber risks posed by Anthropic’s latest AI model, Claude Mythos. The meeting, which reportedly included Federal Reserve chair Jerome Powell, shows how quickly regulators are moving to assess the financial-sector implications of more capable AI systems.

The worry is straightforward: if a model can be used to automate or enhance cyber attacks, banks become a natural target. That makes the issue less about abstract technological progress and more about the resilience of a system that underpins payments, savings and credit.

The intervention also underlines how nervous policymakers have become about AI moving from productivity tool to security risk. For the banking sector, this is a warning that generative AI is now being treated not merely as a business opportunity but as a potential systemic threat.

What happens next will depend on how serious the vulnerabilities are judged to be and whether firms can prove their defences are keeping up. But the fact that the Treasury felt compelled to summon bank chiefs suggests official concern is already high.
