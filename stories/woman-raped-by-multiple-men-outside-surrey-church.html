Woman raped by multiple men outside Surrey church

Police said a woman in her 20s was attacked after leaving a nightclub in Epsom. The incident intensifies scrutiny of night-time safety and the policing of sexual violence.

A woman in her 20s was raped by several men outside Epsom Methodist Church in Surrey after being followed from a nightclub, police have said. The attack, in the town centre, is being treated as a serious sexual assault.

Detectives have launched an investigation to establish the sequence of events and identify the suspects. At the time of writing, police had not announced any arrests or released further details about the number of assailants or the precise timing.

The incident has intensified concern about safety in the night-time economy, particularly around routes home from venues and late transport hubs. Campaigners and local leaders have repeatedly highlighted the risks faced by women moving through town centres after dark, and the need for better stewardship of busy weekend hotspots.

Across the UK, the policing of sexual violence remains under scrutiny, with forces pledging improvements under national strategies to tackle violence against women and girls. While reporting of rape has risen in recent years, charge and conviction rates remain low, contributing to a persistent confidence gap among victims and the wider public.

In cases of this kind, investigators typically prioritise securing forensic evidence, mapping movements via CCTV and dashcam footage, and identifying witnesses who may have seen the victim or suspects in the area. Specialist support services are available to survivors of sexual assault, and the law protects their anonymity.

The case underscores the continuing challenge of keeping public spaces safe after dark while sustaining a vibrant night-time economy. Further updates are expected as officers build a fuller picture of what happened; information from the public can be pivotal to advancing such inquiries.
