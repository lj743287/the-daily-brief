EU orders Meta to open WhatsApp to rival AI chatbots

The European Union has ordered Meta to open WhatsApp to rival AI chatbots, a move Meta has denounced as regulatory overreach and one that could reshape access to one of the world’s largest messaging platforms.

The European Union has ordered Meta to open WhatsApp to rival AI chatbots, escalating its confrontation with one of the world’s biggest technology companies. Meta has denounced the move as regulatory overreach and warned that it could hand rivals, including OpenAI, wider access to the platform.

The ruling matters because WhatsApp is one of the largest messaging services in the world, and any change to access rules could alter how AI tools reach users. It also marks a further push by European regulators to prevent dominant platforms from closing off emerging markets.

For Meta, the decision is both commercial and strategic. If rival chatbots can plug into WhatsApp more freely, the company may lose some control over how its platform evolves. For the EU, the argument is that competition should not be blocked by gatekeepers.

What happens next will depend on compliance, legal challenge and whether the order survives further scrutiny. But the decision underlines a broader European effort to shape the AI market before it hardens around a few dominant firms.
