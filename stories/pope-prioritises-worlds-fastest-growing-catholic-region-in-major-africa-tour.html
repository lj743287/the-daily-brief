Pope prioritises world's fastest-growing Catholic region in major Africa tour

Pope Leo XIV’s Africa visit is being framed as an effort to focus attention on a continent central to the Church’s future, influence and demographic growth.

Pope Leo XIV has begun an Africa tour that is being framed inside the Vatican as a sign of where the Catholic Church’s future lies: in a continent where the faith is growing quickly and where the Church still has major political and social influence.

The visit is intended to put Africa at the centre of global Catholic attention, not only as a mission field but as a source of new clergy, new congregations and new leadership for a changing Church. In many countries, Catholic institutions remain important providers of education, health care and civic authority.

The trip also comes at a moment when the Vatican is trying to balance pastoral diplomacy with the realities of conflict, poverty and demographic change across the continent. What the pope says about peace, governance and social justice will be watched closely both in Africa and beyond.
