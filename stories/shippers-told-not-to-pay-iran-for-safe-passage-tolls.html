Shippers told not to pay Iran for ‘safe passage’ tolls

Tanker operators are being urged to avoid any levies sought by Iran for Hormuz transit, amid legal and sanctions risks. The advice underscores a murky operating environment and the potential for seizures or detentions.

Tanker operators are being urged not to pay any levies sought by Iran for transit through the Strait of Hormuz, amid legal and sanctions risks highlighted in industry guidance reported by the BBC.

Advisers warn that such payments may breach sanctions regimes and do not guarantee immunity from enforcement actions, potentially voiding insurance and complicating liability in the event of incidents.

The recommendation underscores the murky operating environment, where masters and owners face acute choices under the threat of detention, seizure or prolonged inspection.

War‑risk premiums and additional cover are adding significant cost to voyages, prompting some owners to change flags, avoid the chokepoint or seek alternative fixtures with permissive routing.

Charterers are reviewing contractual protections, with selective force majeure language tested by prolonged disruption and by the ambiguity over what constitutes a safe and lawful passage.

Until a recognised transit framework emerges, ad‑hoc payments risk entangling cargoes in future legal disputes and complicating settlement flows between traders, refiners and financiers.
