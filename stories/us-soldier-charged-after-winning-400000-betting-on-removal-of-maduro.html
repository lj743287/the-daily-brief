US soldier charged after winning $400,000 betting on removal of Maduro

US authorities say Gannon Ken Van Dyke made more than $400,000 in trades on Polymarket after allegedly using classified information tied to Nicolás Maduro’s removal from office.

US authorities have charged Gannon Ken Van Dyke, a US soldier, after he allegedly made more than $400,000 in trades on Polymarket linked to the removal of Nicolás Maduro from office.

The justice department says the bets were based on classified information. If proved, that would turn the case into both a national security scandal and a test of how far prediction markets can be manipulated by people with inside access to military or intelligence material.

The allegations matter because they sit at the intersection of war planning, financial speculation and the growing reach of online betting platforms. Polymarket and similar markets have attracted attention for their ability to price political events, but this case suggests they can also create incentives for abuse.

Van Dyke’s case will now move through the US legal system, where prosecutors will have to show both what information he had and how it was used. The outcome may shape future scrutiny of prediction markets, particularly where national security is concerned.
