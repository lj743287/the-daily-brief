Thousands call on UK ministers to cut ties with US tech giant Palantir

More than 200,000 people have signed petitions urging ministers to break with Palantir over its work in the NHS, police, military and councils.

More than 200,000 people have signed petitions urging the UK government to break ties with Palantir, the US technology company with contracts across the NHS, police, military and local councils.

Campaigners say the company’s public-sector role is too extensive, pointing in particular to its £330m patient data contract with the NHS and its links to controversial uses of software abroad. Palantir has long faced criticism over its work with law enforcement and defence agencies.

The level of public opposition matters because it puts pressure on ministers to justify why the company remains so embedded in sensitive public services. It also feeds a wider debate about how much control governments should hand to private tech firms over data and infrastructure.

It is unclear whether the petitions will alter policy, but they raise the political cost of maintaining the relationship. Ministers are likely to face further questions over transparency, procurement and oversight.
