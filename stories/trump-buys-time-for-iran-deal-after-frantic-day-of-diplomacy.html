Trump buys time for Iran deal after frantic day of diplomacy

Donald Trump’s decision to hold back escalation after a frantic day of diplomacy gives Iran deal talks more time, but leaves the US, Israel and Iran facing continued uncertainty over the next step in a war already shaking oil markets, airline schedules and inflation forecasts.

Donald Trump’s decision to hold off on escalation has bought more time for diplomacy around Iran, but it has not reduced the sense of instability surrounding the war.

The conflict continues to move markets and planning far beyond the battlefield, with oil traders, airlines and governments all trying to judge whether the latest pause will hold.

That makes the moment important even without a dramatic breakthrough: every sign of restraint can ease pressure on fuel prices and aviation, while every reversal can quickly feed through to inflation and transport costs.

The problem for policymakers is that the situation remains fluid, and the gap between a temporary pause and a durable settlement is still very wide.
