Germany’s AfD adopts more radical manifesto ahead of key regional polls

The far-right Alternative for Germany sharpened its platform while polling strongly in Saxony-Anhalt. The move will test mainstream parties’ ability to contain the party’s rise amid economic anxieties and migration disputes.

The far‑right Alternative for Germany adopted a more radical manifesto ahead of crucial regional elections, as the party polls strongly in Saxony‑Anhalt, the BBC reported.

The move will test efforts by mainstream parties to contain the AfD’s rise, particularly in eastern states where economic anxieties and migration debates have influenced voter sentiment.

While full details of the platform were not immediately disclosed by broadcasters, party figures framed the changes as a sharpened stance on national sovereignty and domestic security, themes that have underpinned its recent campaigns.

Critics warned that a hardened programme could deepen social polarisation, and the party continues to face scrutiny from domestic intelligence services over elements of its organisation and rhetoric.

Any gains in Saxony‑Anhalt could bolster the AfD’s influence in regional parliaments and complicate coalition arithmetic, even as most established parties reject formal cooperation at state or federal level.

The outcome will be watched closely in Brussels and other EU capitals, where shifts in German regional politics are often read as early signals for national debates on migration, climate policy and relations with European institutions.
