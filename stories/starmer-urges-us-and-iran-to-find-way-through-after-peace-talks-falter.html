Starmer urges US and Iran to 'find way through' after peace talks falter

The prime minister is warning against escalation after the collapse of talks, while trying to keep the UK clear of direct involvement in the conflict.

Sir Keir Starmer has urged the US and Iran to “find a way through” after talks collapsed without agreement, warning against further escalation. His intervention reflects Britain’s interest in keeping the crisis contained while avoiding direct involvement.

The prime minister’s comments also show how much the UK is trying to balance its alliance with Washington against the risks of a wider regional conflict. That is a familiar position for Britain, which has often backed de-escalation in the Gulf while relying on US military power for deterrence.

The uncertainty now is whether diplomacy can be revived quickly enough to prevent fresh disruption to shipping and energy markets. If it cannot, European allies may face growing pressure to respond to a crisis they do not control.
