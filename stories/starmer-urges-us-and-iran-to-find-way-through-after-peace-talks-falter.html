Starmer urges US and Iran to 'find way through' after peace talks falter

The prime minister is pressing for restraint as talks collapse and tensions rise. The real test is whether Britain can keep some diplomatic leverage while avoiding deeper entanglement.

Sir Keir Starmer has urged the United States and Iran to find a way through their deepening dispute after negotiations faltered and tensions rose further.

The prime minister is trying to strike a careful line: supporting restraint while avoiding the impression that Britain is being drawn into the dispute on either side. That matters because the UK has long ties to Washington, but also interests in keeping Gulf shipping open and limiting the wider economic impact.

Britain’s leverage is limited, though not irrelevant. It can press for de-escalation through diplomatic channels and use its position with allies to encourage caution, but it cannot dictate the course of events if the confrontation hardens.

For Starmer, the test is whether Britain can remain a useful interlocutor without becoming entangled in a conflict it did not choose and cannot control.
