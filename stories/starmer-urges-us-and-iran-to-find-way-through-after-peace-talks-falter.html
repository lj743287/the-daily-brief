Starmer urges US and Iran to 'find way through' after peace talks falter

The prime minister is trying to project restraint as the Iran crisis deepens and pressure grows over Britain’s diplomatic and security posture.

Sir Keir Starmer has urged the US and Iran to find a way through their stalled negotiations, seeking to project calm as tensions around the conflict deepen. His intervention reflects Britain’s interest in avoiding further escalation while keeping diplomatic channels open.

The prime minister’s position is politically delicate. Britain is aligned with Washington on security, but it also has an interest in de-escalation because of the impact on energy markets, shipping and regional stability. That leaves London trying to sound supportive without appearing to endorse a wider war.

What happens next will depend on whether the two sides can resume contact and whether allies can help create space for talks. For now, the government is signalling restraint, but the scope for diplomacy appears narrower than before the negotiations failed.
