Carney on verge of Liberal majority government as votes cast in three by-elections

Mark Carney is close to securing a Liberal majority in Canada as three by-elections are held, with defections from the Conservatives strengthening his grip on power.

Mark Carney is close to securing a Liberal majority in Canada as voters go to the polls in three by-elections, a development that could further strengthen his hold on power.

The stakes are significant because one more seat could help turn his already improved position into something much more durable, especially after several Conservatives crossed the floor and joined the Liberals.

Carney’s advance reflects a broader political reordering in Ottawa, where stability and competence have become powerful selling points. The defections from the Conservatives suggest some lawmakers believe the government is the likelier vehicle for influence.

A majority would give the prime minister more room to govern without relying on fragile deals, but it would also sharpen scrutiny of how he brought together such an unlikely coalition of support.

The result of the by-elections will show whether that momentum is real or merely a temporary advantage.
