Peru election drags into second day after ballot delivery fiasco

Peru’s presidential and congressional election has spilled into a second day after technical and logistical failures disrupted voting, deepening doubts about the country’s electoral administration.

Peru’s presidential and congressional election has spilled into a second day after technical and logistical failures disrupted voting across parts of the country.

The delay has added to concerns about the state of Peru’s electoral administration at a time when trust in institutions is already fragile. Voters were meant to choose both a new president and members of Congress, but some polling stations were unable to operate normally.

The problems are the latest reminder of Peru’s political instability, which has seen repeated changes of government and growing public frustration with official incompetence and corruption.

Election authorities now face pressure to explain what went wrong and to show that delayed voting can still produce a result that is seen as credible.

The outcome may matter as much for confidence in the system as for the winner of the race itself.
