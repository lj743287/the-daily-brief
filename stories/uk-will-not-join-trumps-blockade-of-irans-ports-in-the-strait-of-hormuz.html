UK will not join Trump's blockade of Iran's ports in the Strait of Hormuz

The UK has ruled out joining Donald Trump’s blockade of Iran’s ports in the Strait of Hormuz, even as UK minesweepers and anti-drone capabilities remain in the region and pressure rises to reopen the shipping route.

The UK has ruled out joining Donald Trump's blockade of Iran's ports in the Strait of Hormuz, even as the strategic waterway remains under intense pressure after the collapse of peace talks. British minesweepers and anti-drone capabilities are still operating in the region, but ministers have drawn a line at taking part in any US-led blockade.

The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most important energy chokepoints, and any prolonged disruption would quickly affect shipping, insurance and fuel prices across Europe and Asia. That is why pressure is growing to reopen the route as fast as possible.

The government's position leaves it trying to balance support for allied naval security with caution about joining a step that could widen the conflict further. The UK has not broken with Washington entirely, but it is signalling that it does not want to be seen as endorsing economic warfare at sea.
