Iran carried out highest number of executions in decades

Iran has recorded its highest number of executions in decades, with rights groups warning that more people could be executed after the anti-government protests earlier this year.

Iran has carried out its highest number of executions in decades, according to rights groups that say the scale of the killings has risen sharply in the wake of anti-government protests earlier this year.

The figures matter not only because of their severity, but because organisations warn that many more people could still face execution. That has heightened concern about a wider crackdown on dissent as the authorities seek to reassert control.

The executions form part of a long-running pattern in Iran, where the death penalty has often been used as a tool of political intimidation as well as criminal punishment. But the latest surge is being seen as especially alarming by campaigners.

Rights groups are likely to keep pressing for transparency over detainees, charges and appeals, while governments that maintain relations with Tehran may come under renewed pressure to respond.

For families of prisoners and activists inside Iran, the immediate fear is that the worst may not be over.
