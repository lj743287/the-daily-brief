Ministers face summer deadline as British Steel said to need nationalisation

British Steel’s finances are said to be deteriorating to the point where only a state takeover before the summer would secure operations and protect jobs, according to people familiar with the situation. The prospect of nationalisation would mark a dramatic turn for a company seen as strategically important to the UK’s manufacturing base and major infrastructure projects.

Officials now face hard choices over how to balance fiscal caution with industrial capacity and regional employment. Any move to nationalise would raise complex questions about long-term investment needs, decarbonisation of heavy industry and the terms on which the state would eventually exit.

At the same time, ministers have signalled a relaxed approach to Chinese car imports, arguing that consumers benefit from lower prices and greater choice. The stance has unsettled parts of the domestic automotive supply chain, which is wrestling with the cost of the electric transition, rules of origin and the global subsidy race.

Business groups and unions are urging a clearer framework that links trade policy, energy costs and green investment to sectoral resilience. Without it, they warn, ad hoc interventions risk becoming costlier while leaving the UK exposed to external shocks and intensifying international competition.
