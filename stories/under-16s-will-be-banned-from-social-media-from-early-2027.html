Under-16s will be banned from social media from early 2027

The UK government will block major platforms including Instagram, YouTube and TikTok for under-16s from early 2027, a sweeping policy that raises enforcement, verification and free-expression questions.

The UK government will block major social media platforms including Instagram, YouTube and TikTok for under-16s from early 2027. It is a sweeping intervention, and one that immediately raises questions about enforcement, age verification and free expression.

The policy is being framed as a way of protecting children from harmful content and the pressures of online life. But it will only work if platforms can reliably identify younger users, which is a technically and politically difficult task.

The announcement also places the UK among the most interventionist governments in the world on youth access to social media. Supporters will see that as overdue action; critics will argue that the state is moving too far, too quickly, and without a clear sense of how the ban will work in practice.

What happens next will depend on the details, including which platforms are covered and how the rules are enforced. For millions of families, 2027 could bring a major change in how young people use the internet.
