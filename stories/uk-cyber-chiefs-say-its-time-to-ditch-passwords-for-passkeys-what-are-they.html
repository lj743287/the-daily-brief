UK cyber chiefs say it's time to ditch passwords for passkeys - what are they?

The National Cyber Security Centre is urging users to move from passwords to passkeys, arguing the new login method is stronger and more resistant to phishing. The shift could change how millions of UK accounts are secured.

The National Cyber Security Centre is urging people and organisations to move away from passwords and use passkeys instead.

Passkeys are designed to be harder to phish because they use device-based cryptographic authentication rather than a password that can be stolen, reused or guessed. The advice matters because millions of online accounts still rely on passwords, despite their long history of weakness.

The change could affect banks, email providers, retailers and government services if more platforms adopt the technology. For users, it may mean a simpler login process as well as stronger protection.

Cyber chiefs are trying to shift behaviour before criminals do it for them, as phishing and account takeover remain common threats.

The main uncertainty is how quickly services will support passkeys and whether users will trust and understand the new system.

If adoption grows, passkeys could gradually become the default way to secure accounts in the UK, replacing one of the internet's oldest habits.
