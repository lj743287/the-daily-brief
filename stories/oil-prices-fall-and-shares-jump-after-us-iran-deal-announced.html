Oil prices fall and shares jump after US-Iran deal announced

Brent crude and equity markets moved sharply after the US-Iran deal, with Donald Trump saying the Strait of Hormuz would reopen, a development with direct consequences for energy prices, transport costs and inflation.

Oil prices fell and shares rose after the US-Iran deal was announced, with Donald Trump saying the Strait of Hormuz would reopen. The market reaction shows how closely investors had tied the conflict to the global energy outlook.

Brent crude dropped sharply as traders priced in the possibility that tankers could again move through one of the world’s most important oil routes. That matters because the Strait of Hormuz is a choke point for global supplies, and even the prospect of disruption can lift fuel, transport and food costs worldwide.

Equity markets welcomed the prospect of lower energy prices and less geopolitical risk, but the relief may prove uneven. Prices can rebound quickly if the deal weakens, and the reopening of the strait will not by itself restore the market to the conditions that existed before the war.

For households and businesses, the significance is straightforward: cheaper oil can ease inflation pressure, but only if the agreement holds. The deal has changed expectations, not eliminated uncertainty.
