Civilians feared killed after reports of air strike on Nigerian market

Reports of a strike on a village market have raised fears of civilian deaths, while the Nigerian Air Force has yet to address the allegations directly.

There are growing fears that civilians have been killed after reports of an air strike on a village market in north-east Nigeria. The Nigerian Air Force has not yet directly addressed the allegation, leaving the scale of the incident and the number of casualties uncertain.

The reported target was militants, but markets in conflict zones often sit close to civilian life and can become the scene of devastating mistakes. If the reports are confirmed, the incident would add to longstanding concerns about the risks posed by military operations in areas where armed groups and local communities are closely intermingled.

What happens next will depend on whether the authorities acknowledge a misfire, open an investigation and provide a credible account of what occurred. In a region already scarred by years of insurgency, transparency matters not just for public confidence but for the protection of civilians in future operations.
