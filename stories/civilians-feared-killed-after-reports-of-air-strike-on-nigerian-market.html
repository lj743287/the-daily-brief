Civilians feared killed after reports of air strike on Nigerian market

Reports from Nigeria suggest civilians may have been killed after an air strike on a village market, with the Nigerian Air Force not yet addressing claims that it struck the market while targeting militants.

Civilians are feared to have been killed after reports of an air strike on a village market in Nigeria. The Nigerian Air Force has not yet addressed claims that it hit the market while targeting militants.

If confirmed, the strike would add to long-running concerns over civilian casualties in Nigeria's conflict zones, where the military has repeatedly faced questions about the accuracy of its operations. Markets are especially dangerous settings for such attacks because fighters and civilians can be difficult to distinguish in fast-moving raids.

The lack of an immediate response from the air force leaves the facts uncertain, but local reports suggest the death toll could be high. The episode is likely to intensify scrutiny of military tactics and of the government's willingness to investigate possible mistakes.
