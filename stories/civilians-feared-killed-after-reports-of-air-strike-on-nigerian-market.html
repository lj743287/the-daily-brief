Civilians feared killed after reports of air strike on Nigerian market

The Nigerian Air Force has not addressed reports that a strike on a village market in north-east Nigeria killed civilians while targeting militants, raising fresh concerns about the conduct of the campaign.

Reports that the Nigerian Air Force struck a village market in north-east Nigeria while targeting militants have raised fears that civilians were killed in the attack. The air force has not publicly addressed the allegations, leaving questions over what was hit and how the operation was carried out.

The strike matters because it goes to the heart of a long-running and deeply damaging security campaign in the region. Nigeria’s military has repeatedly faced criticism over civilian casualties in operations against insurgents, and each such incident risks eroding trust among people already living amid violence and displacement.

If the reports are confirmed, the political cost could be substantial. For communities in the north-east, the issue is not only the presence of militants but the danger of being caught between insurgent violence and state force. That makes transparency after strikes as important as the military objective itself.

The key uncertainty is whether this was a misfire, a mistaken target or something worse. Until the authorities explain what happened, the incident will fuel concern that the war against militancy is still being fought with too little regard for civilian safety.
