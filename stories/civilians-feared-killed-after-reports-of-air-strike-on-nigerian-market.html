Civilians feared killed after reports of air strike on Nigerian market

Reports of a strike on a village market in Nigeria raise urgent questions about civilian harm, targeting and accountability as the military has not addressed the allegations.

Reports from north-east Nigeria say an air strike hit a village market while the military was targeting suspected militants nearby. If confirmed, it would add to long-running concerns about the protection of civilians in Nigeria’s conflict zone.

The Nigerian Air Force has not publicly addressed the allegations. That silence has left open basic questions about what was struck, whether there was a targeting error and how many people may have been killed or injured.

The area has for years seen operations against Islamist insurgents, especially in Borno and neighbouring states, where civilian populations often live close to front lines and military targets. In such settings, distinguishing militants from civilians can be difficult — but that does not remove the obligation to account for harm.

What matters now is whether the authorities investigate openly and explain what happened. Without that, claims of collateral damage risk deepening distrust among communities that already fear both insurgents and the security forces sent to fight them.
