UK borrowing lowest for three years but Iran war clouds outlook

The UK has borrowed less than expected for three years, but analysts say that improvement may not last because of the Iran war’s effect on energy prices and the wider economy. The fiscal outlook now depends heavily on how long the shock lasts and how far it spreads.

UK government borrowing has come in at its lowest level for three years, but analysts say that improvement may not last because of the Iran war and its effect on energy prices.

The better-than-expected figures offer a rare piece of good news for the public finances, but they may prove temporary if the conflict keeps pushing up import costs and weakening growth.

That matters because the government had been hoping for some breathing space after years of pressure on the budget. A sustained rise in energy prices could quickly erase it.

The fiscal outlook now depends heavily on how long the shock lasts and how far it spreads through transport, industry and consumer spending.

There is also a wider political issue: any deterioration in the economy may feed into debates over tax, spending and how much room ministers really have to ease pressure on households.

For now, the borrowing figures show the underlying finances were improving. The question is whether the Iran war becomes the force that interrupts that trend.
