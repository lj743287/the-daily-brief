UK borrowing lowest for three years but Iran war clouds outlook

Public borrowing has fallen to its lowest level for three years, but analysts say the gain may not last once the economic fallout from the Iran war feeds through.

UK public borrowing has fallen to its lowest level for three years, offering a welcome improvement in the public finances at a difficult moment for the economy.

The near-term gain may prove temporary, however, because analysts say the impact of the war involving Iran has yet to fully hit Britain. Higher fuel costs, weaker confidence and supply disruptions could all weigh on tax receipts and spending.

That makes the borrowing figures a reminder that good news on the fiscal front can be quickly overtaken by events abroad. The government may gain some breathing space now, but the outlook remains fragile.

The question is whether the Treasury can treat this as a durable improvement or merely a pause before the conflict’s economic effects are felt more fully. Much depends on how long energy prices remain elevated.
