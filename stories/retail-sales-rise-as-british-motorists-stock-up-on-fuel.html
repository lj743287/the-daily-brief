Retail sales rise as British motorists stock up on fuel

Retail sales in Great Britain rose 0.7% in March as motorists bought more fuel, with the ONS saying fuel purchases reached their highest level since 2021.

Retail sales in Great Britain rose 0.7% in March, with the Office for National Statistics saying fuel purchases reached their highest level since 2021 as motorists rushed to fill up.

The increase was well above the 0.1% rise expected by analysts, and it appears to have been driven in part by the spike in petrol and diesel prices linked to the Iran war. Sunny weather also helped lift spending in some areas.

The figures suggest a mixed picture for the high street: consumers were spending more, but not necessarily because confidence was improving. In this case, higher fuel demand was a reaction to fear of further price rises rather than a sign of stronger underlying demand.

What happens next will depend on whether fuel prices stay high and whether that starts to squeeze spending elsewhere. For retailers, the question is whether March was a one-off or the start of a more costly year.
