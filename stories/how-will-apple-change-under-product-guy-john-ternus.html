How will Apple change under 'product guy' John Ternus?

John Ternus’s rise to Apple’s top job raises questions about whether the company can broaden beyond the iPhone, sharpen its AI response and maintain the scale that Tim Cook delivered.

John Ternus’s rise to Apple’s top job raises a simple question: can a long-serving product executive steer the company through its next phase of growth?

Apple’s challenge is not just replacing Tim Cook, but deciding whether to push harder into AI, reduce dependence on the iPhone and open new areas of hardware and services.

That matters because the company’s scale can make change difficult even when the need is obvious; the market wants innovation, but it also values the stability and execution that Cook delivered.

Ternus’s background suggests continuity rather than rupture, yet his success will depend on whether he can turn Apple’s resources into a clearer product vision.
