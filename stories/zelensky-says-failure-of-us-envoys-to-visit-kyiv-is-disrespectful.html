Zelensky says failure of US envoys to visit Kyiv is 'disrespectful'

Volodymyr Zelensky has criticised the fact that US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner have travelled to Moscow several times but never to Kyiv, a contrast that underscores the diplomatic imbalance around the war settlement talks.

Volodymyr Zelensky has criticised the fact that US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner have travelled to Moscow several times but never to Kyiv, sharpening a diplomatic row over the shape of war settlement talks.

The Ukrainian president’s complaint highlights a familiar concern in Kyiv: that negotiations over Ukraine’s future can be shaped by outsiders without enough direct engagement with the country most affected.

The contrast matters because visits are often read as a sign of where priorities lie, and repeated trips to Moscow without a reciprocal stop in Kyiv can fuel suspicions about imbalance in the talks.

The broader backdrop is a war in which diplomatic channels remain active but fragile, with no clear sign that either Moscow or Kyiv is ready to accept terms the other would consider acceptable.

For now, the dispute is as much about symbolism and respect as it is about substance, but such signals can still influence the atmosphere around any eventual settlement.
