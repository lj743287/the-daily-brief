Tice £91,000 tax row is 'minor administrative error', party claims

Reform UK says a £91,000 tax row involving Richard Tice’s property company was a minor administrative error, while the allegations continue to raise questions about governance and offshore structures.

Reform UK says a £91,000 tax row involving Richard Tice's property company was a minor administrative error. The claim comes after reports that the company failed to pay tax on dividends, prompting questions about governance and offshore structures.

The issue matters because Tice is the party's deputy leader, and tax discipline is a live political issue for any senior figure who attacks others over waste or accountability. Even if the sums are relatively modest in political terms, the episode creates an awkward contrast with the party's public message.

What remains uncertain is how the matter was handled internally and whether any further detail will emerge about the company's accounts. For now, the dispute is likely to continue as a reputational problem rather than a legal one.
