Tice £91,000 tax row is 'minor administrative error', party claims

Reform UK says a property company linked to Richard Tice’s finances failed to pay £91,000 in tax on dividends, after reporting by the Sunday Times.

Reform UK has described a £91,000 tax issue linked to Richard Tice’s property company as a minor administrative error, after reporting in the Sunday Times. The row is awkward for the party’s deputy leader, who has built part of his political appeal around discipline in public life and criticism of the tax system.

The case matters less for the sum itself than for the contrast between rhetoric and conduct. In politics, tax disputes are rarely just about compliance; they also become tests of credibility, particularly for parties that present themselves as anti-establishment or financially prudent.

Reform will want to contain the issue quickly and frame it as an administrative matter rather than a political one. But any suggestion that a senior figure’s business affairs were mishandled will be seized on by opponents as evidence that the party is not immune from the same standards it demands of others.

What happens next will depend on whether the explanation satisfies scrutiny and whether any further details emerge. Until then, the episode is likely to remain a distraction rather than a decisive blow, but one that does little to help the party’s image.
