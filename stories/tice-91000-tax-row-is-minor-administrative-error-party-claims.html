Tice £91,000 tax row is 'minor administrative error', party claims

Reform UK is seeking to contain the political damage from allegations around a property company linked to its deputy leader and unpaid dividend tax.

Reform UK is trying to contain the fallout after reports that a property company linked to its deputy leader failed to pay £91,000 in dividend tax. The party says the issue amounts to a minor administrative error, but the row is politically awkward for a party that has made tax and waste central to its message.

The case matters because it touches on a familiar vulnerability for politicians who campaign on accountability. Even when the sums are limited, tax disputes tend to raise questions about judgment, compliance and whether leaders apply the rules they demand of others.

Reform will want to avoid letting the issue widen into a broader story about standards or financial opacity. Whether that happens will depend on what further details emerge and how convincingly the party can explain the lapse.
