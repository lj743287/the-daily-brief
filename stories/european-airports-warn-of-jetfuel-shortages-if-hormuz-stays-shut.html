European airports warn of jet‑fuel shortages if Hormuz stays shut

ACI Europe says members are increasingly concerned about fuel availability amid disrupted Gulf flows. Airlines face potential rerouting, hedging strains and higher fares if shortages persist.

European airports are warning of potential jet‑fuel shortages if disruptions at the Strait of Hormuz persist, with ACI Europe flagging growing concern among members, the BBC reported.

The region relies on significant kerosene imports from the Middle East, and shipping delays, higher insurance costs and longer voyages are beginning to bite into inventories at some hubs.

Airlines are preparing to ration uplift at selected airports, adjust routings and increase fuel tankering on certain legs, steps that could translate into schedule changes and higher fares if sustained.

Hedging programmes are being tested by volatile crack spreads and time differentials, exposing carriers with thinner balance sheets to margin calls and tighter liquidity in peak travel periods.

National authorities and regulators are in close contact with industry to monitor stocks and coordinate contingency plans, though no broad‑based rationing has been announced.

Knock‑on effects include pressure on air cargo yields, competitive challenges for long‑haul operations and environmental trade‑offs from potential detours and tankering strategies.
