Mauritius vows renewed push to ‘decolonise’ Chagos after UK shelves handover bill

Port Louis says it will ‘spare no effort’ to regain control of the Chagos Islands after the UK paused legislation amid waning US support. The dispute, long framed by decolonisation claims and strategic basing needs, now risks a prolonged diplomatic standoff.

Mauritius vowed to spare no effort to regain control of the Chagos Islands after the UK paused legislation to facilitate a handover amid waning US support. The move risks stalling talks and deepening a dispute long framed by decolonisation claims and strategic basing needs.

The International Court of Justice in 2019 advised that the UK should end its administration of the archipelago, and the UN General Assembly urged London to facilitate decolonisation. The UK has maintained the British Indian Ocean Territory status while the largest island, Diego Garcia, hosts a key US military base.

Officials in London acknowledged they had run out of time to pass relevant measures in the current parliamentary session, with reported reservations in Washington over basing arrangements. Negotiations between London and Port Louis had sought to reconcile sovereignty with continued defence cooperation.

Mauritius presents the issue as a matter of decolonisation and the rights of displaced Chagossians, while UK officials have highlighted alliance obligations and regional security. Any agreement would need to address governance, environmental protection and the terms of continued military access.

The outcome carries implications for Indian Ocean geopolitics, where US, Indian and Chinese interests intersect. India has historically supported Mauritius’s claim, and regional partners are watching for signals on how sovereignty and security can be balanced in practice.

With the bill shelved, diplomatic momentum risks dissipating and legal pressure alone may not deliver change. Progress will likely depend on a trilateral understanding that secures basing needs, enables resettlement initiatives and provides a clear timetable for transfer.
