‘Irresponsible failure’: Google, Meta, Snap and Microsoft slam EU over child sexual abuse law lapse

The lapse in legal cover for automated abuse detection creates a serious enforcement gap and sharpens the familiar clash between privacy and child protection. The consequences could be immediate and measurable.

Google, Meta, Snap and Microsoft have attacked the EU after a legal lapse created a gap in the framework used to detect child sexual abuse material. The companies say the failure is irresponsible, and the issue has immediate consequences for online safety enforcement.

The dispute sits at the familiar fault line between privacy and child protection. Automated scanning tools can help identify abuse imagery, but they also raise concerns about surveillance and overreach, leaving lawmakers to balance competing risks.

What happens next is urgent because the gap could affect how platforms respond in the short term. If Brussels does not restore legal clarity quickly, the result may be weaker enforcement at exactly the point when authorities want stronger safeguards.
