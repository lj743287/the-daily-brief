‘Irresponsible failure’: Google, Meta, Snap and Microsoft slam EU over child sexual abuse law lapse

Google, Meta, Snap and Microsoft are warning that a legal gap in the EU’s child sexual abuse scanning regime could reduce reporting of abuse and grooming online.

Google, Meta, Snap and Microsoft have warned that a legal gap in the European Union's child sexual abuse scanning regime could reduce the reporting of abuse and grooming online. The companies say the lapse is an irresponsible failure, while privacy advocates remain wary of blanket scanning powers.

The legal change matters because the expired carve-out had allowed automated tools to identify suspected child sexual abuse material, grooming and sextortion on platforms. Without it, experts fear a repeat of the sharp fall in detections seen during a similar gap in 2021.

The row exposes a difficult trade-off between child protection and privacy. The EU will now have to decide whether to restore the exemption, rewrite the law or accept the risk that more abuse will go undetected.
