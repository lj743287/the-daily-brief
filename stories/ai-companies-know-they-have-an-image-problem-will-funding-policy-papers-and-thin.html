AI companies know they have an image problem. Will funding policy papers and thinktanks dig them out?

OpenAI and other AI firms are trying to reshape the political and public narrative around the technology as concerns grow over jobs, cyber risk and the industry’s social costs.

OpenAI and other AI firms are trying to change the political and public conversation around artificial intelligence. Their push includes policy papers, thinktank support and closer engagement with policymakers, all aimed at softening the industry's reputation problem.

The timing is significant because public concern about AI is rising alongside fears about jobs, cyber risk and social costs. OpenAI's recent 13-page paper and its wider outreach suggest that the major firms now believe influence work is as important as product launches.

The strategy may help shape regulation and reassure investors, but it cannot by itself resolve the underlying fears. The more powerful the technology becomes, the harder it will be for companies to persuade people that its benefits outweigh its risks.
