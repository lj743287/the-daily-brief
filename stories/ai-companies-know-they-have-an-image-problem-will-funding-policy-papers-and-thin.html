AI companies know they have an image problem. Will funding policy papers and thinktanks dig them out?

Major AI firms are increasingly using policy, PR and institutional outreach to shape public opinion as regulation and scepticism intensify.

Major AI firms are increasingly funding policy papers, academic-style projects and thinktank outreach as they try to improve their public standing in the face of rising scepticism and tighter regulation.

The strategy is not new, but it has become more important as AI moves from novelty to infrastructure. Companies are no longer just selling products; they are trying to shape the rules, the language and the assumptions that govern those products.

That raises obvious questions about independence. Supporters say such funding helps inform debate; critics say it risks laundering corporate priorities through respected institutions.

The bigger issue is whether reputation management can change the underlying concerns around safety, labour, copyright and power. If those questions remain unanswered, better messaging may only go so far.
