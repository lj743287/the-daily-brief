AI companies know they have an image problem. Will funding policy papers and thinktanks dig them out?

The industry is trying to shift public debate through policy messaging, but public scepticism, regulatory scrutiny and product risks are all rising.

AI companies are trying to improve their standing by funding policy papers, research groups and thinktanks. The aim is to shape the debate around safety, productivity and regulation before governments set the terms for them.

But there is a limit to how far policy messaging can repair public scepticism. The sector is still facing criticism over job losses, misinformation, copyright disputes and the concentration of power in a small number of firms.

That leaves companies with a difficult choice: engage more openly with regulators and critics, or risk looking as though they are trying to buy influence. Funding research may help with access and credibility, but it will not by itself solve the industry’s trust deficit.
