Where does McIlroy rank among greats after Masters defence?

After winning back-to-back Masters titles, Rory McIlroy has strengthened his place among golf’s all-time greats and is now chasing the record books further.

Rory McIlroy’s back-to-back Masters titles have strengthened his standing among golf’s all-time greats. Retaining the green jacket is rare enough; doing it after completing the career Grand Slam pushes him further into a very small historical club.

The significance lies not only in the win itself but in what it says about longevity at the top of the sport. Major titles are usually the best measure of greatness in golf, and McIlroy’s sixth has moved him further up an already crowded list of legends.

What he still lacks, compared with the absolute summit of the game, is a body of major wins on the level of Nicklaus or Woods. But the balance of his career now looks very different from a few years ago, when Augusta was more often a source of frustration than triumph.

The next chapter is straightforward: he is chasing more majors. The Masters defence has not answered every ranking question, but it has made the argument for McIlroy’s greatness much stronger.
