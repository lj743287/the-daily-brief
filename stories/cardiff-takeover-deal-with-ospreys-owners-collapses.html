Cardiff takeover deal with Ospreys owners collapses

The Welsh Rugby Union says negotiations for the Ospreys owners to buy Cardiff have fallen through, leaving Cardiff’s future ownership unresolved.

The Welsh Rugby Union says negotiations for the Ospreys owners to buy Cardiff have collapsed, leaving the club’s future ownership unresolved.

The breakdown matters because Cardiff’s long-term stability has been a recurring issue, and any change in ownership is likely to affect recruitment, finances and planning on and off the field.

For supporters, the immediate concern is uncertainty: without a deal, there is no clear path to settling the club’s structure or reassuring fans about what comes next.

The WRU has not said what alternative options are under discussion, so the position remains fluid.

This leaves Cardiff in a familiar limbo, with the club’s future once again dependent on negotiations away from the pitch.
