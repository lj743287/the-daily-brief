Nigerian airstrike reportedly hits market, killing more than 100 civilians

Amnesty International and local media say an air force strike aimed at jihadists struck a crowded market in Yobe state, leaving over 100 dead. Officials confirmed a misfire but have yet to provide details, as survivors’ accounts point to severe civilian casualties and urgent calls for accountability.

Amnesty International and local media said a Nigerian Air Force strike aimed at jihadist fighters hit a crowded market in Yobe state, leaving more than 100 people dead and many others injured. Officials acknowledged a misfire but have not provided details of the operation or the target.

Survivors described multiple explosions during a busy trading period, with emergency workers and residents rushing victims to nearby clinics and hospitals. The death toll could not be independently verified, and authorities have not released an official figure.

The incident occurred in a region where security forces have long battled Boko Haram and Islamic State West Africa Province, complicating efforts to distinguish militants from civilians. Previous operations in the north-east have prompted concerns from rights groups over civilian harm.

Amnesty called for an independent investigation and accountability for any violations of international humanitarian law. The government has yet to set out the scope of any inquiry or explain how targeting procedures were applied in this case.

The strike is likely to heighten tensions in communities already affected by displacement, disrupted markets and limited access to medical care. Humanitarian agencies warned that health facilities in the area lack the resources to cope with mass-casualty events.

International partners will be watching Nigeria’s response, including any steps to review rules of engagement, improve intelligence vetting and compensate victims. The episode may also influence broader debates over the use of air power in crowded civilian settings.
