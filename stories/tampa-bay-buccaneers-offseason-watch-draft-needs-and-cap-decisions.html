Tampa Bay Buccaneers off‑season watch: draft needs and cap decisions

Attention turns to line play, secondary depth and explosive skill options as Tampa Bay calibrates its draft board. Balancing veteran restructures with value signings will define the Bucs’ ability to contend in the NFC South.

As Tampa Bay calibrate their draft board, the off‑season brief is clear: reinforce the lines, deepen the secondary and inject explosive skill while managing the cap with restraint. In a volatile NFC South, marginal gains in the trenches could decide the division.

Offensively, interior line help is a priority to stabilise protection and revive run efficiency. A centre or guard with anchor and lateral quickness would fit a scheme mixing zone and gap concepts, while a reliable swing tackle would guard against injuries and keep personnel groupings intact.

On defence, a fresh wave of pass‑rush is needed to complement interior power. Day‑two edge prospects who can win with speed‑to‑power and hold the edge on early downs offer value, as does an interior penetrator to pair with heavier run‑stoppers in obvious passing situations.

Secondary depth is thin, particularly at nickel and safety. Modern two‑high looks demand rangy, assignment‑sound safeties and a slot corner comfortable in match coverages and blitz packages. Special‑teams utility from late‑round DBs would help round out the game‑day roster.

Explosiveness is still at a premium on offence. A vertical WR3 who can also return kicks, a seam‑threat tight end, and a running back with third‑down reliability and short‑yardage punch would diversify the attack and raise yards‑after‑catch outcomes.

Cap strategy points to selective restructures and incentive‑laden deals for mid‑tier free agents, preserving flexibility and compensatory‑pick potential. Avoiding overcommitting to one‑year spikes and timing value signings after the first free‑agency wave should keep Tampa Bay competitive without compromising future windows.
