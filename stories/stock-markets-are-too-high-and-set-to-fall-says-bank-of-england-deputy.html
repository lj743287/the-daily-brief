Stock markets are too high and set to fall, says Bank of England deputy

A senior Bank of England deputy has warned that stock markets may be overvalued and vulnerable to a fall, an unusually blunt intervention from the central bank.

A senior deputy at the Bank of England has warned that stock markets appear too high and could be heading for a fall, an unusually blunt intervention from a central banker.

The warning stands out because the Bank generally avoids speaking so directly about market levels. The comments suggest concern that share prices may be running ahead of economic fundamentals, leaving investors exposed if sentiment turns.

That matters because central bank language can influence borrowing costs, pension values and wider confidence, especially at a time when global markets are already sensitive to geopolitical shocks and interest-rate uncertainty.

Whether the warning proves prescient will depend on how markets react to inflation, growth and the fallout from the Iran war. For now, the message from Threadneedle Street is that complacency may be misplaced.
