Officials hugely underestimated impact of AI datacentres on UK carbon emissions

UK officials have sharply revised their estimate of the climate impact of AI datacentres, saying emissions could reach up to 123 million tonnes of carbon dioxide over 10 years.

That is a major increase on earlier assumptions and suggests the government had dramatically understated the power and carbon consequences of AI growth.

The issue matters because datacentres are becoming central to AI expansion, but they also consume huge amounts of electricity and water. If demand keeps rising, the environmental cost could be substantial.

For climate policy, the revision raises awkward questions about whether net zero planning has kept pace with the speed of digital infrastructure growth.

It also creates pressure on ministers to explain how energy security, industrial strategy and emissions targets can be reconciled as more AI systems come online.

The next debate is likely to focus on regulation, grid capacity and whether the UK can expand AI infrastructure without locking in much higher carbon output.
