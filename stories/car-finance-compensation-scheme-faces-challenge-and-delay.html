Car finance compensation scheme faces challenge and delay

The car finance compensation scheme faces fresh legal challenge and delay, raising the prospect that payouts to millions of mis-sold drivers will take longer than expected.

The car finance compensation scheme faces a fresh legal challenge, raising the prospect that payouts to millions of mis-sold drivers will be delayed again.

The dispute matters because it affects one of the largest consumer redress exercises in recent UK financial history, with borrowers already waiting years to learn whether they will receive compensation.

Any further delay will frustrate motorists who believe they were charged unfairly, and it could also complicate the Financial Conduct Authority’s attempts to bring clarity to the market.

The uncertainty reflects how difficult it can be to unwind past mis-selling on a system-wide scale, especially when lenders, lawyers and regulators disagree over the rules for repayment.

For consumers, the next step is likely to be more waiting, even as the legal and regulatory arguments continue.
