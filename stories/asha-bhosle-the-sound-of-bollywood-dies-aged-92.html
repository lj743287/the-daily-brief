Asha Bhosle: The sound of Bollywood dies aged 92

Bhosle’s career spanned decades in which Hindi cinema evolved from studio‑system musicals to global box‑office juggernauts. Her versatility—moving effortlessly between classical, cabaret, folk and pop—made her an enduring presence from the silver‑screen classics to contemporary hits.

She recorded thousands of songs and became synonymous with characters brought to life by actors on screen, fusing vocal virtuosity with storytelling. Composers prized her range and timing, while audiences embraced a voice that could turn from playful to profound in a bar of music.

Tributes from artists, filmmakers and political leaders celebrated a cultural lodestar whose work travelled far beyond India’s borders. For many in the diaspora, her recordings were a soundtrack of memory, binding language and distance through melody.

Her legacy is likely to deepen as remasters, archives and streaming bring new listeners to a remarkable catalogue. In an industry built on collaboration, Bhosle’s singular talent stood out—a reminder that the voice behind the screen can define how the world hears a film.
