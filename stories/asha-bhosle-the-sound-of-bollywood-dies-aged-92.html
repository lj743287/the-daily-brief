Asha Bhosle: The sound of Bollywood dies aged 92

The death of the celebrated Indian singer closes a major chapter in South Asian popular culture, with her voice spanning generations.

Asha Bhosle has died aged 92, closing one of the most influential careers in Indian popular music. Her voice became inseparable from Bollywood's golden age and beyond, carrying songs that were heard across generations and far beyond India.

Bhosle was not simply a singer but part of the cultural machinery that shaped modern South Asian cinema. Her work helped define the emotional range of film music, from romantic ballads to lively dance numbers, and made her a familiar presence in homes, cinemas and public celebrations for decades.

Her death is likely to prompt an outpouring of tributes from across India and the wider diaspora, reflecting the rare cultural reach she achieved. It also marks the passing of an era in which playback singers could become national icons in their own right, with voices that became part of collective memory.
