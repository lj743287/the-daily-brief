Data watch: Vessel traffic through Hormuz remains thin

BBC Verify tracking shows only limited crossings since the ceasefire, indicating persistent caution among shippers and insurers. Sparse flows keep risk premia elevated across crude, products and freight.

BBC Verify tracking shows only limited ship crossings through the Strait of Hormuz since the ceasefire announcement, pointing to persistent caution among owners and insurers.

Automatic Identification System readings suggest reduced movements of laden crude and product tankers, with underwriting still tight and premiums elevated for voyages in the area.

Sparse flows are keeping crude and products time spreads volatile as refiners bid for prompt barrels and traders reassess storage economics in key hubs.

Freight markets reflect extended tonne‑miles from rerouting, sustaining higher rates and lengthening voyage times even for cargoes not directly sourced from the Gulf.

LNG liftings remain patchy, leaving Europe and Asia more exposed to weather‑driven demand swings and to pipeline or nuclear outages that can amplify price moves.

Data gaps remain, as transponders can be disabled and reporting can lag, but the overall picture supports a higher risk premium across energy and shipping benchmarks.
