White House memo claims mass AI theft by Chinese firms

A White House memo from Michael Kratsios alleges that firms, mainly in China, are wrongfully distilling US AI models, sharpening the dispute over industrial policy and technology theft.

A White House memo from Michael Kratsios alleges that firms, mainly in China, are wrongfully distilling US artificial intelligence models, sharpening an already tense dispute over technology and industrial policy.

The claim adds to Washington’s broader effort to frame AI as a strategic asset that needs protection from copying and misuse. It also reflects growing concern that model outputs, training methods and other intellectual property can be replicated faster than governments can regulate them.

The issue matters because AI development is becoming central to economic competition between the US and China. If American models are being systematically copied, the argument for tougher export controls, enforcement measures and corporate safeguards will strengthen.

The memo makes an allegation rather than a tested legal finding, so the details will matter. Whether Washington can prove the scale of the problem may determine how hard it moves against Chinese companies.
