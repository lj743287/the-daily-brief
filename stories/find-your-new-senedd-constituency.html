Wales Explainer

Find your new Senedd constituency

Boundary changes mean many voters will be in newly drawn constituencies. Use the guide to understand how representation and regional balance are shifting.

Next year’s Senedd election will be fought on a new map. The 40 former constituencies and five electoral regions are being replaced by 16 larger, six‑member constituencies designed to create more equal electorates and align with the recent overhaul of Westminster boundaries.

Most of the new Senedd constituencies are formed by pairing the 32 UK Parliament seats now in place for Wales. The boundary authority in Wales has aimed to keep communities together while meeting legal tests on electorate size, geography and accessibility. Final names and precise borders have been confirmed to allow parties and administrators to prepare.

If you want to understand how your area has changed, the simplest route is to compare your current Westminster constituency with the one next to it that shares the strongest local ties. In many cases, urban hubs are matched with their immediate neighbours, and rural areas are grouped along established travel and service patterns, though some cross‑county lines are inevitable.

The practical effect is that you will be represented by six MSs elected from a single constituency rather than by one constituency MS plus additional regional MSs. That should make the overall result more proportional locally, but it also means more than one representative may share responsibility for the same town or neighbourhood.

Until the election, your existing MSs continue to serve the areas they were elected to represent. Parties are already adapting their casework systems and local surgeries to the larger constituencies, and voters can continue to seek help from any of their current representatives in the meantime.

Your poll card will set out your new constituency name before voting day, and official maps and notices will be available from local councils and the boundary body. The Welsh franchise allows residents aged 16 and over, and qualifying foreign nationals living in Wales, to vote in Senedd elections, so new and first‑time voters should check registration deadlines early.
