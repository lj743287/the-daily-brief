Why are respected film-makers suddenly embracing AI?

Steven Soderbergh and other prominent film-makers are experimenting with AI in production, signalling a broader shift in the film industry even as concerns about authorship and labour persist.

A growing number of respected film-makers, including Steven Soderbergh, are experimenting with AI in production, signalling a shift in how parts of the industry think about the technology.

The trend matters because film-makers have often been among the strongest critics of generative AI, especially where it touches authorship, labour and the work of actors and writers.

Yet some directors now appear willing to use AI as another creative tool, whether for imagery, visual effects or idea generation, suggesting the debate is moving from principle to practice.

That does not settle the ethical concerns: questions remain over training data, ownership and whether AI will weaken the bargaining position of creative workers.

What happens next will depend on whether these experiments stay limited or become a normal part of the production process.
