McIlroy holds nerve to win second successive Masters

Rory McIlroy has won a second successive Masters at Augusta, beating Scottie Scheffler by one shot and underlining his status as one of golf’s modern greats.

Rory McIlroy has won a second successive Masters at Augusta, beating Scottie Scheffler by one shot after a tense final round.

The victory underlines McIlroy’s status as one of golf’s modern greats and adds another major title to a career that has already placed him among the game’s elite. Winning back-to-back Masters is rare, and it strengthens the sense that he is still adding to his legacy rather than merely preserving it.

The final round brought enough pressure to test him, but he held his nerve when it mattered most. For Scheffler, the defeat will sting, but the quality of the contest underlined how high the bar now is at the top of the men’s game.

The result will fuel fresh debate about where McIlroy stands historically and whether more majors are now in reach.

For Augusta, it was another reminder that the biggest prizes can still hinge on a single shot.
