Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

Higher petrol prices linked to the Iran war have pushed US inflation to 3.3%, the highest rate in almost two years and a warning sign for households and policy-makers.

Higher petrol prices linked to the Iran war have pushed US inflation to 3.3%, the highest rate in almost two years. The rise is a warning sign for households already facing higher transport costs and for policymakers trying to judge how far the conflict is feeding into the wider economy.

Fuel prices tend to spread quickly through the price of goods and services, so pump costs can amplify inflation beyond drivers alone. That makes the latest figure important not just as a snapshot of consumer pain but as a signal that the war is affecting everyday spending.

The question now is whether this is a one-off shock or the start of a longer period of price pressure. Much depends on how long energy markets remain unsettled and whether shipping through the Gulf can return to normal.
