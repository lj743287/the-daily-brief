Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

US inflation has jumped to 3.3% as the Iran war feeds through to fuel costs, giving policymakers and consumers a fresh reminder of how quickly geopolitical shocks reach household budgets.

US inflation has risen to 3.3%, with higher pump prices helping push the rate to its strongest level in almost two years. The jump is a reminder of how fast a geopolitical shock can move from the Gulf to American households.

Fuel costs feed directly into transport spending and indirectly into a wide range of goods and services, so the impact is not limited to drivers filling up their cars. Policymakers will now be more alert to the risk that energy-driven price rises become more persistent if the conflict continues.

For the Federal Reserve, the timing is awkward. A fresh inflation pulse reduces room for comfort on interest rates, while consumers face another squeeze on real incomes. The big uncertainty is whether this is a one-off shock or the start of a more durable rise in prices if supply conditions worsen further.
