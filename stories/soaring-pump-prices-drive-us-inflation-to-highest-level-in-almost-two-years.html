Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

Higher fuel costs linked to the Iran war have lifted US inflation, strengthening expectations that the shock is feeding through into the wider economy.

Higher fuel prices linked to the Iran conflict have pushed US inflation to its highest level in almost two years. The rise at the pump is feeding into the wider economy at a moment when households are already sensitive to the cost of living.

The immediate effect is straightforward: motorists pay more, transport costs rise and businesses that depend on fuel face tighter margins. Over time, that can feed through to food, travel and other everyday prices, making the inflation shock harder to dismiss as temporary.

The question for policymakers is whether this is a one-off energy spike or the start of a broader inflationary spell. If the strait remains constrained and oil prices stay elevated, the pressure on the Federal Reserve and the White House is likely to intensify.
