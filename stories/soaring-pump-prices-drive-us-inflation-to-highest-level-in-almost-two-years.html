Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

Higher fuel prices linked to the Iran war have lifted US inflation, reinforcing concerns that the conflict is feeding directly into consumer costs and complicating the outlook for central bankers.

Higher pump prices linked to the Iran war have pushed US inflation to its highest level in almost two years, reinforcing concerns that the conflict is being felt directly in household budgets. Fuel is often among the first areas where geopolitical shocks show up in daily life.

The increase matters because it complicates the outlook for policymakers already trying to judge whether price pressures are temporary or more persistent. Energy costs can filter through to transport, food and other goods, making inflation harder to bring back under control.

For consumers, the effect is immediate and visible at the petrol station. For central bankers, it is another reminder that a conflict far from the US can still reshape the domestic economic debate quickly and unpredictably.
