Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

Higher fuel costs have fed through into the US inflation data, underlining how quickly the Iran conflict is transmitting into consumer prices.

Rising pump prices have pushed US inflation to its highest level in almost two years, offering another sign of how quickly the Iran conflict is filtering into household costs.

The main transmission is fuel. As crude prices rise and refiners pass on higher costs, motorists feel the impact first, but the effect rarely stops there. Transport, food distribution and a range of consumer goods can all become more expensive if energy stays elevated.

That gives the dispute a domestic political dimension in the US as well as a global economic one. If fuel remains dear, it will complicate efforts to bring inflation down and may strengthen pressure on policymakers to avoid a prolonged confrontation in the Gulf.
