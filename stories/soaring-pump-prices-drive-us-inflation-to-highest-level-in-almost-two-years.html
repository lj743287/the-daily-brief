Soaring pump prices drive US inflation to highest level in almost two years

Higher fuel costs linked to the conflict have pushed US inflation upwards, complicating the outlook for households and policymakers alike.

US inflation has risen to its highest level in almost two years as higher petrol prices linked to the conflict with Iran fed through to consumers. The increase adds to pressure on households already facing persistent living-cost strains.

The latest figures matter because fuel prices tend to move quickly into broader inflation readings, affecting transport, shipping and the cost of everyday goods. That in turn complicates the outlook for the Federal Reserve, which must weigh weaker growth risks against the possibility of renewed price pressure.

For Americans, the immediate impact is being felt at the pump. For policymakers, the harder problem is whether the rise proves brief or becomes part of a wider inflationary rebound if energy markets remain unsettled.
