Key figure in Mandelson vetting row will not appear to give evidence to MPs

Ian Collard will not appear before MPs on the Foreign Affairs Committee and will instead provide written evidence in the Peter Mandelson vetting row. The decision prolongs scrutiny of how Mandelson’s clearance was handled.

Ian Collard will not appear in person before MPs on the Foreign Affairs Committee as part of the Peter Mandelson vetting row, and will instead provide written evidence.

The decision prolongs scrutiny over how Mandelson's clearance was handled and whether the process was thorough enough. MPs had wanted to question Collard directly as part of their inquiry.

This matters because vetting is meant to provide confidence that sensitive appointments are handled properly. When a central figure declines to give oral evidence, it can feed suspicions that important questions remain unanswered.

The committee will still be able to examine written material, but that is usually less useful than live questioning when trying to establish accountability and sequence.

The row has become a test not only of Mandelson's handling but of the transparency of the government's appointments system more generally.

Further pressure is likely if MPs decide the written evidence leaves gaps, or if other witnesses differ sharply on what was known and when.
