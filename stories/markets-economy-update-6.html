Markets & Economy update 6

A significant development in markets & economy with wider consequences under review.

The biggest market story in view remains the fallout from the crisis around Iran, with oil prices, shipping costs and airline shares all moving sharply as investors reassess risk.

That matters because energy shocks do not stay confined to commodity markets. They quickly feed into inflation expectations, consumer bills and business costs, which is why governments and central banks are watching developments so closely.

The wider point is that geopolitics is now driving market pricing as much as economic data. Traders are trying to judge whether this is a short, violent correction or the start of a longer period of disruption.

For businesses and households, the next few days will be shaped by the same question: how far can the shock spread before policy makers or diplomacy bring some relief?
