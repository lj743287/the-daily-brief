Markets & Economy update 6

A significant development in markets & economy with wider consequences under review.

Oil prices and shares have moved sharply after the US-Iran deal, with the market betting that the Strait of Hormuz will reopen. That has immediate consequences for fuel costs, shipping rates and inflation expectations.

Yet the economic benefit is not guaranteed. Even if crude stays lower for a while, businesses still face uncertainty over whether the agreement can hold and whether regional tensions will flare again.

The deal has given markets a reason to breathe easier, but not a reason to forget the risks. For consumers, the question is whether relief at the pump and in the shops will last long enough to matter.
