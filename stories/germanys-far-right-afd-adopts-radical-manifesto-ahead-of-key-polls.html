Germany's far-right AfD adopts 'radical' manifesto ahead of key polls

The AfD is sharpening its message before key state elections, a sign of how far-right politics continues to gain traction in parts of Germany.

Germany’s Alternative for Germany, or AfD, has adopted a more radical manifesto as it prepares for key state elections, sharpening a strategy that has helped it gain ground in parts of the country. The party is particularly strong in Saxony-Anhalt, where it is riding high in opinion polls.

The move matters because it shows the AfD continuing to push a hard-line message on migration, identity and national politics despite years of controversy and scrutiny from opponents and much of the mainstream press. Far-right politics has remained resilient in sections of eastern Germany, where resentment over economic and political change has been fertile ground for the party.

The question now is whether the tougher line widens its appeal further or hardens resistance to it. Either way, the manifesto underlines that the AfD is not moderating in the run-up to the polls; it is intensifying the same politics that have made it one of Germany’s most consequential and divisive parties.
