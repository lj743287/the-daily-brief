At least 30 killed in crush at Haitian tourist fortress

Authorities say many young people were among the dead after a crowd crush at the 19th‑century Citadelle Henry in northern Haiti. The tragedy, which left dozens injured or missing, raises urgent questions over event management and emergency access at heritage sites.

Authorities in northern Haiti said at least 30 people were killed in a crowd crush at the Citadelle Henry, a 19th‑century mountaintop fortress popular with tourists. Many of the victims were reported to be young people, with dozens more injured or unaccounted for.

The site’s steep access routes and limited egress points appear to have complicated evacuation efforts. Emergency responders reported difficulties reaching casualties quickly, highlighting the challenges of crowd management at heritage locations with constrained infrastructure.

Officials opened an investigation into planning and safety measures for the event, including capacity controls, stewarding and communications. No formal findings have been released, and questions remain over who authorised the gathering and how risks were assessed.

Hospitals in Cap‑Haïtien and nearby towns treated the injured amid shortages of medical supplies and staff. The country’s fragile health system, already under strain, has made it harder to provide timely care to the most seriously hurt.

The tragedy comes amid broader instability and insecurity in Haiti, where gang violence and weak state capacity often hamper emergency responses. Aid organisations warned that securing access routes and protecting medical teams may be necessary in the days ahead.

Conservation experts and tour operators called for urgent reviews of safety protocols at heritage sites, including controlled entry, clear evacuation plans and better liaison with emergency services. Authorities said further announcements would follow once preliminary inquiries conclude.
