Interest in EVs surges in Europe as fuel prices jump after Iran war

Car buyers in the UK, Germany, France and Spain are showing a sharp rise in interest in electric vehicles as petrol costs jump and the conflict in Iran changes consumer behaviour.

Interest in electric vehicles has risen sharply across the UK, Germany, France and Spain as fuel prices jump after the war in Iran. The change suggests that geopolitical shocks are already influencing consumer behaviour, not just government policy and financial markets.

For car buyers, higher petrol costs make the economics of switching to an EV more attractive. Online marketplaces say enquiries have surged, and some analysts believe the shift in demand could become a higher normal rather than a temporary spike if fuel prices stay elevated.

That matters for Europe’s car market and for the broader transition away from fossil fuels. A crisis driven by insecurity in the Middle East may end up nudging consumers towards cleaner transport, even if only because charging looks cheaper than filling up.

The uncertainty is whether the trend will last. If fuel prices retreat, some of the new interest may fade. But even a short-lived shock can change expectations, and car dealers may now have to reckon with a more price-sensitive customer base than before.
