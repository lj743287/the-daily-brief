Interest in EVs surges in Europe as fuel prices jump after Iran war

Higher petrol costs are pushing more buyers towards electric cars, though demand may settle only after the crisis passes.

Higher petrol prices are pushing more European buyers to look at electric cars as they reassess the cost of running a vehicle. The shift is being driven less by enthusiasm for technology than by a sudden reminder of how vulnerable drivers are to global fuel shocks.

The spike may not translate into a permanent change in demand, but it does expose how quickly consumer behaviour can change when fuel becomes more expensive. For manufacturers and dealers, that means a short-term lift in interest for EVs and hybrids, even if some of the demand later fades when prices settle.

The broader significance is that the energy crisis is affecting decisions well beyond oil markets. It is changing how households think about transport, and how governments and carmakers think about the pace of the transition away from petrol and diesel.
