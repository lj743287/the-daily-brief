Explosions and gunfire as armed groups launch co-ordinated attacks across Mali

Armed groups launched co-ordinated attacks across Mali, including the centre and north, in what witnesses and local reporting describe as the largest jihadist assault in years. The violence highlights the persistent insecurity facing Bamako and other parts of the country.

Armed groups have launched co-ordinated attacks across Mali, with explosions and gunfire reported in the centre and north of the country.

Witnesses and local reporting say the assault is among the largest jihadist attacks in years, highlighting how fragile security remains despite repeated military offensives and years of international concern.

The scale of the violence will increase pressure on the Malian authorities, already struggling to contain insurgent groups across a vast territory and to protect major towns and transport routes.

Mali has faced chronic instability since the 2012 crisis, and the latest attacks suggest militant networks still retain the ability to strike several locations at once.

That matters not just for Bamako, but for neighbouring states across the Sahel, where violence, displacement and weakening state control continue to feed one another.

What happens next will depend on whether the attackers can be pushed back quickly and whether the government can show it still has the reach to respond beyond the capital.
