Dancer with MND performs on stage again through digital avatar

Brainwave and avatar technology have enabled a performer with motor neurone disease to return to the stage, offering a striking example of assistive tech in practice.

A dancer with motor neurone disease has returned to the stage through a digital avatar created using brainwave technology, in a striking example of assistive technology in performance. The project allowed the artist to reclaim a form of expression that illness had taken away.

The significance goes beyond one performance. It shows how emerging technology can help people with severe physical conditions remain creatively active, not just communicate or move through daily life. In that sense, the work offers a glimpse of a future in which digital tools extend participation as well as convenience.

There are still limits, of course, because an avatar cannot fully replace bodily presence. But the project matters because it demonstrates that technology can restore a degree of agency and connection in places where medicine alone cannot.
