Dancer with MND performs on stage again through digital avatar

Brainwave and avatar technology has allowed a dancer with motor neurone disease to return to the stage, showing the growing reach of assistive digital tools.

A dancer living with motor neurone disease has returned to the stage through digital avatar technology, in an example of how rapidly assistive tools are advancing. The project used brainwave-linked systems to recreate movement and expression, allowing performance to continue in a new form.

The significance is wider than a single performance. As health and disability technology improves, it is opening up possibilities that were once unimaginable, from communication aids to more sophisticated forms of creative participation for people with severe physical limitations.

That said, the technology also raises questions about access, cost and how far digital substitutes can or should stand in for the human body on stage. Even so, the performance demonstrates the growing practical reach of tools that can restore agency as well as function.
