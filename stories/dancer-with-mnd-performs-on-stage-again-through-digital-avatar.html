Dancer with MND performs on stage again through digital avatar

Breanna Olson has returned to the stage through a digital avatar, using brainwave technology to restore something of the expression and connection lost to motor neurone disease.

Breanna Olson has returned to the stage through a digital avatar after motor neurone disease took away much of the physical expression her work depended on. The performance is a striking example of how brainwave technology is beginning to offer new forms of artistic participation for people living with severe disability.

The story matters because it is not simply about novelty. For performers whose bodies have changed dramatically, technology can restore something of the connection between intention and audience. In Olson’s case, the avatar allowed her to reclaim part of the presence that illness had interrupted.

That does not mean the technology replaces what was lost. Rather, it creates a different kind of stagecraft, one that can widen access while also raising questions about the future of live performance and the relationship between body, identity and art.

For now, the significance lies in what the project makes possible. It shows how assistive technology can be more than a tool of convenience; it can become a means of artistic continuity and emotional expression.
