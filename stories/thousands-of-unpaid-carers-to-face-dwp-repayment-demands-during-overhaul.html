Thousands of unpaid carers to face DWP repayment demands during overhaul

The government is starting a large audit of historical carer’s allowance cases, but many carers will continue to face repayment demands while the review proceeds.

Thousands of unpaid carers are set to face repayment demands as the Department for Work and Pensions begins a wide-ranging review of historical carer’s allowance cases. The overhaul is intended to address past errors, but for many carers the immediate reality remains more letters, more stress and continuing uncertainty.

Carer’s allowance has long been criticised for its complexity and for the harsh consequences of accidental overpayment. Many recipients are on low incomes and were never trying to defraud the system, which is why repayment demands have caused such anger among campaigners and MPs.

The government’s review could eventually lead to a fairer system, but the process is likely to be slow and contested. The key question is whether ministers will combine the audit with clearer rules and more lenient treatment of those who were caught out by a system they did not understand.
