Van Aert outsprints Pogacar to win first Paris‑Roubaix title

Wout van Aert ended a decade‑long run of misfortune on the cobbles, beating world champion Tadej Pogacar in a select sprint. Rival Mathieu van der Poel’s mechanicals and punctures shaped a brutal edition of the Monument classic.

Wout van Aert finally claimed Paris‑Roubaix, outsprinting world champion Tadej Pogacar on the Roubaix velodrome to end a long, luckless relationship with the cobbled Monument.

A brutal, attritional race was shaped by punctures and mechanicals across the favourites. Pogacar and Van Aert both endured problems yet remained in contention, while Mathieu van der Poel’s issues at key moments blunted his challenge and removed him from the decisive sprint.

On a day when the famed secteurs pavés thinned the field, the leading group formed through patience as much as power. Pogacar’s surges forced selections and drew out errors, but Van Aert rode within himself, marked the danger, and kept enough in reserve for the track.

For the Belgian, this was long overdue redemption on the Hell of the North after years of near‑misses and ill‑timed setbacks. It also adds a second Monument to his palmarès after Milan–Sanremo, underlining his range from high‑speed sprints to the most punishing cobbles.

The result nudges the season’s balance in the ongoing three‑way rivalry with Pogacar and Van der Poel, a thread that has defined the spring. With the remaining classics and Tour de France preparations ahead, momentum and workload management will now be under scrutiny.

Conditions again highlighted Roubaix’s unpredictability: high speeds, dust and the constant threat of roadside hazards made positioning and equipment choices pivotal. Teams will pore over tyre and wheel decisions and the timing of service interventions after another edition decided as much by nerve as by legs.
