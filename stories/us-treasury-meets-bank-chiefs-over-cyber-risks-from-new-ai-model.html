US Treasury meets bank chiefs over cyber risks from new AI model

Washington convened major lenders after Anthropic unveiled a tool it says could raise unprecedented cybersecurity concerns. The move signals regulators’ growing focus on systemic tech risks to finance.

Washington convened major lenders after Anthropic unveiled a tool it says could raise unprecedented cybersecurity concerns, signalling growing regulatory focus on systemic technology risks to finance.

Discussions centred on model‑assisted phishing, malware generation and code exploitation that could compress attack timelines and overwhelm traditional defence playbooks.

Banks outlined steps to harden controls, including stricter access governance for powerful models, upgraded anomaly detection and accelerated patching cycles.

Supervisors reiterated expectations on incident reporting, resilience testing and third‑party oversight, positioning AI risks within existing operational and cyber risk frameworks.

Markets took the development in stride, with limited share‑price reaction, but boards are likely to lift investment in detection, response and staff training as models proliferate.

The policy backdrop is tightening as US agencies map AI guidance and global standard‑setters examine how model risk and synthetic threats intersect with prudential rules.
