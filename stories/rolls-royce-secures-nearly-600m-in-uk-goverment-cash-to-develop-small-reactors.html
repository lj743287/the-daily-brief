Rolls-Royce secures nearly £600m in UK goverment cash to develop small reactors

Rolls-Royce has secured up to £599m from Britain’s national wealth fund to develop small modular reactors at Wylfa on Anglesey, a major industrial and energy policy commitment.

Rolls-Royce has secured up to £599m from Britain's national wealth fund to develop small modular reactors at Wylfa on Anglesey. The deal is a major vote of confidence in the company's nuclear ambitions and in the government's wider industrial strategy.

The money is intended to support the design and development of the UK's first small modular reactor programme, a technology ministers hope can provide low-carbon power more quickly and flexibly than conventional nuclear plants. Wylfa has long been viewed as a strategically important site for future energy investment.

The commitment also reflects how closely energy security, decarbonisation and industrial policy are now linked. If the project succeeds, it could help create a new domestic supply chain and jobs; if it stalls, it will be seen as another expensive wager on a technology that has long promised more than it has delivered.
