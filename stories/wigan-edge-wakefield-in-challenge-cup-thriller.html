Wigan edge Wakefield in Challenge Cup thriller

The Warriors survived a late fightback at Belle Vue to reach the last four. The narrow win keeps their domestic and cup ambitions on course.

Wigan Warriors edged Wakefield Trinity in a Challenge Cup quarter‑final at Belle Vue, surviving a late surge to secure a place in the last four. The visitors’ game management under pressure saw them home.

Wakefield rallied impressively in the closing stages, stretching Wigan’s defence and threatening an upset. The response from the holders was measured, with field position and composure reasserted when it mattered.

Kicking accuracy, set completion and discipline were the separating factors in a contest of fine margins. In a knockout environment, Wigan’s ability to control tempo in the final minutes carried extra weight.

The victory sustains Wigan’s dual ambitions across league and cup as the season accelerates. Their standard remains high, but semi‑finals offer no hiding places for lapses in concentration.

Despite elimination, Wakefield take substantive positives, particularly in resilience and attacking intent. Translating that into consistent league performances will be their next task.

The Challenge Cup’s heritage of jeopardy was on full display, with momentum swinging sharply before class and experience asserted themselves. Wigan’s pedigree ensures expectations remain exacting from here.
