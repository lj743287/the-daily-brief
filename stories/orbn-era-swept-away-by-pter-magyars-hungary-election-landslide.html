Orbán era swept away by Péter Magyar's Hungary election landslide

Viktor Orbán’s 16-year rule in Hungary has been ended by Péter Magyar, the 45-year-old former insider who persuaded a majority of voters to oust him in a result with major implications for Europe and for Vladimir Putin’s remaining allies in the EU.

Péter Magyar has ended Viktor Orbán's 16-year grip on power in Hungary after convincing a majority of voters to back him in a sweeping election result. The 45-year-old former Fidesz insider turned opposition challenger has turned a protest movement into a governing mandate.

The scale of the defeat matters well beyond Budapest. Orbán has been one of the European Union's most persistent nationalist outliers, often clashing with Brussels over democratic standards, migration and sanctions on Russia.

For Moscow, the result is likely to be read as another loss of influence inside the EU. Orbán has been Vladimir Putin's most dependable ally among the bloc's leaders, and Magyar's victory could alter Hungary's posture on Ukraine and other European security questions.

The election also reflects how far Orbán's politics had become a target for voters frustrated by inflation, corruption claims and the concentration of power around his government. Magyar has campaigned as a conservative reformer rather than a traditional left-wing rival, broadening his appeal.

What happens next will depend on whether he can turn an anti-Orbán landslide into a stable administration. He will now face the tests of governing, including relations with parliament, the courts, the economy and Hungary's place in Europe.

The vote marks the clearest political break in Hungary for more than a decade and could reshape the country's role in a region still unsettled by Russia's war in Ukraine and tensions over democratic backsliding in the EU.
