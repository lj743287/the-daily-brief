Starmer says UK will not join Trump's blockade of Iran's ports

Keir Starmer says the UK will not join Donald Trump’s blockade of Iran’s ports, while keeping British minesweepers and anti-drone capabilities in the region.

Keir Starmer has said the UK will not join Donald Trump’s blockade of Iran’s ports, drawing a line between British and American policy as pressure builds around the Strait of Hormuz. The UK will, however, keep its minesweepers and anti-drone capabilities in the region.

The decision matters because Britain remains one of the allies most closely tied to US military strategy, yet it is clearly trying to avoid being pulled into an escalation it does not control. By refusing to join the blockade, Starmer is signalling caution even as London continues to support maritime security.

The UK’s continuing presence in the region also shows the limits of disengagement. Minesweepers and anti-drone assets are not symbolic; they are part of the effort to keep shipping routes open and protect allied interests if the crisis worsens.

The next question is how long Britain can stay in this middle position. If the blockade threat becomes a prolonged confrontation, the pressure on London to choose between allied solidarity and restraint will only grow.
