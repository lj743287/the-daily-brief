Labour risks handing power to Reform without drastic change, says union leader

Andrea Egan has warned that Labour could hand power to Reform unless it makes drastic changes, arguing that voters are demanding to be heard after a local election result and broader frustration with the government.

Andrea Egan has warned that Labour could hand power to Reform unless it makes drastic changes. Her intervention reflects growing frustration in parts of the party’s wider base, especially after recent local election results.

The warning matters because it comes from a trade union leader, not an opponent, and therefore speaks to anxieties inside Labour’s own coalition. Egan’s argument is that voters want to be heard and that Labour has not yet delivered enough to satisfy them.

Reform’s rise has become one of the defining pressures on British politics, particularly where cost of living, immigration and anti-establishment sentiment overlap. Labour’s problem is not simply to avoid losing votes to the right, but to convince sceptical supporters that it can govern in a way that feels responsive.

What happens next will depend on whether Labour can translate concern into visible policy change. If not, warnings like this are likely to become more frequent, and more damaging.
