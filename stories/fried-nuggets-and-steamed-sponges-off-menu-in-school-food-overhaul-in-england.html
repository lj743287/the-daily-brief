Fried nuggets and steamed sponges off menu in school food overhaul in England

The government’s school food overhaul aims to tackle childhood obesity and improve nutrition, but it will also face questions over cost, implementation and how far schools can realistically adapt.

The government is pressing ahead with a school food overhaul in England designed to reduce unhealthy meals and improve nutrition. Fried nuggets and steamed sponges may be the symbolic casualties, but the policy is really about changing habits in school dining rooms.

The aim is to tackle childhood obesity and ensure children are offered healthier food more consistently. That matters because school meals can shape diet, attention and long-term health, especially for children who rely on them every day.

The practical challenge is cost and implementation. Schools will have to adapt kitchens, suppliers and menus, while ministers will need to show that the changes are workable rather than simply well-intentioned.
