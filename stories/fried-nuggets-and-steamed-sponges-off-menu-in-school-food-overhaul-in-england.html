Fried nuggets and steamed sponges off menu in school food overhaul in England

The first major update to England’s school food standards in 13 years will ban items such as fried nuggets and steamed sponges from September, in a push to improve children’s diets.

England is preparing the first major update to school food standards in 13 years, with items such as fried nuggets and steamed sponges set to be removed from menus from September.

The overhaul is part of a broader push to improve children’s diets and help lower obesity rates. Campaigners have welcomed the changes, arguing that school food policy can make a real difference to long-term health.

The timing matters because childhood obesity remains a major public health issue, with pressure growing on ministers to show they can act on prevention rather than simply treatment later on.

For schools, the immediate challenge will be cost, compliance and keeping meals appealing as well as healthier. For parents, the reforms may be seen as overdue or overly prescriptive, depending on their view of state intervention.

The deeper question is whether better standards can translate into better habits outside the school gate.
