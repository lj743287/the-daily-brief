Fried nuggets and steamed sponges off menu in school food overhaul in England

England’s first major update to school food standards in 13 years will ban items such as fried nuggets and steamed sponges from September.

England is set for its first major update to school food standards in 13 years, with items such as fried nuggets and steamed sponges due to be removed from menus from September. The changes are part of a wider effort to reduce childhood obesity and improve the nutritional quality of school meals.

The scale of the problem helps explain the government’s intervention: NHS data for 2024 showed that 24% of nursery and primary school children were overweight or living with obesity. Ministers are betting that school menus can help shift those numbers over time.

The overhaul will be welcomed by campaigners who have argued that the standards had become outdated. But it will also put pressure on schools and caterers to deliver meals that are both healthier and still affordable and appealing to children.

The policy is a reminder that public health measures often work slowly and unevenly. The real test will be whether the new rules change what children actually eat, not just what appears on paper.
