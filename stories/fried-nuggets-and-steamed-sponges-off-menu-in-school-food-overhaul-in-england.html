Fried nuggets and steamed sponges off menu in school food overhaul in England

England’s first major update to school food standards in 13 years will ban some calorie-heavy items, including fried nuggets and steamed sponges, as ministers try to curb child obesity.

England is preparing its first major update to school food standards in 13 years, with fried nuggets and steamed sponges among the calorie-heavy items set to be restricted. The overhaul is part of a wider effort to tackle child obesity.

The change matters because school meals are one of the few levers government can use to influence children's diets at scale. Ministers want menus to contain more vegetables and whole grains, and to reduce the kinds of foods that make it easier for schools to serve excess calories.

The new rules are due to apply from September, and campaigners have welcomed the update after a long wait. But the policy will only make a difference if schools can afford to implement it and if healthier food remains attractive enough for children to eat.
