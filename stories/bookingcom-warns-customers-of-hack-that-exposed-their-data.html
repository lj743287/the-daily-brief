Booking.com warns customers of hack that exposed their data

Booking.com says a cyber breach exposed some customers’ booking information, raising fresh questions about online travel security and data protection.

Booking.com has warned customers that a cyber breach exposed some booking information, raising fresh concerns about the security of online travel platforms.

The company says unauthorised parties gained access to some guests’ details, including booking information. While the full scale has not been made public, even limited exposure can leave customers vulnerable to scams and phishing attempts.

The incident adds to a run of data breaches across major consumer services, where attackers increasingly target large databases of personal and contact information. That makes trust and data protection central commercial issues, not just technical ones.

For travellers, the immediate advice is likely to be vigilance over emails, messages and payment accounts. For the company, the bigger challenge is restoring confidence after another breach.

Questions will now focus on how the access happened and whether customers were notified quickly enough.
