UK inflation rises to 3.3% amid biggest jump in fuel prices in more than three years

The Office for National Statistics says UK inflation rose to 3.3% in March as the Iran war pushed up fuel costs, with food and air fares also rising and the pressure now feeding into the cost of living debate.

UK inflation rose to 3.3% in March, according to the Office for National Statistics, after the Iran war pushed fuel prices up by the biggest amount in more than three years.

The figures are the first official sign of how the conflict is feeding into the cost of living in Britain, with food and air fares also moving higher.

That matters because fuel costs often feed quickly into broader household spending, from transport to freight to supermarket bills, and they can complicate the Bank of England’s choices on interest rates.

The March reading was up from 3% in February and matched City expectations, but it still reinforces pressure on families already facing high prices in several parts of the economy.

If energy markets remain volatile, the inflation picture could worsen again in coming months, adding to political arguments over living standards and economic resilience.
