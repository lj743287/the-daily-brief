Amazon to end support for older Kindles, prompting user outcry

The move again raises the question of how much control consumers really have over devices that depend on long-term software support.

Amazon’s decision to end support for older Kindles has triggered user anger, particularly among owners of devices released before 2013 who will lose the ability to download new e-books.

The row highlights a persistent problem in consumer technology: ownership is rarely absolute when a device depends on software, updates and company servers. Even products that seem durable can become functionally obsolete when support ends.

For customers, the immediate concern is access to books already purchased or tied to the device’s ecosystem. More broadly, the episode adds to criticism that big technology firms can quietly shorten product lifespans and leave consumers with fewer practical rights than they expect.
