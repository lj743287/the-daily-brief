Nigeria Air Force orders investigation after dozens killed in airstrike

Nigeria’s air force has ordered an investigation after an airstrike killed dozens, with reports saying more than 100 people may have died in the attack.

Nigeria’s air force has ordered an investigation after an airstrike killed dozens of people, with reports suggesting that more than 100 may have died in the attack.

The episode has revived longstanding fears about civilian casualties in Nigeria’s conflict zones, where military operations against armed groups have repeatedly hit the wrong targets or caused indiscriminate damage.

Authorities have not yet commented publicly on the reported toll, but any confirmed misfire would intensify pressure on the government to explain how the strike was authorised and what safeguards were in place.

The incident is also likely to deepen mistrust in affected communities, especially if local accounts of the death toll prove far higher than official statements.

The inquiry will now be watched closely for whether it is independent, how quickly it reports, and whether anyone is held accountable.
