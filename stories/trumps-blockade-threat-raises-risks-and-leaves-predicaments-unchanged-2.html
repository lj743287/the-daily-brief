Trump's blockade threat raises risks and leaves predicaments unchanged

Trump’s blockade threat turns the Strait of Hormuz into a test of resolve, but it does not resolve the underlying strategic dilemma: Iran can absorb punishment, while the US must weigh escalation, shipping disruption and the wider economic cost.

Donald Trump’s threat to blockade the Strait of Hormuz has raised the stakes in the stand-off with Iran, but it does not solve the underlying problem.

The strait remains a vital route for oil and gas shipments, so any disruption would quickly rattle energy markets and shipping. But Iran can probably endure pressure for longer than Washington can tolerate the economic and strategic consequences of a prolonged shutdown.

That leaves the US facing an old dilemma: how to show strength without triggering a wider conflict. Tehran, meanwhile, can use the threat itself as leverage, even if it cannot safely close the waterway for long.

So the threat may add danger, but it does not change the basic balance.
