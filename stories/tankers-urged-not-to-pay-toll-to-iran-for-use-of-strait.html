Tankers urged not to pay toll to Iran for use of strait

Tanker firms are being told not to pay levies in return for safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz. The advice signals how quickly commercial shipping is being pulled into the conflict’s economic and security risks.

Tanker firms are being advised not to pay Iran any levies in return for safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz, as commercial shipping is drawn further into the confrontation around the waterway. The advice reflects concern that any payment scheme could be read as accepting Iranian authority over transit through a route that is crucial to global energy supplies.

The warning comes amid heightened tensions after Donald Trump said the US would begin blockading the strait, raising fresh alarm across the shipping industry. Even the threat of interference has already led some operators to slow sailings, review routes or hold back vessels until the security picture is clearer.

If ships stop transiting freely, the consequences would extend well beyond the Gulf. Higher freight costs, more expensive insurance and delays to oil and fuel deliveries would quickly feed into markets in Europe and Asia, while governments would face pressure to protect shipping without triggering a broader military clash.
