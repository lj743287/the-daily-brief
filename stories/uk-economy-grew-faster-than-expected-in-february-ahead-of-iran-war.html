UK economy grew faster than expected in February ahead of Iran war

The UK economy expanded faster than expected in February, recording its biggest monthly rise in more than two years before the outbreak of the US-Israeli war with Iran. The timing matters because the shock since then may yet weigh on the next set of figures.

The UK economy grew faster than expected in February, recording its biggest monthly rise in more than two years before the outbreak of the US-Israeli war with Iran. The timing matters because the shock that followed could yet weigh on the next set of figures.

The stronger-than-expected monthly performance suggests the economy was on firmer footing than many analysts had assumed. But that is only part of the picture, because official data now have to absorb the impact of the conflict and its effect on energy, confidence and trade.

For households and businesses, the key issue is whether the February strength can survive the disruption that followed. If the war has pushed up fuel costs or dampened investment, later numbers may look much weaker.

The latest data therefore carry a warning as well as some good news. They show momentum before the crisis, but not yet the full cost of what came after.

Economists will be watching the next releases closely to see whether the conflict has merely delayed growth or begun to erode it.
