Cyclone Vaianu slams New Zealand’s North Island

Heavy rain, coastal flooding and winds above 130km/h forced evacuations and caused widespread outages. Authorities warn of landslides and lingering flood risk as the system tracks past the Chatham Islands.

Cyclone Vaianu brought heavy rain, coastal flooding and winds above 130km/h to New Zealand’s North Island, forcing evacuations and causing widespread power outages, local authorities and forecasters said.

Officials warned of landslides in saturated terrain and fast‑rising rivers as the system drove dangerous swells into low‑lying communities. Residents were urged to avoid unnecessary travel and heed local evacuation orders.

MetService said the cyclone crossed the coast near the Maketu peninsula before tracking south-east, with the system expected to move past the Chatham Islands while maintaining hazardous conditions offshore.

Emergency teams reported blocked roads, toppled trees and damage to coastal properties, complicating access for utility crews. Maritime authorities cautioned against boating and beach activities until conditions ease.

The storm has disrupted transport and logistics, with some ferry services and flights affected and supply routes slowed by debris and flooding. Farmers and small businesses in exposed districts face days of clean‑up and assessment.

Officials noted that residual flood risk would persist even as winds abate, given high soil moisture and swollen catchments. Attention has turned to restoring power safely and assessing longer‑term recovery needs.
