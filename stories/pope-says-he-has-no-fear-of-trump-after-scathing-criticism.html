Pope says he has 'no fear' of Trump after scathing criticism

Pope Leo has responded to Donald Trump’s attack by saying he has “no fear”, keeping the Vatican and the White House on a collision course over crime, foreign policy and the Church’s public stance.

Pope Leo has hit back at Donald Trump after the US president accused him of being weak on crime and terrible for foreign policy, saying he has “no fear” in the face of the criticism.

The exchange keeps the Vatican and the White House on a collision course over issues that extend beyond personal sparring. It touches on the Church’s public voice on migration, violence and diplomacy at a time of acute global strain.

Trump’s attack adds to a long history of friction between American political leaders and popes over moral authority, social policy and the language used to discuss public life.

The Vatican has not signalled any retreat from its positions, and Leo’s response suggests he is willing to meet political pressure head-on rather than avoid confrontation.

What happens next will depend on whether the dispute remains a verbal clash or spills into wider disagreements on foreign policy and the Church’s role in public debate.
