Warner Bros Discovery vote to approve $110bn merger with Paramount Skydance

Warner Bros Discovery shareholders have backed a $110bn merger with Paramount Skydance, although the deal still needs government approval and could face legal challenges.

Warner Bros Discovery shareholders have overwhelmingly approved the company’s $110bn merger with Paramount Skydance, clearing an important hurdle for the deal.

The transaction still needs government approval and could yet be delayed by a lawsuit aimed at blocking it. Shareholders also rejected generous executive pay packages, including a proposed $550m payout for outgoing chief executive David Zaslav.

The vote is significant because it shows investors backing a larger media combination even as regulators and rivals may question its implications for competition and content control. The deal would further concentrate power in an industry already under pressure from streaming losses and fragmented audiences.

The next stage will be regulatory review, which may prove slower and more contentious than the shareholder vote. For now, the merger has political and legal obstacles still ahead of it.
