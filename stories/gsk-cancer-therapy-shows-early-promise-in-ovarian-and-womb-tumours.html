GSK cancer therapy shows early promise in ovarian and womb tumours

An early-stage trial found Mo‑Rez reduced or eliminated tumours in over 60% of patients where chemotherapy had failed. Analysts say the drug could become a blockbuster, though larger trials and safety data are still needed.

An early‑stage trial of GSK’s cancer candidate Mo‑Rez has shown encouraging results in ovarian and womb cancers where chemotherapy had failed. The company reported tumour reductions or eliminations in more than 60% of patients in the study.

The findings are preliminary and derived from a small cohort, with safety and durability still being assessed. Clinicians caution that early response rates need confirmation in larger, controlled settings.

Analysts say the data point to potential blockbuster prospects if efficacy and tolerability hold up in subsequent trials. Hard‑to‑treat gynaecologic cancers remain areas of significant unmet medical need.

The results add to a growing body of work in targeted and immune‑based therapies aimed at previously refractory tumours. Combination approaches and biomarkers are likely to feature in next‑phase designs.

Regulators typically require robust phase II and III evidence before approval, though accelerated pathways may be considered in defined populations. Timelines will depend on enrolment speed and clinical endpoints.

Investors welcomed the signal, but oncologists stress the importance of longer follow‑up and comparative data. Broader access will hinge on consistent benefit, manageable side effects and clear guidance on patient selection.
