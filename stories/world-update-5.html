World update 5

A significant development in world with wider consequences under review.

Donald Trump has cancelled a planned trip by US envoys to Pakistan for talks linked to the Iran war, a sign that the conflict is disrupting diplomacy far beyond the immediate combat zone.

Iran had already said there were no plans for a direct meeting with the American delegation led by Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner, suggesting the route to negotiations was uncertain even before the cancellation.

The episode matters because regional diplomacy has become harder to separate from the war itself. Countries such as Pakistan may find themselves drawn into efforts to manage a conflict they do not control.

It also shows how quickly the crisis is spreading political consequences through the Middle East and South Asia, where governments are watching for shifts in US policy and regional security.

For now, there is no sign of a breakthrough. Instead, the cancellation points to a more fluid and fragile diplomatic picture, in which talks can be announced, doubted and dropped in short order.

What comes next will depend on whether Washington and Tehran can still find a channel for contact, or whether the war keeps closing off the space for discussion.
