SpaceX IPO raised $10bn more than thought

SpaceX’s stock market debut raised $87.5bn rather than the $75bn initially expected, underlining the scale of investor appetite for Elon Musk’s company and the extraordinary valuation now attached to the business.

SpaceX raised $87.5bn in its stock market debut, around $10bn more than had initially been expected. The scale of the listing underlines the extraordinary investor appetite surrounding Elon Musk’s company and the valuation now attached to it.

The company’s debut matters because it confirms SpaceX is being treated not simply as a rocket firm but as a major technology and infrastructure business with deep strategic importance. Its rise also adds to the extraordinary wealth of Musk, whose fortune has been propelled by the market value of his companies.

A listing of this size is likely to intensify debate about whether public markets are valuing the business on performance, promise or both. The difference between the initial expectation and the actual raise suggests investors were prepared to pay a premium for access to the company.

What happens next will depend on whether SpaceX can justify that optimism with growth in launch services, satellites and related technologies. For now, the IPO has set a new benchmark for private-turned-public ambition.
