Cyclone Vaianu lashes New Zealand’s North Island

The storm made landfall with destructive winds, heavy rain and large swells, triggering evacuations and power cuts. Authorities warned of coastal flooding and landslides as emergency services responded to multiple incidents.

Cyclone Vaianu made landfall on New Zealand’s North Island with destructive winds, heavy rain and large swells, prompting evacuations and widespread power outages. Authorities warned residents to avoid coastal areas and heed local instructions as conditions deteriorated rapidly.

The national forecaster described Vaianu as a life‑threatening system, cautioning of coastal flooding, storm surge and landslides. The cyclone crossed the coast near the Maketu peninsula before tracking south‑east, with outlying communities warned that hazardous seas would persist after landfall.

Emergency services responded to multiple incidents, including flooded roads, downed lines and stranded motorists. Councils opened evacuation centres and urged people in low‑lying or landslide‑prone areas to move to higher ground or seek shelter with friends and family.

In recent seasons, severe weather has repeatedly tested the country’s infrastructure and emergency protocols. Officials have stressed the importance of preparedness, from stocking essentials to planning evacuation routes, as more intense storms converge on populated areas.

The immediate economic impact will be felt in disrupted transport, damaged property and losses for agriculture and tourism. Insurers are braced for claims, while utility crews face difficult conditions to restore power and communications in hard‑hit districts.

Forecasters expect the system to weaken as it moves away, though dangerous conditions may linger along coasts and river catchments. Authorities have urged people to limit non‑essential travel and to stay clear of beaches and floodwaters until the all‑clear is given.
