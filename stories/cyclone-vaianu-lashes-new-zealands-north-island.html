Cyclone Vaianu lashes New Zealand’s North Island

Destructive winds, heavy rain and storm surges forced evacuations and caused power outages as Cyclone Vaianu made landfall. Authorities warn of landslides and coastal flooding as emergency services respond to widespread disruption.

Cyclone Vaianu made landfall on New Zealand’s North Island with destructive winds, heavy rain and storm surges, forcing evacuations and causing widespread power outages. Authorities described the system as life‑threatening and warned residents to heed official advice.

MetService issued alerts for landslides and coastal flooding, with the Bay of Plenty and adjacent coastal areas among those facing severe impacts. The cyclone crossed the coast near the Maketu peninsula before tracking south‑east.

Civil defence officials closed vulnerable roads, suspended some public services and opened emergency shelters. Thousands of people had earlier been ordered to leave low‑lying and exposed areas as the storm approached.

Transport and utilities were heavily disrupted, with fallen trees and floodwaters hampering crews. Ferry schedules were curtailed and airlines adjusted domestic flights, while telecommunications in some districts experienced intermittent failures.

Communities still recovering from earlier severe weather face compounding risks, prompting renewed debate over infrastructure resilience. Warmer seas and shifting weather patterns have increased concerns about the intensity and frequency of damaging storms.

Forecasters expect Vaianu to weaken as it moves away from the mainland, passing west of the Chatham Islands. Damage assessments and clean‑up operations will begin as conditions allow, with final tallies of losses likely to take days.
