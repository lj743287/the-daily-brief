Kyiv and Moscow trade blame over fresh ceasefire breaches

Both Kyiv and Moscow reported a surge in ceasefire violations, with each side alleging breaches by the other across multiple sectors of the front. The claims point to continued use of artillery, drones and small-unit skirmishes that have kept the line of contact volatile despite periodic efforts to reduce hostilities.

The uptick in incidents threatens to undermine already tentative de-escalation efforts and adds to the difficulty of establishing stable humanitarian access. Aid groups say stop-start fighting hampers evacuations and the repair of critical infrastructure, from power networks to water systems, in communities repeatedly caught in the crossfire.

Western governments have called for restraint and renewed engagement through diplomatic channels, but there is little sign of a breakthrough. The military picture remains fluid, with localised advances and withdrawals that have not altered the broader strategic stalemate.

As winter gives way to spring, commanders on both sides are recalibrating positions and logistics, raising the risk of miscalculation. With talks faltering and trust scarce, international mediators face a narrowing window to stabilise the situation before battlefield dynamics harden further.
