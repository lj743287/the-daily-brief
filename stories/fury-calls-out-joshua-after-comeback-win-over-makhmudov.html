Fury calls out Joshua after comeback win over Makhmudov

Tyson Fury urged a long‑awaited showdown after a unanimous decision victory in London. Promoters face a familiar puzzle of dates, venues and broadcast partners.

Tyson Fury returned with a unanimous decision win over Arslanbek Makhmudov in London, then called for the long‑anticipated showdown with Anthony Joshua. The message was clear: he wants the biggest domestic fight available next.

Fury managed the threat in front of him with ringcraft and timing, shaking off rust and avoiding extended exchanges. Makhmudov carried danger throughout, but the champion’s control of distance and tempo kept the scorecards comfortable.

Attention immediately shifted to logistics. Any Fury‑Joshua agreement would require alignment on purse split, rematch provisions and the role of sanctioning bodies, each capable of stalling momentum if not resolved swiftly.

Venues and broadcast partners will shape the outcome. A UK stadium would carry historical resonance, while international hosts could offer substantial site fees; either route demands a single coherent media plan to make the economics work.

Public appetite for the fight remains undimmed after years of false starts. Both camps signal intent, but recent history counsels caution until contracts are signed and obligations elsewhere are cleared.

Negotiations are expected to gather pace in the coming weeks. With calendars filling and mandatories looming, the window for a summer or early autumn date is tight, making early clarity on terms essential.
