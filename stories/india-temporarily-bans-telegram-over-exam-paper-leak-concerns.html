India temporarily bans Telegram over exam paper leak concerns

India has temporarily banned Telegram amid concerns it was being used in connection with exam paper leaks, including controversy around the NEET exam that was previously scrapped after allegations of a leak and mass protests.

India has temporarily banned Telegram amid concerns that the messaging platform was being used in connection with exam paper leaks. The move comes against a background of long-running public anger over cheating, leakage and corruption in the country’s highly competitive exam system.

The controversy is especially sensitive because of the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test, or NEET, which was previously scrapped after allegations of a leak and mass protests. That episode turned exam integrity into a national political issue, with students and parents demanding stronger action from the authorities.

By targeting Telegram, the government is signalling that encrypted or semi-private online platforms are now part of the enforcement challenge. But a temporary ban will also raise questions about proportionality, effectiveness and whether punishing an entire service can really stop organised leak networks.

The deeper problem is that exam fraud in India has become both a technological and a social issue. As criminal groups move information quickly through messaging apps, regulators face pressure to police digital platforms without undermining legitimate communication for millions of users.
