As Carney eyes Liberal majority, there are risks for him and Poilievre

Mark Carney is trying to turn Monday’s by-elections into a stronger mandate for the Canadian Liberals, but his broad coalition may yet prove difficult to hold together.

Mark Carney is looking to use Monday’s by-elections to strengthen the Liberal Party’s hold on power, but the result could cut both ways. A stronger mandate would help consolidate his authority; a weaker showing would sharpen questions about whether his broad coalition can hold together.

Carney’s appeal has rested on breadth as much as leadership, bringing together voters and factions that do not always share the same priorities. That can be an asset in government, but it can also make discipline harder and policy choices more contested, especially if the party is asked to absorb too many competing interests.

For Pierre Poilievre and the Conservatives, the by-elections matter too. A good Liberal result would make it harder to argue that the government is vulnerable, while a poor one could revive the opposition’s claim that Carney’s support is fragile. Either way, the vote will be read as an early measure of political momentum rather than a final verdict.

The stakes are not only parliamentary arithmetic. In a polarised political climate, even local results can reshape perceptions of who is in command, and whether the electorate is rewarding caution, competence or change.
