Police appeal after woman raped by several men outside Surrey church

A woman in her 20s was attacked after leaving a nightclub in Epsom. Detectives urged witnesses to come forward as specialist officers support the victim.

Police have appealed for witnesses after a woman in her 20s reported she was raped by several men outside Epsom Methodist Church in Surrey. She was followed after leaving the Labyrinth Epsom nightclub between 2am and 4am on Saturday, according to statements carried by the BBC and the Guardian.

Detectives are asking anyone who was in the area during the early hours, or who may have dashcam or CCTV footage from nearby streets, to come forward. Officers said specialist support is being provided to the victim as the investigation progresses.

The incident took place in the vicinity of the church in Epsom town centre during a period when there would have been late‑night footfall. Police are working to establish a detailed timeline of the victim’s movements and to identify potential witnesses who may have seen interactions or suspects before or after the attack.

Surrey Police have urged the public not to speculate on social media and to share any information directly with investigators. Protecting the victim’s privacy remains a priority, and officers emphasised that reporting and supporting enquiries can be done confidentially.

Officers typically review CCTV from licensed premises, taxi ranks and key junctions in cases of this nature. They are also likely to liaise with local venues and community leaders to assist with appeals and reassurance as enquiries continue.

Anyone with information is asked to contact Surrey Police via usual non‑emergency channels, quoting the relevant reference, or to reach out anonymously via Crimestoppers. Police said further updates will be provided as soon as operationally appropriate.
