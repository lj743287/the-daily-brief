At least 30 feared dead in crush at Haitian tourist site

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a tourist event in Haiti, where the prime minister said many of the victims were young people.

The incident, described by officials as taking place at an event attended by many young people, is a grim reminder of how quickly large gatherings can turn deadly in a country already grappling with political and security crises.

Haiti has endured years of violence, instability and weak public services, which can make emergency response and crowd control especially difficult. That context makes the scale of the tragedy more acute.

Authorities will now face questions about safety planning, crowd management and whether more could have been done to prevent the crush.

The immediate priority is search, treatment and identification, but the wider test will be whether the state can provide answers as well as condolences.
