At least 30 feared dead in crush at Haitian tourist site

A crowd crush at a tourist event in Haiti has left many feared dead and dozens more injured. The incident adds to the country’s acute security and governance pressures.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a tourist event in Haiti, in an incident that has once again exposed the country’s severe security and governance strains. Officials said many of those caught up in the crush were young people attending the gathering.

Crowd disasters of this kind are often a sign of deeper failures in planning, policing and emergency response. In Haiti, where state capacity is already badly stretched, even a single incident can quickly overwhelm hospitals and rescue services.

The immediate concern is for the injured and for families still waiting for news. But the broader significance is grimly familiar: each such tragedy lands in a country already struggling with violence, political instability and weak public institutions, making recovery harder and trust thinner.
