At least 30 feared dead in crush at Haitian tourist site

A crush at a tourist event in Haiti has left dozens feared dead, adding to the country’s long running security and public safety crisis.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a tourist event in Haiti, according to officials. The incident adds another layer to the country’s prolonged crisis of insecurity, weak public services and limited emergency response capacity.

The crush is believed to have occurred at a popular site crowded with young people, making the toll especially alarming. In a country where large gatherings can quickly become dangerous, any mass-casualty incident raises immediate questions about crowd control and rescue arrangements.

Haiti has endured years of gang violence, political instability and repeated humanitarian shocks. That wider backdrop matters because it leaves little margin for managing accidents of this kind, and it means families often face delayed medical care and limited official information after disasters.

Authorities have yet to provide a full account of the cause, the number of injured or whether the site was properly secured. Those answers will be crucial both for accountability and for understanding whether this was a tragic accident or a preventable failure.
