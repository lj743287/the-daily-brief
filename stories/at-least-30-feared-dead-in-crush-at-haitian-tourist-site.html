At least 30 feared dead in crush at Haitian tourist site

A crush at a tourist event in Haiti has left many feared dead, highlighting the country’s continuing instability and weak public safety capacity.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a tourist event in Haiti, according to reports and the country’s prime minister. The incident took place at a popular site attended by many young people, underlining how quickly large gatherings can turn deadly in a country with weak public safety capacity.

Haiti has endured years of political crisis, gang violence and fragile state institutions, and that context matters when emergencies unfold. Even routine crowd control, rescue and medical response can be difficult to organise quickly.

The immediate priorities are to establish the final death toll, identify those injured or missing, and understand how the crush began. But the broader picture is already clear: public events in Haiti remain vulnerable to catastrophe because basic protections that would reduce the danger elsewhere are often absent.
