At least 30 feared dead in crush at Haitian tourist site

A fatal crush at a crowded tourist event has again exposed the risks of weak crowd management and emergency response at major public gatherings.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a crowded tourist event in Haiti, according to officials. The gathering reportedly drew many young people, and the scale of the incident has raised concern that the emergency response was overwhelmed.

Haiti has long struggled with weak public services, limited crowd control capacity and fragile transport and medical infrastructure, all of which can turn a large public event into a disaster. When people are packed tightly into confined spaces, even a brief panic can lead to catastrophic consequences.

The immediate questions are how the crowd became trapped, whether organisers had adequate safety plans and how quickly injured people could be reached. The incident is likely to renew scrutiny of public event planning in a country where many basic state functions are under severe strain.
