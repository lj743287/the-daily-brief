Police arrest two over alleged drug sales through shops in West Midlands

Police have arrested two people after a BBC investigation alleged that shops in parts of the West Midlands were being used to sell drugs.

Police have arrested two people after a BBC investigation alleged that shops in parts of the West Midlands were being used to sell drugs.

The investigation found that some High Street mini-marts were allegedly acting as fronts for drug dealing in local towns. That has now prompted enforcement action, although the full scale of the problem is still being assessed.

The case matters because it points to a wider pattern in which apparently ordinary retail premises can be exploited by criminal gangs. That has implications for policing, local licensing and the safety of people living near those shops.

It remains unclear whether the arrests are the start of a broader crackdown or just one step in a longer investigation. But the police response suggests the allegations are being treated seriously.
