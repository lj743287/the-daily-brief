Union Berlin appoints first female head coach in a top-five men’s league

Marie-Louise Eta takes interim charge, a landmark for European football. The move could ripple through hiring practices and gender pathways in the men’s game.

Union Berlin have appointed Marie-Louise Eta as interim head coach, making her the first woman to manage a men’s team in one of Europe’s top five leagues. The Bundesliga club’s move marks a historic milestone for the men’s professional game on the continent.

Eta takes temporary charge while the club determines its longer-term plans, stepping into the role from within Union’s coaching set-up. The interim designation means both the team’s performances and the club’s strategic horizon will shape how long she remains in post.

The significance extends well beyond Berlin. Europe’s so‑called top five leagues — in England, Spain, Italy, Germany and France — dominate global club football, and a woman leading a men’s top-flight side there challenges long-standing assumptions about who gets to manage at the highest level.

While women have previously taken charge of men’s professional teams in lower divisions — including short-term roles in England’s fourth tier and a multi-year tenure in France’s second division — none had crossed the boundary into a top-five men’s top flight until now. Eta’s promotion shifts that boundary in a way that is likely to be closely studied across Europe.

The appointment could influence how clubs think about hiring, development pathways and the value placed on coaching credentials over playing background. Advocates argue that visible representation at the apex may encourage more women to pursue elite licences and technical roles, while sceptics will warn that an interim posting risks being tokenistic unless it is backed by institutional support and ongoing opportunities.

Attention will now turn to the touchline and the immediate results that often define managerial tenures. Beyond Berlin, the wider question is whether other clubs in Europe’s leading leagues will consider a broader pool of candidates in forthcoming cycles — a test of whether this landmark becomes precedent rather than exception.
