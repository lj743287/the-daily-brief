Palestine Action ban is lawful, Court of Appeal rules

The Court of Appeal has ruled that the Home Office’s ban on Palestine Action is lawful, overturning an earlier High Court decision and keeping in place a significant counter-terrorism test for protest groups.

The Court of Appeal has ruled that the Home Office’s ban on Palestine Action is lawful, overturning an earlier High Court decision. The judgment keeps in place one of the most significant counter-terrorism tests for a protest group in recent years.

The ruling matters because it touches directly on the balance between public protest and the state’s power to proscribe organisations. Supporters of Palestine Action have argued that the ban is an attack on dissent, while the government has treated the group as a serious security concern.

By upholding the ban, the court has strengthened the government’s position, but it has not ended the controversy. Questions about where lawful protest ends and prohibited activity begins are likely to continue, especially if the group or its supporters pursue further legal challenge.

For campaigners, the decision narrows the space for direct action. For ministers, it offers legal backing for a politically contentious move that will remain closely watched.
