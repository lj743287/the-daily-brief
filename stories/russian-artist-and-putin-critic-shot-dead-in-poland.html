Russian artist and Putin critic shot dead in Poland

Robert Kuzovkov, the Russian artist who used the pseudonym Semyon Skrepetsky and was known for caricatures of Vladimir Putin, has been shot dead in Poland, raising questions about the safety of outspoken exiled critics in Europe.

Robert Kuzovkov, the Russian artist who used the pseudonym Semyon Skrepetsky, has been shot dead in Poland. He was known for caricatures of Vladimir Putin and other politicians, making his killing immediately significant for exiled Russian critics in Europe.

The circumstances of the shooting have not been fully explained, and police will now need to establish whether this was a targeted attack, a crime of another kind, or something linked to his political profile. Whatever the motive, the case will raise fears among Russians who have left the country because of their opposition to the Kremlin.

Europe has become a refuge for many dissidents, journalists and artists who judged it safer to speak freely abroad. A killing in Poland will therefore sharpen concerns that exile does not necessarily mean protection, especially for those who have publicly mocked or challenged Vladimir Putin.

For now, the priority is to establish who killed Kuzovkov and why. But the wider issue is already clear: outspoken Russian opponents in Europe remain vulnerable, and the line between political hostility and physical danger can be alarmingly thin.
