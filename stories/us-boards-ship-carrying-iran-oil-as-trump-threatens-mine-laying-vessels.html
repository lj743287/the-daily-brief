US boards ship carrying Iran oil as Trump threatens mine-laying vessels

US forces have intercepted a vessel carrying Iranian oil as Donald Trump threatens action against mine-laying ships, underlining the widening maritime risks around the Iran war.

US forces have boarded a vessel carrying Iranian oil, deepening the maritime confrontation around the war involving Iran as Donald Trump warns of action against mine-laying ships.

Central Command said it has intercepted 33 vessels since imposing a blockade on ships coming to and from Iran. That figure suggests the scale of the effort to disrupt maritime traffic linked to Tehran and to control the wider flow of energy and supplies through contested waters.

The risk is that pressure at sea could spill into a broader regional escalation. Shipping lanes have become a crucial front in the conflict, with each seizure, interception or threat raising the danger of miscalculation.

It is not yet clear how far Washington intends to go in policing the blockade or what the legal basis will be for future boardings. But the incident underlines how quickly the Iran war is widening beyond the battlefield.
