At least 30 killed in crush at Haitian tourist site

Young people were among the dead after a crowd surge at the landmark Citadelle Henry fortress in northern Haiti. Officials reported dozens injured or missing, adding to the country’s broader security and humanitarian crisis.

At least 30 people were killed in a crowd surge at the Citadelle Henry fortress in northern Haiti, officials said, with many of the victims reported to be young people. Dozens more were injured or are missing, and the death toll could rise as authorities assess the scene.

The Citadelle, a landmark mountaintop fortress built after Haiti’s independence, draws both tourists and locals. A weekend event drew large numbers, but what triggered the sudden crush remains unclear. Investigators are gathering witness accounts and reviewing crowd management arrangements.

Emergency services struggled to reach the remote site quickly, highlighting the challenges of operating in difficult terrain with limited resources. Hospitals in the region faced a surge of casualties, adding strain to a health system already under pressure.

The tragedy lands in the midst of a wider security and humanitarian crisis. Political uncertainty and gang violence have hampered basic governance and stretched public services, with civil protection agencies often operating with minimal equipment and personnel.

Crowd safety experts say clear ingress and egress routes, capacity limits and on‑site medical response are critical safeguards at mass gatherings. Whether such measures were in place or enforced will be central to the official inquiry and any subsequent accountability.

Authorities have pledged to publish casualty lists and support families of the victims. There are likely to be calls for new standards at cultural and tourist events, and for international assistance to strengthen Haiti’s emergency response capacity.
