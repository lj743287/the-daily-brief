US government watchdog to investigate Epstein files release

The US justice department’s inspector general will review the withholding and overdue release of Jeffrey Epstein files, including why secret records were not produced sooner.

The US justice department’s inspector general is to investigate the handling of Jeffrey Epstein files, including why secret records were withheld and why some were not released sooner.

The review adds fresh scrutiny to a long-running and politically toxic story that has repeatedly raised questions about how law enforcement dealt with Epstein’s network and the evidence around it. The delayed release of files has fuelled suspicion among victims, campaigners and lawmakers alike.

At issue is not only what the documents contain, but who decided to hold them back and on what basis. That makes the inquiry significant for public trust in the justice department as much as for the Epstein case itself.

The watchdog’s findings could increase pressure for further disclosure, though it is not yet clear how much material will be made public or whether the review will lead to any disciplinary action. For Epstein’s survivors, the central demand remains fuller transparency.
