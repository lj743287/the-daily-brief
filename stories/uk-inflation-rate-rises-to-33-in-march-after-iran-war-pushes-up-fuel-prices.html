UK inflation rate rises to 3.3% in March after Iran war pushes up fuel prices

The UK’s March inflation rate has risen to 3.3% as the Iran war pushed up fuel prices, the first official sign of how the conflict is feeding into household costs.

UK inflation rose to 3.3% in March after the Iran war pushed fuel prices higher, giving the first official reading of how the conflict is affecting household budgets.

The increase matters because fuel feeds into transport, food distribution and air fares, meaning the effect can spread well beyond the petrol station.

For policymakers, the numbers add to a difficult cost-of-living picture: inflation is no longer easing cleanly, and external shocks are making it harder to predict the path ahead.

The March figure provides a snapshot rather than the full story, and more will depend on whether energy markets stabilise or remain volatile in the weeks ahead.

If the war continues to unsettle oil and shipping markets, British consumers may feel the pressure again before long.
