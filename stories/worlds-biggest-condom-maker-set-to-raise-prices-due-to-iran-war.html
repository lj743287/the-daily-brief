World's biggest condom maker set to raise prices due to Iran war

Malaysia’s Karex, the world’s biggest condom maker, says it may raise prices as freight costs and supply-chain disruption linked to the Iran war continue to squeeze margins.

Karex, the world’s biggest condom maker, says it may raise prices as the Iran war continues to push up freight costs and disrupt supply chains.

The Malaysia-based company produces more than five billion condoms a year and supplies major global brands such as Durex and Trojan, so any price change could feed through to retailers in multiple markets.

Its warning is a reminder that war-driven cost pressures do not stop at obvious sectors like fuel and aviation; they can also affect consumer goods through shipping delays, higher insurance costs and tighter inventories.

For buyers, the impact may be modest on any single purchase, but it adds to a wider pattern of inflationary pressure that is becoming harder for companies to absorb.

How far prices rise will depend on how long logistics remain strained and whether the conflict continues to disrupt regional trade routes.
