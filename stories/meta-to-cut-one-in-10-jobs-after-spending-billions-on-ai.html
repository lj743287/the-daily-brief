Meta to cut one in 10 jobs after spending billions on AI

Meta will cut about 10% of staff, its largest layoff since 2023, after spending billions on artificial intelligence and signalling a sharper focus on efficiency.

Meta will cut about 10% of its workforce, in what employees had already expected would be the company’s largest layoff since 2023.

The move comes after years of heavy spending on artificial intelligence, reflecting a sharper push for efficiency as the company tries to show that its AI investment is translating into productivity gains. It is one of the clearest signs yet that the AI race is reshaping corporate structures, not just products.

The cutbacks matter beyond Meta because they highlight a broader pattern across tech: firms are pouring money into AI systems while reducing roles they believe can be automated or consolidated. That will deepen concern about the effect of the technology on white-collar employment.

The company’s next test is whether the restructuring improves its business enough to justify the disruption. If rivals follow suit, more layoffs across the sector may follow.
