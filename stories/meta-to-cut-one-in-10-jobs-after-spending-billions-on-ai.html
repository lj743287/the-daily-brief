Meta to cut one in 10 jobs after spending billions on AI

Meta is cutting about one in 10 jobs after spending billions on AI, its largest layoff since 2023. The move adds to pressure on the company to justify the scale of its artificial-intelligence investment.

Meta is cutting about one in 10 jobs after spending billions on AI, in what will be its largest layoff since 2023.

The move comes after months of heavy investment in artificial intelligence and will intensify pressure on the company to show that those spending plans can translate into business returns.

For employees, the cuts are a direct reminder that the AI boom has not insulated big tech from cost discipline. For investors, they raise the question of whether Meta is scaling too aggressively or simply trying to reset after a costly expansion.

The layoffs also sit within a broader technology market that has been trying to balance growth, automation and weaker hiring across parts of the sector.

Meta's challenge is not only financial but strategic: it must convince shareholders that AI spending will deliver future products and profits, even as it trims staff today.

More job cuts may follow across the industry if companies decide that the race to build and deploy AI still needs to be matched by tighter control of expenses.
