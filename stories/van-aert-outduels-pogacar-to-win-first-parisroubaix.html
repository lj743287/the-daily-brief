Van Aert outduels Pogacar to win first Paris–Roubaix

Wout van Aert ended years of near‑misses to take the cobbled Monument in a sprint, with Tadej Pogacar second as mechanical issues hit key rivals. The victory reshapes early‑season narratives ahead of the Ardennes and the Tour.

Wout van Aert claimed his first Paris–Roubaix with a decisive sprint in the Roubaix velodrome, ending years of frustration at the cobbled Monument. The Belgian held off Tadej Pogacar to cap a punishing day over the pavé.

Mechanical problems punctuated the race’s key phases, with both Van Aert and Pogacar delayed by punctures and their long‑time rival Mathieu van der Poel hindered by issues that left him unable to contest the finale. In the end, the front group splintered under repeated accelerations before Van Aert proved the strongest when it mattered.

The victory delivers Van Aert a second Monument after Milan–Sanremo in 2020 and closes a chapter of near‑misses that included crashes and ill‑timed setbacks. It also underlines his ability to win the sport’s most attritional one‑day tests rather than just animate them.

For Pogacar, second place extends a formidable spring and reinforces a rivalry that has defined early‑season narratives. With the Ardennes Classics looming and the Tour de France on the horizon, the balance of power among the sport’s headline names looks newly energised.

The women’s edition produced its own headlines as Germany’s Franziska Koch took the win, while Lucinda Brand suffered neck pain after colliding with a spectator, according to Dutch and UK reports. Safety around crowded cobbled sectors remains under scrutiny across both races.

Attention now shifts to the Ardennes, where different terrain awaits but the same protagonists are likely to feature. Van Aert’s breakthrough on the cobbles restores momentum for the rest of his spring, and hints at broader ambitions once the Grand Tours begin.
