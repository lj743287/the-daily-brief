Oil prices fluctuate as Trump extends Iran war ceasefire

Oil markets are swinging as Donald Trump extends the Iran war ceasefire, leaving traders to assess whether the latest pause will hold and whether shipping disruption can be contained.

Oil prices have been fluctuating after Donald Trump said he was extending the Iran war ceasefire, leaving traders to judge whether the latest pause will hold.

The president also said the US would continue to blockade Iran’s ports until peace talks progress, a sign that pressure on Tehran is still being used alongside diplomacy.

Markets are reacting to a familiar problem: even a temporary easing in the fighting can move prices sharply, but any sign of renewed disruption to shipping can send them back up just as fast.

For consumers and businesses, the issue is not only the level of oil prices but the uncertainty itself, which makes budgeting for transport, fuel and goods more difficult.

The next phase will depend on whether ceasefire terms are honoured and whether the shipping routes most exposed to the conflict remain open.
