Dolce & Gabbana co‑founder steps down as chair amid debt strain

The luxury house faces a tougher market and higher leverage as global high‑end demand cools. Leadership changes aim to steady the brand’s strategy in a more cautious consumer environment.

A co‑founder of Dolce & Gabbana has stepped down as chair, as the Italian luxury house contends with higher leverage and a cooling in global high‑end demand, the BBC reported. The leadership change is intended to steady the company’s trajectory.

Luxury spending has softened after a post‑pandemic surge, with more cautious consumers in key markets weighing on growth. Brands carrying more debt face less room for manoeuvre as conditions normalise.

Investors have tended to favour groups with stronger balance sheets and diversified exposure, putting pressure on peers to tighten costs and focus on core franchises. Financing conditions have also become more exacting.

D&G is expected to emphasise discipline in inventory, marketing and store investment while safeguarding brand equity. The task will be to maintain creative momentum without overextending in a slower market.

Succession at founder‑led labels is often closely watched for signals on governance and direction. Ensuring continuity in design while recalibrating strategy will be central to investor and retailer confidence.

Across the sector, pricing power is moderating and expansion plans are being trimmed. The shift from exceptional growth to steadier demand is testing the resilience of luxury business models.
