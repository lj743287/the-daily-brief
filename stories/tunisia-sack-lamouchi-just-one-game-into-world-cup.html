Tunisia sack Lamouchi just one game into World Cup

Tunisia have dismissed Sabri Lamouchi after only one World Cup game and replaced him with Hervé Renard, a swift change that underlines the tournament’s early pressure on coaches.

Tunisia have sacked Sabri Lamouchi after just one game at the World Cup and replaced him with Hervé Renard. The rapid change underlines how little patience many national teams have once tournament results begin to disappoint.

The decision matters because it shows the pressure coaches face when major competitions begin badly. One poor result can be enough to trigger panic, even when the broader causes of underperformance may run deeper than a single match.

Renard is a familiar emergency option in international football, and his appointment suggests Tunisia want an immediate reset. But changing a manager so quickly also risks exposing uncertainty inside the camp rather than solving it.

What happens next will depend on whether the team responds quickly on the pitch. At World Cup level, there is rarely time for patience, and Tunisia have acted accordingly.
