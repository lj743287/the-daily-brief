Holders Bordeaux beat Toulouse to set up Bath semi-final

The Champions Cup bracket pits French champions against Premiership holders next. Set-piece dominance and backline incision will decide a finely balanced tie.

Reigning European champions Bordeaux-Bègles knocked out domestic rivals Toulouse to reach the Champions Cup semi-finals, booking a last-four meeting with Premiership holders Bath. The all-French quarter-final delivered the intensity expected of two heavyweight squads, with Bordeaux advancing to keep their title defence intact.

The contest was shaped by set-piece pressure and discipline. Bordeaux’s pack established a foothold, turning territory into points, while their backs struck decisively when space finally opened. Toulouse, usually so precise, were forced into errors at critical moments and could not sustain field position long enough to wrest back the momentum.

The result sets up a marquee cross-Channel tie between the champions of France and England. Bath, who have rediscovered steel alongside fluency this season, will now test themselves against a Bordeaux side comfortable in the tight exchanges and confident in broken play. It is a semi-final with no clear favourite.

This matters beyond bragging rights. The clash is a barometer of the power balance between France’s resource-rich Top 14 and a Premiership rebuilding depth but retaining elite standards at the top. With Europe’s leading clubs converging in the season’s defining month, the match will speak to coaching adaptability, squad management and how best to navigate congested calendars without sacrificing precision.

On the field, two themes are likely to decide it: set-piece dominance and backline incision. Bath’s maul and line-out accuracy have underpinned their campaign, while Bordeaux’s scrum and breakdown aggression can sap tempo and generate penalties. If the English side can control the kick battle and exit cleanly, they will unleash runners off front-foot ball; if Bordeaux slow ruck speed and counter with sharp midfield angles, their strike plays can open the edges. Discipline and the contestable kicking game sit at the heart of both plans.

Both teams now pivot to domestic duties before reconvening, looking to balance rotation with rhythm. Fitness updates will be pivotal given the collisions to come, and coaches will probe match-ups that can be tilted in their favour, from aerial duels to defensive spacing. The prize is a place in the European showpiece final—either a statement of successful title defence for Bordeaux or a landmark step for Bath’s resurgence—and the tie has all the ingredients to be decided in the tightest of margins.
