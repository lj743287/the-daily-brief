How many ships are crossing the Strait of Hormuz?

A BBC Verify check suggests traffic through the strait remains far below normal, indicating how nervous shipping firms remain.

BBC Verify analysis suggests that only a few vessels have crossed the Strait of Hormuz since the ceasefire deal, far below normal traffic levels. That matters because the strait is one of the world's most important shipping routes, and a sharp drop in crossings is a clear sign that firms remain nervous.

Shipping companies are not waiting for a formal blockade before reacting. If insurance costs rise, crews face greater risk or the political situation looks unstable, firms can slow sailings, divert routes or hold vessels back altogether. The result is that a maritime crisis can deepen even when no ship has been stopped outright.

The low traffic levels indicate that confidence has not yet returned. Unless the security picture improves, the bottleneck could keep energy prices and freight costs elevated well beyond the immediate conflict.
