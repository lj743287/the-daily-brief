How many ships are crossing the Strait of Hormuz?

BBC Verify analysis suggests traffic through the strait remains severely reduced. Shipping data is becoming a key indicator of whether the conflict is easing or simply entering a new phase of constrained trade.

BBC Verify analysis suggests traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remains severely reduced, making shipping data one of the clearest indicators of how the crisis is developing. The number of vessels crossing the strait is being watched closely by traders, insurers and governments.

Reduced traffic matters because it can signal not just immediate danger but a longer-term change in commercial behaviour. If shipowners avoid the route, the practical effect can be almost as disruptive as a formal closure, with knock-on effects for energy prices and supply chains.

The central uncertainty is whether traffic picks up as the ceasefire settles or whether companies continue to keep vessels away. For now, the strait remains a live measure of confidence in the region’s security.
