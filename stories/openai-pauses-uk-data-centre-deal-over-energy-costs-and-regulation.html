OpenAI pauses UK data centre deal over energy costs and regulation

OpenAI has paused its UK data centre deal, saying energy costs and regulation remain unresolved, a setback for the government’s ambition to position Britain as an AI hub.

OpenAI has paused its UK data centre deal, saying energy costs and regulation remain unresolved. The setback matters because the project had been presented as part of Britain's bid to become an AI hub and attract large-scale technology investment.

The company's pause highlights a familiar problem for governments trying to court big tech: the promise of jobs and infrastructure can run into practical concerns about power supply, planning and rules. In this case, the UK's energy pricing and regulatory environment appear to have become stumbling blocks.

For ministers, the move is awkward because it suggests that grand claims about an AI economy may be harder to turn into investment than expected. The deal is not necessarily dead, but the delay will sharpen questions about whether Britain can compete with other countries offering cheaper power and faster approvals.
