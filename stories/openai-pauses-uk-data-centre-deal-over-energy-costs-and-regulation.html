OpenAI pauses UK data centre deal over energy costs and regulation

The pause underlines the gap between ambitious AI investment rhetoric and the realities of power availability, regulation and project economics.

OpenAI has paused a planned UK data centre deal, citing pressures around energy costs and regulation. The decision is a reminder that even the biggest AI ambitions still depend on practical questions such as power supply, planning rules and project economics.

Data centres are among the most energy-intensive pieces of digital infrastructure, and the UK has been trying to attract more of them as part of its push to become a serious AI hub. But the scale of that ambition runs into a familiar constraint: the electricity grid is not always ready for it.

The pause will worry policymakers who have marketed the UK as open for frontier-tech investment. It also suggests that the race for AI infrastructure is not just about chips and talent, but about whether countries can provide cheap, reliable power at the scale the industry wants.
