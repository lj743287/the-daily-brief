US summons bank chiefs over cyber risks from new AI model

Treasury officials met major lenders after Anthropic flagged unprecedented cybersecurity implications from a new system. The move reflects regulators’ growing concerns that advanced AI could amplify financial‑sector vulnerabilities.

US Treasury officials met major bank leaders this week to discuss cybersecurity risks linked to Anthropic’s latest AI system, according to The Guardian. Federal Reserve chair Jerome Powell reportedly attended the Washington meeting.

Anthropic has warned that its new model, referred to as Claude Mythos, carries unprecedented implications for cyber threats, though detailed capabilities have not been disclosed publicly. The company’s assessment prompted regulators to seek industry input.

Supervisors fear advanced AI could lower barriers for attackers, speed reconnaissance and exploitation, and amplify social‑engineering risks across the financial system. The concern is less about a single tool than about the compounding effect of rapid model advances.

Banks are reviewing controls, vendor dependencies and incident‑response playbooks in light of AI‑enabled attack scenarios. Authorities are considering whether to issue guidance, expand testing regimes and coordinate more closely on threat intelligence.

The meeting highlights a swift escalation of AI oversight as newer systems move from research into wide‑ranging applications. It follows broader government attention to model governance, content safeguards and sector‑specific resilience.

Markets have shown little immediate reaction, but firms expect tighter supervisory expectations around model risk and cyber defences. The balance regulators seek is to harness productivity gains without widening the financial sector’s attack surface.
