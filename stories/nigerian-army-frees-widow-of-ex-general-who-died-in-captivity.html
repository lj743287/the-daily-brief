Nigerian army frees widow of ex-general who died in captivity

The Nigerian army has freed Amina Abubakar, widow of Maj Gen Rabe Abubakar, after the pair were abducted in north-west Nigeria at the end of last month; her husband died in captivity.

The Nigerian army has freed Amina Abubakar, the widow of Maj Gen Rabe Abubakar, after the couple were abducted in north-west Nigeria at the end of last month. Her husband died in captivity, turning an already serious kidnapping into a fresh reminder of the insecurity afflicting parts of the country.

Abductions for ransom have become a grim feature of life in north-west Nigeria, where armed groups and criminal gangs exploit weak policing and difficult terrain. Even people with military connections are not immune, which underlines how far the threat has spread beyond ordinary civilians.

The release of Amina Abubakar is welcome, but it does not remove the underlying problem. Her husband’s death will intensify pressure on the authorities to show that they can protect travellers, residents and prominent figures alike in regions where kidnapping has become an economy in itself.

For families in affected areas, the central fear remains the same: being taken in the first place, and then surviving whatever comes after. The case will likely renew debate over whether Nigeria’s security forces have enough reach, coordination and local intelligence to curb the violence.
