Benin votes in presidential election months after failed coup attempt

The West African nation heads to the polls with the finance minister seen as favourite to succeed Patrice Talon. The vote is viewed as a measure of stability after a coup bid and amid concerns over democratic openness.

Benin headed to the polls for a presidential election viewed as a test of stability four months after a failed coup attempt, with the finance minister seen as the favourite to succeed Patrice Talon, according to the Guardian.

Talon is stepping down after two terms, with supporters of the governing camp emphasising economic continuity while critics question the openness of the political environment.

Security forces were deployed across major urban centres and key transport hubs as authorities sought to reassure voters and avoid disruptions on polling day.

Regional observers from organisations including ECOWAS and the African Union have taken an interest in the conduct of the vote, amid international calls for transparency and due process.

Economically, Benin has posted steady growth in recent years, but voters also face pressures from high living costs and unemployment, factors that could shape post‑election priorities.

Official results are expected after counting is completed, with candidates urging supporters to remain calm as the electoral commission tallies ballots and handles any challenges.
