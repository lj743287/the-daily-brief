After Iran talks falter, the big question is what happens next?

The failure of the talks leaves the ceasefire fragile and the diplomatic track uncertain, with major implications for regional security and global energy flows.

The collapse of the talks leaves the ceasefire and the wider diplomatic track in an uncertain place. Twenty-one hours of discussions were not enough to bridge the hostility that has defined relations between Iran and the US for decades.

That matters because the alternative to diplomacy is not stability but drift: a tense stand-off in which both sides test the limits of the ceasefire while trying to avoid being blamed for breaking it. In the region, that is often how fragile truces begin to unravel.

What happens next will depend on whether the US and Iran can keep channels open, and whether regional actors can restrain their allies and proxies. The risks are not only military but economic, because any renewed disruption around the Gulf quickly feeds into oil markets and shipping.
