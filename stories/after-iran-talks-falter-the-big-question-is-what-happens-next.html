After Iran talks falter, the big question is what happens next?

The weekend’s failed diplomacy has not resolved the underlying conflict. The likely next steps include military escalation, disruption to shipping and renewed pressure on allies to clarify their position.

The collapse of weekend talks has not settled the dispute between Washington and Tehran; it has simply moved the crisis into a more volatile phase. Diplomacy failed to deliver agreement, and that leaves the region exposed to the next move, which could come from either side or from their regional allies.

The most immediate danger is military escalation, whether through further strikes, retaliatory attacks or actions aimed at disrupting shipping in the Gulf. Any serious interference with the Strait of Hormuz would quickly spread beyond the two protagonists, affecting oil markets, insurers, ports and consumers far from the conflict zone.

Allies are also being forced into a position they would rather avoid. European governments, Gulf states and others with forces or trade interests in the region will come under pressure to clarify how far they are prepared to support deterrence, protect shipping and restrain a conflict that is already broadening in economic terms.
