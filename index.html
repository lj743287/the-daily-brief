Front Page

Lead: After Iran talks falter, attention turns to what comes next. With diplomatic channels straining, concerns are growing over energy supplies and maritime security as traffic through the Strait of Hormuz faces disruption. European airlines warn of fuel shortages if closures persist, while shipping groups advise tankers not to pay any Iranian tolls. Political leaders, including Keir Starmer, are urging restraint and a renewed push for dialogue.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a Haitian tourist site. Emergency services are working to identify victims as authorities investigate how the tragedy unfolded. International assistance offers are expected as the scale of the disaster becomes clearer.

Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations. The escalation raises questions over the durability of existing agreements and the prospects for any broader settlement as winter preparations give way to renewed military activity.

British Steel may require nationalisation by the summer, according to warnings that have reignited debate over industrial policy. The company’s future is tied to investment, decarbonisation pledges and safeguarding skilled jobs in steelmaking hubs.

Markets & Economy

Energy markets remain on edge as the diplomatic breakdown with Iran intersects with maritime uncertainty in the Strait of Hormuz. Airlines in the EU caution that prolonged disruption could squeeze jet fuel supplies, potentially forcing schedule changes and higher operating costs. Insurers and shippers are weighing guidance that vessels should avoid paying any passage tolls to Iranian authorities, highlighting wider legal and security risks.

The government is seen as relatively relaxed about a wave of Chinese electric vehicle imports. Proponents argue consumers benefit from lower prices and faster green adoption; critics warn of pressure on domestic manufacturing, supply chain vulnerabilities and an uneven playing field. Industry is seeking clarity on incentives, rules of origin and the UK’s long-term competitiveness.

British Steel’s potential nationalisation would mark a pivotal moment for UK heavy industry. Any move would need to balance immediate liquidity support with conditions on transformation, from low-carbon furnaces to workforce protections and long-term procurement guarantees.

UK

Keir Starmer has appealed to Washington and Tehran to find a way back to talks, framing UK support for de-escalation alongside a broader push for regional stability. Westminster is tracking knock-on risks to energy prices, shipping routes and aviation.

A murder charge has been filed following a fatal stabbing on Primrose Hill. Police say enquiries are continuing and have appealed for witnesses as the victim is formally identified.

Police are investigating the rape of a woman by several men outside a church. Detectives have asked the public to share dashcam and CCTV footage from the area and urged anyone with information to come forward.
