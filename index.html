Stalemate with Iran raises stakes in Strait of Hormuz as fuel squeeze fears grow

Diplomatic talks with Iran have faltered, increasing uncertainty over the future of shipping and energy flows through the Strait of Hormuz. Airlines and shippers are drawing up contingency plans, while politicians urge restraint and a rapid return to negotiations.

Kyiv and Moscow trade blame over fresh ceasefire breaches

Ukraine and Russia have accused each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations, underscoring the fragility of attempts to cool fighting along the front. The exchanges risk complicating diplomatic efforts and deepen concern for civilians near contested areas.

Ministers face summer deadline as British Steel said to need nationalisation

The government is under mounting pressure to intervene after British Steel was reported to need nationalising by the summer to stay afloat. The warning lands as ministers signal a relaxed stance on Chinese car imports, sharpening debate over industrial strategy and competitiveness.

Asha Bhosle, the sound of Bollywood, dies aged 92

Asha Bhosle, one of Indian cinema’s most beloved playback singers, has died aged 92. Her seven-decade career, spanning thousands of songs across genres and languages, helped define the soundscape of Bollywood and resonated with audiences worldwide.
