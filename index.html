After Iran talks falter, the world confronts a volatile next phase

The collapse of high-stakes talks between Iran and the United States underscores how far the sides remain from stabilising the Gulf after months of confrontation. With traffic through the Strait of Hormuz still curtailed and energy prices elevated, the shock is ricocheting through aviation, shipping and inflation. The immediate questions are whether a fragile ceasefire can be shored up, how quickly oil and fuel supply lines normalise, and which third parties can still broker a workable framework.
