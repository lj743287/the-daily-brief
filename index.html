The chokepoint economy: how a faltering Gulf peace ripples through the world

As talks with Iran stall and the Strait of Hormuz sits at the centre of a mounting stand-off, the world is learning again how much can hinge on a narrow stretch of water. From airline fuel stocks to household energy bills, the costs of a geopolitical misstep are already being felt — and could yet reshape politics and industry far from the Gulf.

Front Page

After Iran talks falter, the big question is what happens next?

Fal­tering talks have left the Gulf on edge as policymakers weigh how to prevent a wider crisis. The aviation and shipping sectors warn of knock‑on effects if disruption around the Strait of Hormuz persists, while political leaders urge renewed diplomacy.

Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Kyiv and Moscow have each reported hundreds of violations, reflecting a grinding conflict in which civilians and infrastructure remain at risk. Independent verification is scarce, and neither side shows signs of easing pressure along the front.

British Steel 'needs nationalising by the summer' as ministers face industrial test

Calls to bring British Steel into public ownership by the summer have sharpened scrutiny of the government’s industrial strategy. At the same time, ministers appear relaxed about a wave of Chinese car imports, and Reform UK defends its leader over a £91,000 tax row.

'Every drop of water counts': Fear for the future of Argentina's glaciers

Argentina’s glaciers, vital to water security and ecosystems across the Andes, face mounting pressure from a warming climate. Scientists and local communities warn that losses could imperil drinking water, agriculture, tourism and hydropower.
