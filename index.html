Front Page

Lead: After Iran talks falter, the big question is what happens next? Diplomatic efforts to calm tensions around the Strait of Hormuz have stalled, raising urgent questions over energy flows and shipping safety. European carriers warned of aviation fuel shortages if the chokepoint remains constrained, and maritime advisers told tankers not to pay any Iranian tolls for passage through the strait.

Regional partners and Washington are assessing limited de-escalation steps, but none have yet changed conditions at sea. Keir Starmer urged the US and Iran to “find a way through”, calling for sustained diplomacy and restraint, while industry groups pressed governments to maintain navigational freedom.

Insurers and shippers are revisiting risk calculations after weeks of disruption, with pressure growing for an internationally backed maritime security arrangement. Traders are bracing for higher freight and fuel costs if uncertainty persists.

Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations along the frontline, underscoring the fragility of local truces and complicating humanitarian access. Observers report shelling spikes around key transit towns.

At least 30 people are feared dead after a crush at a Haitian tourist site. Emergency services struggled to reach the area amid poor road access and crowding, with authorities urging people to avoid the scene as a rescue operation continues.

Asha Bhosle, the peerless playback singer whose voice defined generations of Indian cinema, has died aged 92. Tributes poured in from artists and political leaders across South Asia and beyond, celebrating a career that spanned more than seven decades.

Markets & Economy

Airlines and energy markets are on alert as the Strait of Hormuz uncertainty drags on. Carriers warn of potential jet fuel shortages in Europe and higher costs if re-routing or stockpiling becomes necessary. Shipowners are advised not to pay Iranian tolls, a stance that could spark legal and diplomatic tussles while pushing up insurance premia.

In the UK, British Steel’s future is again in focus, with calls for nationalisation by the summer absent fresh investment. The debate highlights the trade-offs between long-term industrial policy, green transition costs and regional jobs.

Ministers signal a relaxed stance on Chinese car imports, arguing lower prices benefit consumers and spur competition in EVs. Domestic manufacturers want clarity on subsidies, rules of origin and charging networks to ensure a level playing field.

Investors remain cautious, weighing geopolitical risk against resilient consumer demand. Any prolonged disruption to energy supply chains would test inflation progress and complicate interest-rate paths on both sides of the Atlantic.

UK

The government’s relaxed approach to Chinese car imports has divided opinion. Supporters say cheaper EVs accelerate the transition for households; critics fear a squeeze on UK producers and call for targeted safeguards and investment in domestic capacity.

Keir Starmer urged the US and Iran to “find a way through” after peace talks faltered, aligning the UK with European partners calling for maritime de-escalation and protection of vital energy routes.

A man has been charged with murder following a fatal stabbing on Primrose Hill in London. In a separate case, police are investigating the reported rape of a woman by several men outside a church, appealing for witnesses and CCTV.
