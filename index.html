After Iran talks falter, the big question is what happens next?

Fragile diplomacy with Iran has stumbled, amplifying fears over the Strait of Hormuz and the knock‑on effects on global fuel supplies. Europe’s airlines warn of jet fuel shortages if the choke point remains shut, while shippers are urged not to pay Iranian tolls.

Ukraine and Russia accuse each other of hundreds of ceasefire violations

Kyiv and Moscow traded allegations of large‑scale truce breaches amid intensified shelling and drone strikes. The claims underscore how fragile any lull remains, with civilians again bearing the brunt.

British Steel ‘needs nationalising by the summer’ as policy tensions sharpen

A stark warning that British Steel may require nationalisation within months has thrust industrial policy back to the top of the agenda. It comes as ministers are said to be relaxed about Chinese car imports, highlighting a split‑screen approach to competitiveness and consumer prices.

Asha Bhosle: The sound of Bollywood dies aged 92

Asha Bhosle, whose voice defined generations of Hindi cinema, has died aged 92. Her versatility and prolific output made her a defining figure of South Asian popular culture.
